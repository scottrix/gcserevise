🏛 Course Overview

GCSE Ancient History explores the civilisations of ancient Greece and Rome, developing students' understanding of key events, individuals and societies. OCR J198 is the only GCSE Ancient History qualification available. It has two papers: Paper 1 (Period Study, 50%, 1hr 30min) covering a broad chronological period such as the Persian Wars or the foundation of Rome; and Paper 2 (Depth Study, 50%, 1hr 30min) focusing in detail on a shorter period such as Sparta, Athens or the Roman Emperors. Both papers assess source analysis, interpretation skills, and the ability to construct sustained historical arguments.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are aligned with the OCR J198 specification. OCR is the only exam board offering GCSE Ancient History. The Period Study options include the Persian Wars (Greece) and the founding of Rome (Rome). The Depth Study options include Sparta, Athens, the Roman Emperors and the Roman Republic. Each topic page indicates the relevant specification content and provides source analysis practice aligned with AO3.

🔑 Key Topics

🏛 The Greeks in the Archaic Age: Greek city-states, Spartan society, Athenian democracy, Greek religion and warfare

⚔ Athens in the Classical Age: The Athenian Empire, Pericles and the Golden Age, philosophy, theatre, and daily life in Athens

⚓ The Persian Wars: The Ionian Revolt, Marathon, Thermopylae and Salamis, and the Greek victory at Plataea

📜 The Roman Republic: The founding of Rome, the Roman Republic, the Conflict of the Orders, and the Punic Wars

👑 The Fall of the Republic & Emperors: The fall of the Roman Republic, Augustus and the Principate, and the Roman army and engineering

✍ Exam Technique: Command words, source analysis, essay writing, Period Study technique, and Depth Study technique
