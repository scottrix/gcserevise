Foundation Higher AQA 8463 & Edexcel 1AS0

Complete revision notes for GCSE Astronomy covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with AQA 8463 and Edexcel 1AS0 specifications. Covers planet Earth, the Solar System, stars, galaxies & cosmology, observational astronomy, and space exploration & exam technique.

🔭 Course Overview

GCSE Astronomy develops students' understanding of the universe, from our local Solar System to the most distant galaxies. AQA 8463 has two papers: Paper 1 (Solar System, 50%, 1hr 45min) and Paper 2 (Stars, Galaxies & Cosmology, 50%, 1hr 45min). Students must also complete two naked-eye observational tasks. Edexcel 1AS0 similarly has two papers: Paper 1 (Understanding the Solar System, 50%, 1hr 45min) and Paper 2 (Understanding Stars & Galaxies, 50%, 1hr 45min), plus an unaided observation task. Both boards emphasise practical observing skills alongside theoretical knowledge of celestial mechanics, stellar evolution and cosmology.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are relevant across AQA 8463 and Edexcel 1AS0. Each topic page indicates which board(s) require it. Both specifications cover the Solar System, stellar astronomy, galaxies and cosmology, and observational skills. AQA places particular emphasis on the naked-eye observational tasks, while Edexcel requires an unaided observation project. The core astronomical content is common to both boards.

🔑 Key Topics

🌍 Planet Earth: Earth's shape, rotation, seasons, lunar phases, and celestial coordinates

🪐 The Solar System: Solar system structure, planets, small bodies, the Sun, and orbital mechanics

⭐ Stars: Star properties, stellar lifecycle, the HR diagram, and nucleosynthesis

🌌 Galaxies & Cosmology: The Milky Way, galaxy types, the Big Bang, dark matter/energy, and exoplanets

🔭 Observational Astronomy: Telescopes, observing techniques, astrophotography, and practical skills

🚀 Space Exploration & Exam Technique: Space exploration, satellites, probes, and exam technique
