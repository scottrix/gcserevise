🏛️ GCSE Citizenship Studies

Foundation Higher AQA 8100, OCR J270 & Edexcel 1CS0

Complete revision notes for GCSE Citizenship Studies covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with AQA 8100, OCR J270, and Edexcel 1CS0 specifications. Covers British values, democracy, the constitution, politics & participation, law & justice, global citizenship, active citizenship, and rights & responsibilities — including how citizens can engage with and influence the political and legal systems.

🏛️ Course Overview

GCSE Citizenship Studies explores how modern society functions and how citizens can participate effectively. AQA 8100 has 2 papers: Paper 1 "British Politics, Parliament and Government" (50%) covering Life in Modern Britain and Rights & Responsibilities; Paper 2 "Active Citizenship" (50%) covering Politics & Participation and Active Citizenship. OCR J270 has 2 components: Component 1 "Citizenship Studies Today" (50%) covering Rights, the Law, and the Legal System plus The UK and Global Citizenship; Component 2 "Creating an Informed and Active Citizen" (50%) covering Making Democratic Processes Work and Active Citizenship. Edexcel 1CS0 has 2 papers: Paper 1 "Citizenship Studies Today" (50%) and Paper 2 "Creating an Informed and Active Citizen" (50%).

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are relevant across AQA 8100, OCR J270, and Edexcel 1CS0. Each topic page indicates which board(s) require it. While core content (British values, democracy, law, rights) is common to all three boards, there are differences in emphasis and in the active citizenship requirements. Check your specification for which topics you need to know in detail.

🔑 Key Topics

Life in Modern Britain: British values, the constitution, democracy, media, human rights

Politics & Participation: Electoral systems, political parties, local government, influencing decisions

Law & Justice: Legal system, criminal & civil law, the justice system, young people & the law

Global Citizenship: UK & the world, Commonwealth & international organisations, EU & Brexit, sustainability

Active Citizenship: What is active citizenship, action planning, campaigning & advocacy, evaluation

Rights & Responsibilities: Equality & diversity, consumer rights, employment rights, digital rights & privacy
