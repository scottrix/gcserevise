📊 Course Overview

GCSE Design and Technology (AQA 8552) is assessed through a written exam (50%) and a Non-Exam Assessment (NEA) coursework project (50%). The written exam has three sections: Section A covers Core Technical Principles (20 marks), Section B covers Specialist Technical Principles (30 marks), and Section C covers Designing and Making Principles (50 marks). The NEA involves a design-and-make task from a contextual challenge set by AQA.

🧪 Key Topics

New Technologies: Industry 4.0, 3D printing, IoT, AI in design

Materials: Papers/boards, timbers, metals, polymers, textiles, composites, smart materials

Design Process: Investigating contexts, developing ideas, making, evaluating

Manufacturing: Injection moulding, CNC, press forming, casting, 3D printing
