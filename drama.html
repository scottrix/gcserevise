📊 Course Overview

GCSE Drama is assessed through three components: a written exam (Understanding Drama, 40%) and two NEA components (Devising Drama 30%, Texts in Practice 30%). Students devise original drama, perform extracts from scripts, and analyse live theatre. The written exam tests knowledge of a set text and evaluation of live theatre.

🎤 Key Practitioners

Stanislavski: Naturalism, system, given circumstances, magic if, emotion memory

Brecht: Epic theatre, alienation effect, gestus, political theatre

Artaud: Theatre of Cruelty, total theatre, assault on the senses

Frantic Assembly: Physical theatre, chair work, push hands, lift and lean

Boal: Theatre of the Oppressed, forum theatre, spect-actors
