🏛 Course Overview

GCSE Electronics develops students' understanding of electronic systems and how they work in the real world. WJEC Eduqas C690QS has three components: Component 1 (Discovering Electronics, 40%, 1hr 45min) covering fundamental electronic principles, components and circuits; Component 2 (Application of Electronics, 35%, 1hr 45min) covering digital and analogue systems and their applications; and Component 3 (Practical Project, 25% NEA) involving the design, construction and testing of an electronic circuit. All components test knowledge and understanding, application to circuit problems, and analysis and evaluation of electronic systems.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are aligned with the WJEC Eduqas C690QS specification. Component 1 (Discovering Electronics) covers fundamental principles, components and basic circuits. Component 2 (Application of Electronics) covers digital systems, analogue systems and their real-world applications. Each topic page indicates the relevant specification content and provides circuit calculation practice aligned with AO2 and evaluation practice aligned with AO3.

🔑 Key Topics

Electronic Fundamentals: Voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's law, capacitors, diodes, transistors and power

Digital Systems: Logic gates, Boolean algebra, combinational and sequential logic, digital design

Analogue Systems: Op-amps, amplifier circuits, comparators and analogue signals and filters

Practical Circuits: Circuit construction, testing, troubleshooting and safety

Electronics Applications: Power supplies, sensors and transducers, control systems and microcontrollers

Exam Technique: Command words, circuit calculations, circuit diagrams, Component 2 and practical technique
