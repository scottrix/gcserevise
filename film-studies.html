Complete revision notes for GCSE Film Studies covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with the Eduqas C680QS specification. Covers the English legal system, criminal law, civil law, rights and responsibilities, justice and law reform, and essential exam technique for legal problem questions and evaluation.

🏛 Course Overview

GCSE Film Studies develops students' understanding of the English legal system and how the law affects everyday life. Eduqas C680QS has two papers: Paper 1 (The Legal System, 50%, 1hr 45min) covering the English legal system, criminal law and law making; and Paper 2 (Law in Action, 50%, 1hr 45min) covering the law of tort, human rights and family law. Both papers test knowledge and understanding, application to legal scenarios, and evaluation of legal rules and principles.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are aligned with the Eduqas C680QS specification. OCR is the primary exam board offering GCSE Film Studies; WJEC Eduqas also offers a GCSE Film Studies qualification (C300QS). Paper 1 (The Legal System) covers the English legal system, criminal law and law making. Paper 2 (Law in Action) covers the law of tort, human rights and family law. Each topic page indicates the relevant specification content and provides legal scenario practice aligned with AO2 and evaluation practice aligned with AO3.

🔑 Key Topics

Film Language: Cinematography, mise-en-scene, editing, sound and film language analysis

Film Genres: Genre theory, genre hybridity, science fiction and horror genres

US Film: US film industry, narrative, representation, independent film and contexts

UK Film: UK film industry, narrative, representation and contexts

Global Film: Global film industries, international film movements and world cinema audiences

Exam Technique: Command words, scene analysis, writing about film, Component 1 and Component 2 technique
