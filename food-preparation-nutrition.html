🍜 Course Overview

GCSE Food Preparation & Nutrition develops students' knowledge and practical skills in cooking, nutrition, food science, and food safety. AQA 8585 has 1 written paper "Food Preparation and Nutrition" (50%, 1hr 45min) covering all five content areas, plus NEA Task 1: Food Investigation (15%) and NEA Task 2: Food Preparation (35%). OCR J309 has 1 written paper "Food Preparation and Nutrition" (50%) plus NEA Task 1 (15%) and Task 2 (35%). WJEC C560QS has 1 written paper (50%) plus NEA Task 1 (15%) and Task 2 (35%). All three boards integrate practical cooking skills throughout the course.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are relevant across AQA 8585, OCR J309, and WJEC C560QS. Each topic page indicates which board(s) require it. The five content areas (nutrition & health, food science, food safety, food choice, food provenance) are common to all three boards, with practical cooking skills integrated throughout. While specific NEA task briefs differ between boards and years, the underlying knowledge and skills assessed are the same.

🔑 Key Topics

Nutrition & Health: Macronutrients, micronutrients, the Eatwell Guide, diet-related health issues, nutritional needs across life stages

Food Science: Heat transfer, functional properties of food, raising agents, food preservation, sensory properties

Food Safety: Food spoilage, food poisoning, food hygiene, HACCP and legislation

Food Choice: Factors affecting choice, food labelling, British & international cuisine, special diets

Food Provenance: Food production, food processing, the food industry, food waste & sustainability

Cooking Techniques: Preparation skills, cooking methods, NEA Task 1 investigation, NEA Task 2 preparation
