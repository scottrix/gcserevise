🏛 GCSE Law

OCR J271

Complete revision notes for GCSE Law covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with the OCR J271 specification. Covers the English legal system, criminal law, civil law, rights and responsibilities, justice and law reform, and essential exam technique for legal problem questions and evaluation.

🏛 Course Overview

GCSE Law develops students' understanding of the English legal system and how the law affects everyday life. OCR J271 has two papers: Paper 1 (The Legal System, 50%, 1hr 45min) covering the English legal system, criminal law and law making; and Paper 2 (Law in Action, 50%, 1hr 45min) covering the law of tort, human rights and family law. Both papers test knowledge and understanding, application to legal scenarios, and evaluation of legal rules and principles.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are aligned with the OCR J271 specification. OCR is the primary exam board offering GCSE Law; WJEC Eduqas also offers a GCSE Law qualification (C300QS). Paper 1 (The Legal System) covers the English legal system, criminal law and law making. Paper 2 (Law in Action) covers the law of tort, human rights and family law. Each topic page indicates the relevant specification content and provides legal scenario practice aligned with AO2 and evaluation practice aligned with AO3.

🔑 Key Topics

The English Legal System: The legal system overview, courts and court structure, legal personnel, sources of law and law making

Criminal Law: Elements of crime, offences against the person, property offences and criminal defences

Civil Law: Contract law, tort and negligence, family law and consumer rights

Rights & Responsibilities: Human rights and the ECHR, rights of UK citizens, police powers and equality law

Justice & Law Reform: The criminal justice process, sentencing and punishment, civil dispute resolution and law reform

Exam Technique: Command words, legal problem questions, evaluation skills, Paper 1 and Paper 2 technique
