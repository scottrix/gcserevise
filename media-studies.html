🎬 GCSE Media Studies

Foundation Higher AQA 8572, OCR J200 & Eduqas C680QS

Complete revision notes for GCSE Media Studies covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with AQA 8572, OCR J200, and Eduqas C680QS specifications. Covers media language, representation, industries, audiences, and practical production — including Close Study Products (CSPs), key theorists, and exam techniques.

🎬 Course Overview

GCSE Media Studies explores how media products communicate meaning, represent the world, and influence audiences. AQA 8572 has 2 papers: Paper 1 "Media Language and Representation" (35%) covering CSPs in television, music, radio, film, and newspapers; Paper 2 "Media Industries and Audiences" (35%) covering CSPs in online, social, video games, advertising, and film. The NEA (Non-Exam Assessment) is worth 30%. OCR J200 has 2 examined components plus NEA: Component 1 "Television and Promoting Media" (35%), Component 2 "Music and News" (35%), and Component 3 "Creating Media" (30%). Eduqas C680QS has 2 examined components plus NEA: Component 1 "Investigating Media" (40%), Component 2 "Understanding Media" (30%), and Component 3 "Creating Media" (30%).

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are relevant across AQA 8572, OCR J200, and Eduqas C680QS. Each topic page indicates which board(s) require it. While core theoretical concepts (media language, representation, industries, audiences) are common to all three boards, the specific Close Study Products (CSPs) differ. Check your specification for which CSPs you need to study in detail.

🔑 Key Topics

Key Concepts: Media language, representation, industries, audiences, and contexts

Media Forms: Television, film, music video, advertising, newspapers, radio, online media, video games

Theorists: Mulvey (male gaze), Hall (reception theory), Blumler & Katz (uses and gratifications), Goodwin (music video), Dyer (star theory), Cohen (moral panics)

Creating Media: Practical production, NEA coursework, and evaluation
