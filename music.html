Foundation Higher All Boards

Complete revision notes for GCSE Music covering 28 topics across 7 strands. Aligned with the AQA 8271 specification. Covers Musical Elements, four Areas of Study (Western Classical Tradition, Popular Music, Traditional Music, Western Classical Since 1910), Performing and Composing — including study pieces, musical vocabulary and exam technique.

📊 Course Overview

GCSE Music is assessed through three components: a written/listening exam (Understanding Music, 40%) and two NEA components (Performing 30%, Composing 30%). Students study four Areas of Study, with two set study pieces. The exam tests listening skills, knowledge of musical elements and critical appraisal of study pieces.

ComponentDescriptionWeighting
Understanding MusicWritten exam: Section A listening (unfamiliar music from all 4 AoS), Section B study pieces40%
Performing MusicOne solo performance (minimum 1 min) + one ensemble performance (minimum 1 min)30%
Composing MusicOne free composition (minimum 1 min) + one composition to a set brief (minimum 1 min)30%
