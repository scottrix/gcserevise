🏛️ GCSE Sociology

Foundation Higher AQA 8192 & OCR J205

Complete revision notes for GCSE Sociology covering 26 topics across 6 strands. Aligned with AQA 8192 and OCR J205 specifications. Covers Sociological Foundations, Families & Households, Education, Crime & Deviance, Social Stratification, and Research Methods — including key perspectives (Functionalism, Marxism, Feminism, Interactionism), classical sociologists (Durkheim, Marx, Weber), and contemporary research.

🏛️ Course Overview

GCSE Sociology explores how society shapes human behaviour and social structures. AQA 8192 has 2 papers: Paper 1 "The Sociology of Families and Education" (50%) covering families, education and research methods; Paper 2 "The Sociology of Crime and Deviance and Social Stratification" (50%) covering crime, deviance, social stratification and research methods. OCR J205 has 2 components: Understanding Social Processes (50%) covering families, crime and deviance, research methods; and Understanding Social Structures (50%) covering social stratification, education, and research methods.

📋 Board Coverage

All 26 topics are required for AQA 8192 and OCR J205. Each topic page indicates which board(s) require it. The core content (families, education, crime, stratification, research methods) is common to both specifications, though the specific studies and examples may differ.

🔬 Key Topics

Key Perspectives: Functionalism, Marxism, Feminism, Interactionism, Postmodernism

Core Topics: Families, Education, Crime & Deviance, Social Stratification

Research Methods: Questionnaires, interviews, observations, experiments, official statistics, documents

Key Thinkers: Durkheim, Marx, Weber, Parsons, Murdock, Becker, Merton
