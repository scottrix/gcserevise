Art, Craft and Design: Building Your Portfolio Component 1

What constitutes a portfolio; selecting work that shows AO coverage; demonstrating sustained project development; including supporting studies; showing breadth of media and approaches; written annotation requirements; organisation and presentation; meeting assessment criteria.

Key Fact: Component 1 (Portfolio) is worth 60% — it must show a sustained project plus supporting studies that demonstrate all four AOs.
Key Fact: Your portfolio should show a CLEAR NARRATIVE: from starting point, through research and experimentation, to a resolved personal response.
Key Fact: Supporting studies show breadth — additional experiments, artist responses and explorations that complement your main project.
Key Fact: Written annotation must use specialist vocabulary and show REFLECTIVE THINKING — not just descriptions of what you did.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Building Your Portfolio Component 1, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring building your portfolio component 1. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with building your portfolio component 1. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with building your portfolio component 1. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to building your portfolio component 1, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Art, Craft and Design Exam Tips:
MAP your portfolio against the four AOs — can a moderator see evidence of each? Include PROCESS work (thumbnails, experiments, test pieces) alongside final outcomes. Annotate REFLECTIVELY throughout. Show SUPPORTING STUDIES for breadth. Organise with clear narrative flow.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: A portfolio is just a collection of your best finished pieces of artwork. Correct: A portfolio is EVIDENCE OF YOUR CREATIVE JOURNEY, not a gallery of finished pieces. AQA assesses how you DEVELOPED ideas (AO1), EXPERIMENTED (AO2), RECORDED (AO3) and PRESENTED (AO4). A beautiful final piece without the journey of development scores poorly. Include process work, experiments, failures and annotations.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Review your current portfolio. Create an annotated plan showing how each section addresses the four AOs. Identify any gaps and suggest what you could add to improve AO coverage.

A grade 9 response will: map all four AOs clearly across the portfolio; show sustained, in-depth development; include strong written annotation with specialist vocabulary; demonstrate breadth through supporting studies; present work coherently with clear narrative; identify specific improvements with actionable plans.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
