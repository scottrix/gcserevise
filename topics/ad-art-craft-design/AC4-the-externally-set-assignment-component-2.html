Art, Craft and Design: The Externally Set Assignment Component 2

Understanding the ESA paper; choosing a starting point; planning and preparing (preparatory period); supervised time (10 hours); what examiners look for; managing time effectively; ensuring all four AOs are addressed; presenting a coherent response.

Key Fact: The ESA gives you starting points from AQA — you choose one and develop it independently during the preparatory period.
Key Fact: The preparatory period is YOUR time to research, experiment and develop ideas — show the same depth as your portfolio.
Key Fact: The 10 hours supervised time is for producing your FINAL RESPONSE — all preparation must be complete before it starts.
Key Fact: Your ESA preparation must address ALL four AOs — examiners mark the preparation alongside the supervised work.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For The Externally Set Assignment Component 2, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring the externally set assignment component 2. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with the externally set assignment component 2. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with the externally set assignment component 2. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to the externally set assignment component 2, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Art, Craft and Design Exam Tips:
Start preparation IMMEDIATELY after receiving the paper — do not waste time. Address ALL FOUR AOs in your preparatory work. Plan your 10 hours carefully — create a schedule. Test materials and techniques BEFORE the supervised time. Your preparation folder is MARKED — make it as strong as your portfolio.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: The ESA is an exam where you just make art for 10 hours with no preparation. Correct: The ESA has two parts: a PREPARATORY PERIOD (weeks of research, development and experimentation — like a mini-portfolio) and the 10 HOURS SUPERVISED TIME (where you create your final response). The preparatory work is marked alongside the final piece. Without thorough preparation, you cannot score well.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Choose one ESA starting point and plan your preparatory work. Create a timeline showing how you would address each AO during the preparatory period. Explain how you would use the 10 hours supervised time.

A grade 9 response will: show thorough, independent preparation addressing all four AOs; create a realistic, detailed timeline; demonstrate understanding that preparation is MARKED alongside the final piece; explain how the 10 hours would be used efficiently; plan for contingencies.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
