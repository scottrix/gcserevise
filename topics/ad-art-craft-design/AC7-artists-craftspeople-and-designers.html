Art, Craft and Design: Artists Craftspeople and Designers

Researching across art, craft and design disciplines; understanding different contexts and purposes; how practitioners inspire and inform personal work; comparing approaches across disciplines; critical analysis and written annotation; developing informed personal responses.

Key Fact: The Art, Craft and Design title requires research across MULTIPLE disciplines — not just fine art, but craft and design contexts too.
Key Fact: Understanding different purposes: art (personal expression), craft (material mastery), design (problem-solving) — each discipline has different values and approaches.
Key Fact: Comparing practitioners across disciplines reveals how the SAME theme can be explored through different media, techniques and intentions.
Key Fact: Your research must be CONNECTED to your own work — show how understanding a practitioner's approach has TRANSFORMED your thinking and practice.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Artists Craftspeople and Designers, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring artists craftspeople and designers. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with artists craftspeople and designers. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with artists craftspeople and designers. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to artists craftspeople and designers, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Art, Craft and Design Exam Tips:
Research ACROSS disciplines — do not just study fine artists. Include: practitioner NAME, work TITLE, DATE, MEDIUM. Analyse using appropriate vocabulary for each discipline. Show CONNECTIONS to your own work: how did understanding this practitioner CHANGE what you did? Compare approaches across art, craft and design.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: Artist research means copying their style to show you have studied them. Correct: AQA requires you to DEVELOP ideas informed by sources — understand WHY a practitioner works that way and use their approach to inspire your OWN personal response. Research should TRANSFORM your work, not just decorate your sketchbook with reproductions. Slavish copying scores low on AO4.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Research practitioners from at least two different disciplines (e.g. a fine artist and a textile designer, or a photographer and a product designer). Compare their approaches and show how each has influenced your own cross-disciplinary work.

A grade 9 response will: research practitioners from multiple disciplines with depth; analyse HOW and WHY each works, not just describe; compare approaches across disciplines; show CLEAR, SPECIFIC connections to personal work; evaluate how research transformed their creative decisions; use specialist vocabulary from each discipline.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
