FA8: Printmaking Techniques

Foundation Higher All Boards

Relief printing (linocut, woodcut); intaglio (drypoint, etching); screen printing; monoprinting; collagraph; editioning; registration; combining print with other media.

Fine Art: Printmaking Techniques

Relief printing (linocut, woodcut); intaglio (drypoint, etching); screen printing; monoprinting; collagraph; editioning; registration; combining print with other media.

Key Fact: Relief printing (linocut, woodcut) cuts away negative space; the raised surface is inked and printed.
Key Fact: Intaglio (drypoint, etching) holds ink in incised lines below the surface; the plate surface is wiped clean.
Key Fact: Screen printing forces ink through a mesh stencil; monoprinting creates one unique print from a painted surface.
Key Fact: Editioning means producing multiple identical prints; registration ensures colours align in multi-plate prints.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Printmaking Techniques, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring printmaking techniques. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with printmaking techniques. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with printmaking techniques. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to printmaking techniques, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Fine Art Exam Tips:
Show the PLATE/BLOCK alongside the print. Include artist proofs and edition numbers. Annotate each stage of the process. Show EXPERIMENTATION — vary ink density, paper type, colour combinations.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: The print will look the same as the block or plate. Correct: Relief and intaglio prints are MIRROR IMAGES of the plate — text and directional elements will be reversed. Plan for this by working in reverse or accepting the reversal as part of the process.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Produce a series of prints using at least two different printmaking techniques. Annotate each print explaining the process, how you achieved different effects, and what you learned from comparing techniques.

A grade 9 response will: demonstrate competent use of at least two techniques; show understanding of the unique qualities of each; annotate with precise technical vocabulary (registration, edition, burr, bite); evaluate which technique best serves their intention.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
