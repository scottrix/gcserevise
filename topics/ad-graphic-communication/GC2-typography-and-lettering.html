Graphic Communication: Typography and Lettering

Anatomy of type (baseline, x-height, ascenders, descenders); serif, sans-serif, script, display fonts; hierarchy and readability; hand lettering; logo type; combining type and image.

Key Fact: Type anatomy: baseline, x-height, ascenders, descenders, cap height — understanding these helps you choose and use type effectively.
Key Fact: Serif fonts have decorative strokes; sans-serif are cleaner and more modern; script fonts suggest elegance or informality.
Key Fact: Typographic hierarchy uses size, weight, colour and position to guide the reader through information in order of importance.
Key Fact: Kerning (space between letters), tracking (overall letter spacing) and leading (line spacing) dramatically affect readability.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Typography and Lettering, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring typography and lettering. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with typography and lettering. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with typography and lettering. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to typography and lettering, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Graphic Communication Exam Tips:
Show FONT EXPERIMENTS — compare different typefaces for the same brief. Demonstrate understanding of hierarchy through varied weight, size and position. Annotate with type vocabulary.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: Using lots of different fonts makes a design more interesting and creative. Correct: Good typography uses a LIMITED palette of 1-2 font families. Too many fonts create visual chaos and look unprofessional. Create variety through WEIGHT, SIZE and CASE within one family instead.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Design a typographic poster using only letterforms (no images). Use typography to communicate a specific message, mood or theme. Annotate explaining your font choices, hierarchy and layout decisions.

A grade 9 response will: demonstrate understanding of type anatomy and classification; use typographic hierarchy effectively; show experimentation with type as image; annotate with specialist vocabulary (kerning, leading, tracking, x-height).

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
