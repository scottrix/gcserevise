GC4: Branding and Corporate Identity

Foundation Higher All Boards

Logo design principles; brand guidelines; colour palettes and type systems; applying identity across media; stationery, packaging, signage; consistency and recognition; rebranding.

Graphic Communication: Branding and Corporate Identity

Logo design principles; brand guidelines; colour palettes and type systems; applying identity across media; stationery, packaging, signage; consistency and recognition; rebranding.

Key Fact: A logo must work at ALL scales — from a billboard to a favicon; simplicity and clarity are key.
Key Fact: Brand guidelines document: logo usage rules, colour palette (Pantone, CMYK, RGB), typography, imagery style, spacing requirements.
Key Fact: Consistency across all applications builds recognition — stationery, packaging, digital, signage must all speak the same visual language.
Key Fact: Rebranding involves updating an existing identity — it must balance familiarity with freshness.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Branding and Corporate Identity, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring branding and corporate identity. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with branding and corporate identity. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with branding and corporate identity. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to branding and corporate identity, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Graphic Communication Exam Tips:
Test your logo at postage-stamp size. Show it in COLOUR and BLACK-AND-WHITE. Apply it to at least 3 different items. Create simple brand guidelines (colours, fonts, spacing).

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: A good logo just needs to look great on a screen. Correct: A logo must work across ALL applications — business card, website, signage, uniform, packaging, social media. It must be legible at tiny sizes and reproduced in colour AND black-and-white. Test your logo at multiple scales.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Create a complete brand identity for a fictional company. Design the logo, choose colours and typography, and apply the identity across at least three different media. Include brand guidelines documentation.

A grade 9 response will: design a simple, scalable, memorable logo; define clear brand guidelines; apply the identity consistently across media; annotate every decision with rationale; show the logo working at different scales and in monochrome.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
