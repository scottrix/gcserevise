Photography: Camera Controls and Exposure

Aperture, shutter speed, ISO and their relationship; depth of field; motion blur and freeze; exposure compensation; metering modes; shooting in manual, aperture priority and shutter priority; histograms.

Key Fact: The exposure triangle: aperture (f-stop), shutter speed and ISO work together — changing one affects the others.
Key Fact: Aperture controls depth of field: wide aperture (f/1.8) = shallow DoF; narrow aperture (f/16) = deep DoF.
Key Fact: Shutter speed controls motion: fast (1/1000s) freezes action; slow (1/15s) creates blur and sense of movement.
Key Fact: ISO controls sensor sensitivity: low ISO (100) = clean image; high ISO (3200+) = noisy/grainy but usable in low light.

For Camera Controls and Exposure, you must know:

  • Topic-specific techniques, materials and processes
  • How to experiment with and refine approaches
  • How to record ideas and observations visually and through annotation
  • How to develop personal responses informed by contextual research
  • Relevant artists, designers or craftspeople and their contexts

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Create a series of experimental studies exploring camera controls and exposure. Annotate your process.

Q2: Research an artist or designer who works with camera controls and exposure. Write a critical analysis of their approach and explain how it has informed your own work.

Q3: Evaluate the effectiveness of your experiments with camera controls and exposure. Which approaches were most successful and why?

✅ Answers

  1. A range of experimental studies showing different approaches to camera controls and exposure, each annotated with media, technique and purpose. Show development from initial experiments to refined outcomes.
  2. A critical analysis naming a specific artist/designer, using specialist vocabulary, explaining their context and approach, and making clear connections to personal work.
  3. A reflective evaluation identifying successful and less successful experiments, explaining WHY using technical and conceptual language, and showing how evaluation led to further refinement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Address ALL four Assessment Objectives — AO1 (develop), AO2 (refine), AO3 (record), AO4 (present)
  • Show the FULL creative journey — from initial research to final outcome
  • Annotate your work using specialist vocabulary (not just "I like it")
  • Draw from primary sources wherever possible — real objects, not photos
  • Experiment with a RANGE of media and techniques before selecting
  • Make clear connections between artist research and your own developing work

📝 Exam Technique

Photography Exam Tips:
Show your camera SETTINGS for every image (aperture, shutter speed, ISO). Demonstrate UNDERSTANDING of the exposure triangle — not just what each setting does, but how they interact. Explain the CREATIVE REASON for each technical choice.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes with this topic. Wrong: Auto mode will give you the best exposure every time. Correct: Auto mode makes AVERAGE exposure decisions that may not suit your creative intention. Manual control lets you CHOOSE: shallow or deep depth of field? Frozen or blurred motion? Clean or grainy aesthetic? Understanding the exposure triangle gives you creative control auto mode cannot provide.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Photograph the same subject using three different aperture settings and three different shutter speeds. Create a contact sheet showing all exposures. Annotate the creative effect of each setting.

A grade 9 response will: demonstrate understanding of the exposure triangle; show deliberate use of aperture for depth of field control; show deliberate use of shutter speed for motion control; annotate with technical vocabulary (f-stop, DoF, motion blur, ISO noise); explain creative INTENT behind each technical choice.

Mark scheme: AO1 (develop through investigation) + AO2 (refine through experimentation) + AO3 (record ideas and observations) + AO4 (present personal response) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Art and Design has four equally-weighted Assessment Objectives: AO1 Develop ideas through investigations informed by contextual and other sources, demonstrating analytical and cultural understanding (25%); AO2 Refine ideas through experimenting and selecting appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes (25%); AO3 Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions in visual and other forms (25%); AO4 Present a personal, informed and meaningful response demonstrating critical understanding, realising intentions and making connections (25%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional independence, sustained investigation, sophisticated understanding of sources, confident and purposeful experimentation, perceptive recording, and a highly personal, coherent response that makes illuminating connections.
