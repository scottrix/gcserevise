Athens & the Birth of Democracy

The development of Athenian democracy from Solon through Cleisthenes to Pericles, and how the democratic system worked.

Key Fact: Athenian democracy developed in stages: Solon's reforms (594 BC) cancelled debts and banned debt slavery; Cleisthenes (508 BC) reorganised citizens into demes and tribes; Pericles (460s BC) introduced pay for office holders
Key Fact: The ecclesia (assembly) was the sovereign decision-making body: all male citizens could speak and vote on laws, war, treaties and ostracism
Key Fact: The boule (council of 500) set the agenda for the assembly and oversaw day-to-day administration; members were chosen by lot from the ten tribes
Key Fact: Ostracism allowed citizens to exile one person for 10 years by majority vote, removing potential tyrants
Key Fact: The court system (dikasteria) used large citizen juries (201-501 members) chosen by lot, ensuring popular justice
Key Fact: Democracy was direct (not representative): citizens voted on issues themselves, not through elected representatives

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Athens & the Birth of Democracy, you must know:

  • Ecclesia: The Athenian citizen assembly; the supreme decision-making body where all male citizens could speak and vote
  • Boule: The council of 500 citizens that set the agenda for the ecclesia and handled daily administration
  • Deme: A local administrative district in Attica; the basis of Cleisthenes' democratic reorganisation of citizens
  • Ostracism: A democratic procedure allowing the assembly to exile one citizen for 10 years by majority vote on pottery shards (ostraka)
  • Thetes: The lowest property class in Athens; rowers in the navy who gained political influence under radical democracy

✅ Answers

  1. Solon (594 BC) cancelled all debts (seisachtheia), banned debt slavery, and reorganised citizens into four property classes. This prevented civil war between rich and poor, freed those enslaved by debt, and laid foundations for later democracy by giving even the poorest some political rights.
  2. Cleisthenes (508 BC) reorganised Attica into 140 demes (local districts) and grouped them into ten new tribes, each containing demes from city, coast and inland areas. This broke the power of aristocratic families by mixing citizens across regions. The boule of 500 (50 from each tribe) was created, giving broader participation in government.
  3. Ostracism was a procedure where the assembly voted to exile one citizen for 10 years. Citizens wrote the name of the person they wanted exiled on pottery shards (ostraka). If at least 6,000 votes were cast, the person with the most votes was exiled. Its purpose was to remove potential tyrants or those becoming too powerful.
  4. Pericles introduced pay (misthophoria) for jury service and public office in the 450s BC, enabling even poor citizens (thetes) to participate in government without financial loss. He also made the boule selection by lot rather than election, reducing the influence of wealth and rhetoric.
  5. Athenian democracy was direct: citizens voted on laws and policies themselves in the assembly, not through elected representatives. Offices were filled by lot (random selection) rather than election. However, it excluded women, slaves and metics — probably only 10-20% of the population were citizens.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Three stages: Solon (594 BC), Cleisthenes (508 BC), Pericles (450s BC)
  • Direct democracy: citizens vote on issues, not representatives
  • Ostracism = 10-year exile by majority of 6,000+ votes on ostraka
  • Always note who was EXCLUDED: women, slaves, metics

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Athenian democracy included all residents ✓ Only free adult male citizens (perhaps 10-20% of the population) could participate. Women, slaves and metics were completely excluded from politics

✗ Solon created democracy ✓ Solon laid the foundations by cancelling debts and banning debt slavery, but full democracy was created by Cleisthenes (508 BC) and extended by Pericles (450s BC)

✗ Ostracism was a criminal punishment ✓ Ostracism was not a criminal penalty — no crime needed to be proved. It was a political tool to remove anyone deemed too powerful or a threat to democracy

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the development of Athenian democracy from Solon to Pericles and explain how the democratic system worked. [6 marks]

Athenian democracy developed in three key stages. Solon (594 BC) cancelled debts (seisachtheia), banned debt slavery and reorganised citizens into four property classes, preventing civil war and giving the poorest some political rights. Cleisthenes (508 BC) created the foundation of radical democracy by reorganising Attica into 140 demes and ten tribes that mixed citizens from city, coast and inland, breaking aristocratic power. He established the boule of 500 and introduced ostracism. Pericles (450s BC) extended democracy by introducing pay for jury service and public office, enabling poor citizens (thetes) to participate without financial hardship. The democratic system operated through several institutions. The ecclesia (assembly) was sovereign: all male citizens could speak, debate and vote on laws, war, treaties, finance and ostracism. It met about 40 times per year. The boule (council of 500, 50 from each tribe chosen by lot) set the assembly agenda and oversaw administration. The dikasteria (people's courts) used large juries chosen by lot to try legal cases. Most officials were selected by lot rather than election, reflecting the principle that any citizen was capable of governing. However, democracy excluded women, slaves and metics — only perhaps 30,000 of Athens' 250,000+ residents were full citizens.
