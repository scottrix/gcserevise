Roman Government & the Constitution

The Roman Republican constitution: consuls, Senate, assemblies, and the system of checks and balances.

Key Fact: The Republic was a mixed constitution with monarchical (consuls), aristocratic (Senate) and democratic (assemblies) elements
Key Fact: Two consuls were elected annually with imperium (power of command); they could veto each other (intercessio)
Key Fact: The Senate was the most powerful body: 300 patrician elders who controlled foreign policy, finances and state religion
Key Fact: The comitia centuriata (centuriate assembly) elected consuls and declared war; voting was weighted by wealth
Key Fact: Tribunes of the plebs (10 annually elected plebeian officers) could veto any magistrate's action and summon the Senate
Key Fact: In emergencies, a dictator could be appointed for six months with supreme power

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Roman Government & the Constitution, you must know:

  • Imperium: The power of command held by consuls and praetors; the authority to lead armies, interpret laws and enforce order
  • Intercessio: The right of a consul or tribune to veto the action of another magistrate; a key check on power
  • Tribune of the plebs: One of ten annually elected plebeian officers with the power to veto any magistrate and convene the plebeian assembly
  • Cursus honorum: The prescribed sequence of political offices: quaestor, aedile, praetor, consul; a minimum age applied to each
  • Dictator: A magistrate with supreme power appointed in emergencies for a maximum of six months; chosen by consuls and confirmed by the assembly

❓ Practice Questions

Q: How did the Roman Republic balance different elements of government?

Q: What powers did the Senate hold?

Q: Why were tribunes of the plebs important?

Q: What was the cursus honorum?

Q: When and why was a dictator appointed?

✅ Answers

  1. Consuls provided monarchical executive power (but there were two, each vetoing the other). The Senate provided aristocratic guidance and expertise. The assemblies provided democratic participation. This mixed constitution was admired by later political thinkers including Polybius.
  2. The Senate controlled foreign policy (declaring war, making treaties), managed state finances, supervised the treasury, appointed provincial governors, and issued decrees (senatus consulta) that were effectively law. Its 300 members served for life, giving it institutional memory and immense authority.
  3. Tribunes were the plebeians' protection against patrician oppression. They could veto any magistrate's action, convene the plebeian assembly, propose laws, and their persons were sacrosanct (inviolable). They were the primary mechanism through which plebeians gained political rights over time.
  4. The prescribed sequence of political offices: quaestor (financial administrator, minimum age 30), aedile (public works and games), praetor (judicial and military command, minimum age 39), and consul (chief magistrate, minimum age 42). Two-year intervals between offices were required.
  5. In extreme emergencies (military crisis or internal disorder), a dictator was appointed by the consuls for a maximum of six months with supreme authority. All other magistrates were subordinate to the dictator. The most famous example is Cincinnatus, who was appointed, defeated the enemy, and resigned after just 16 days.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Mixed constitution: monarchy (consuls) + aristocracy (Senate) + democracy (assemblies)
  • Senate = most powerful long-term; Consuls = executive; Tribunes = plebeian protection
  • Veto power was the key check: consuls veto each other, tribunes veto anyone
  • Dictator = 6-month emergency power, then must resign

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ The Roman Republic was a democracy like Athens ✓ Rome was a mixed constitution with strong aristocratic elements. The Senate (unelected patricians) held more real power than the assemblies. Voting in the comitia centuriata was heavily weighted toward the wealthy, unlike Athens' direct democracy

✗ The Senate made laws in the Republic ✓ The Senate technically only issued advisory decrees (senatus consulta), not laws. Laws were passed by the assemblies. However, in practice, the Senate's authority meant its decrees were almost always obeyed

✗ The dictatorship was like modern dictatorship ✓ The Roman dictator was a constitutional office with strict limits: maximum six months, appointed only in emergencies, and expected to resign as soon as the crisis passed. It was not arbitrary rule

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the Roman Republican constitution and explain how checks and balances prevented any individual from gaining too much power. [6 marks]

The Roman Republic was a mixed constitution combining monarchical, aristocratic and democratic elements. Two consuls, elected annually, held imperium (power of command) — providing monarchical executive authority. However, the existence of two consuls meant each could veto the other (intercessio), and their one-year term prevented any individual from building a permanent power base. The Senate, a body of 300 former magistrates serving for life, controlled foreign policy, finances and provincial appointments — the aristocratic element. Three assemblies provided democratic participation: the comitia centuriata elected consuls and declared war (but votes were weighted by wealth), the concilium plebis elected tribunes and passed plebiscites, and the comitia tribuna handled other legislation. Additional checks included: tribunes of the plebs (10 annually) could veto any magistrate's action and were sacrosanct; the cursus honorum required progressing through offices with minimum ages and intervals; and in emergencies a dictator could be appointed for maximum six months with supreme power. This system of checks and balances — mutual veto rights, annual terms, shared power and the requirement for colleagues in every office — was designed to prevent any individual from gaining the kind of absolute power that the last king, Tarquin Superbus, had abused. Polybius admired this system as the key to Rome's success.
