The Conflict of the Orders

The 200-year struggle between Patricians and Plebeians for political equality, from the First Secession to the Hortensian Law.

Key Fact: The Conflict of the Orders (494-287 BC) was the struggle between patricians and plebeians for political equality
Key Fact: The First Secession (494 BC): plebeians withdrew to the Sacred Mount, forcing the patricians to create the tribunes of the plebs
Key Fact: The Twelve Tables (451-450 BC): Rome's first written law code, created because plebeians were being judged by unwritten patrician customs
Key Fact: The Licinian-Sextian Laws (367 BC) required one consul to be a plebeian; the office was opened to plebeians
Key Fact: The Hortensian Law (287 BC) made plebiscites (laws passed by the plebeian assembly) binding on all citizens, effectively ending the Conflict
Key Fact: Key tactics: secession (plebeians refusing to serve in the army or work), tribunician veto, and threat of mass action

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For The Conflict of the Orders, you must know:

  • Secession: The plebeian tactic of withdrawing from the city en masse, refusing military service and labour until their demands were met
  • Twelve Tables: Rome's first written law code (451-450 BC), displayed publicly so all citizens could know the law; a plebeian victory
  • Licinian-Sextian Laws: Laws of 367 BC that required at least one consul to be a plebeian and limited land ownership
  • Hortensian Law: The law of 287 BC that made plebiscites (decisions of the plebeian assembly) binding on all Romans without Senate approval
  • Nexum: Debt bondage in early Rome; plebeians could be imprisoned or enslaved for unpaid debts, a key grievance in the Conflict

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What was the Conflict of the Orders?

Q: Why was the First Secession significant?

Q: Why were the Twelve Tables important?

Q: How did the Licinian-Sextian Laws change Roman politics?

Q: What was the significance of the Hortensian Law?

✅ Answers

  1. A 200-year struggle (494-287 BC) between patricians (aristocratic families who monopolised political power) and plebeians (common citizens who were excluded from office and lawmaking). The plebeians used secession, tribunician power and popular pressure to gradually win political equality.
  2. In 494 BC, plebeians withdrew en masse to the Sacred Mount, refusing military service. The patricians, facing military crisis, conceded by creating the tribunes of the plebs — officers with veto power to protect plebeian interests. This established the principle that plebeians could use collective action to force political change.
  3. Before the Twelve Tables (451-450 BC), law was unwritten and interpreted exclusively by patrician magistrates, often unfairly. The Tables published laws publicly for all to see, preventing arbitrary judgement. Though they contained harsh provisions, they represented a fundamental principle: equality before the law.
  4. In 367 BC, these laws required at least one of the two consuls to be plebeian, ended the military tribunate with consular power, and limited individual land ownership to 500 iugera. This opened the highest office to plebeians and addressed economic inequality.
  5. The Hortensian Law (287 BC) made plebiscites (laws passed by the plebeian assembly) binding on all Roman citizens, including patricians, without needing Senate approval. This effectively ended the Conflict of the Orders by giving the plebeian assembly full legislative power.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ The plebeians were poor and the patricians were rich ✓ The distinction was hereditary, not economic. Some plebeians were wealthy, and some patrician families had become poor. The conflict was about political and legal rights, not wealth

✗ The Conflict of the Orders ended with complete equality ✓ Although plebeians gained legal equality (right to hold any office, binding plebiscites), in practice the Senate and wealthy families (both patrician and plebeian) continued to dominate politics. A new elite of nobiles (patrician and plebeian families who had held consulships) emerged

✗ The plebeians won through violence ✓ The plebeians' most effective weapon was secession — peaceful withdrawal of their labour and military service. The patricians needed plebeian soldiers and workers, so this economic pressure was more effective than violence

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the main events of the Conflict of the Orders and explain how the plebeians achieved political equality. [6 marks]

The Conflict of the Orders (494-287 BC) was a struggle between patricians, who monopolised political power, and plebeians, who were excluded from office and law. The plebeians' main tactic was secession: withdrawing from the city and refusing military service and labour. The First Secession (494 BC) forced patricians to create tribunes of the plebs — officers with veto power to protect plebeian interests. A second key milestone was the Twelve Tables (451-450 BC), Rome's first written law code. Previously, law was unwritten and interpreted by patrician magistrates arbitrarily. The Tables published laws publicly, establishing the principle that all citizens could know and reference the law. The Licinian-Sextian Laws (367 BC) required one consul to be plebeian and limited land ownership to address economic inequality. Over subsequent decades, further offices were opened: the praetorship (337 BC), the censorship (339 BC requiring one censor be plebeian) and eventually both consulships could be held by plebeians (172 BC). The final milestone was the Hortensian Law (287 BC), which made plebiscites (laws passed by the plebeian assembly) binding on all citizens without Senate approval. This gave the plebeian assembly full legislative equality. However, practical power remained with the wealthy: a new elite of nobiles (both patrician and plebeian families who had held consulships) continued to dominate the Senate. Legal equality did not mean genuine democratic equality.
