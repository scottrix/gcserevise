L7: Structure Analysis

Foundation Higher All Boards AO2

Analysing structural choices in texts - how writers organise and arrange their work.

📋 What is Structure?

Definition: Structure is how a text is organised - the arrangement of events, sections, acts, chapters, or stanzas. Structure analysis asks: "How has the writer ordered this? Why here? Why now?"

Structure is different from form:

  • Form = what type of text (play, novel, poem)
  • Structure = how it's organised (acts, chapters, stanzas)
Key Structural Questions:
• How does the text begin? End?
• What comes before/after a key moment?
• How is information revealed?
• Is the order chronological? Why?

🎭 Dramatic Structure

Act and Scene Divisions

Plays are divided into acts (major sections) and scenes (smaller units). These divisions are structural choices.

"Macbeth" - Five-Act Structure
  • Act 1: Exposition - witches, prophecy, Duncan's trust
  • Act 2: Rising action - Duncan's murder, discovery
  • Act 3: Climax - Banquo's murder, ghost, Macbeth's tyranny
  • Act 4: Falling action - witches' prophecies, Macduff's family killed
  • Act 5: Resolution - Lady Macbeth's death, Macbeth's defeat

Scene Length and Pacing

Technique Effect Example
Long scenes Slows pace, allows development Duncan's arrival at Macbeth's castle
Short scenes Accelerates pace, creates urgency Act 5's rapid battle scenes
Alternating locations Creates contrast, parallel action Macbeth vs Macduff's family

Entrances and Exits

Significance of Entrances/Exits in "Macbeth"
  • Witches - appear suddenly, vanish mysteriously - supernatural power
  • Duncan - enters praising Macbeth, exits to die - dramatic irony
  • Banquo's ghost - enters during feast, visible only to Macbeth - psychological horror
  • Lady Macbeth - exits early in Act 5, returns dead - her story ends off-stage

Opening and Closing Scenes

Structural Significance: Opening scenes establish key themes, mood, and expectations. Closing scenes resolve (or complicate) the text's central conflicts and deliver final messages.
"Macbeth" Opening and Closing
  • Opens: Witches - supernatural, "fair is foul" - theme of appearance vs reality established
  • Closes: Malcolm's speech - order restored, but questions remain about future

📖 Narrative Structure

Chronological vs Non-Linear

Type Definition Effect
Chronological Events in time order Clear cause and effect, natural progression
Non-linear Events out of order Mystery, psychological truth, contrast
Cyclical Ends where it began Suggests repetition, inevitability, stasis
"A Christmas Carol" - Non-Linear Structure

Stave 1: Present - Scrooge introduced
Staves 2-3: Past - childhood, Fezziwig, Belle
Stave 4: Future - Scrooge's death
Stave 5: Present - Scrooge transformed

Effect: Ghosts force Scrooge to confront his past and future, creating the conditions for transformation. Past explains who he became; future shows consequences. Present shows change is possible.

Cyclical Structure

"An Inspector Calls" - Cyclical Ending

The play ends with another phone call about a dead girl, repeating the pattern. This suggests:

  • The Birlings haven't learned their lesson
  • Without social change, tragedy repeats
  • The Inspector's warning is urgent
  • Time loop reinforces moral message

📚 Chapter Structure

Chapter Significance

Writers choose where to break chapters. Each chapter has a purpose.

"Jekyll and Hyde" - Titled Chapters
  • "Story of the Door" - mystery established
  • "Search for Mr Hyde" - investigation begins
  • "Dr Jekyll Was Quite at Ease" - contrast with Hyde
  • "The Carew Murder Case" - horror escalates
  • "Incident at the Window" - Jekyll's distress
  • "The Last Night" - climax, Hyde revealed
  • "Dr Lanyon's Narrative" - truth from outside perspective
  • "Henry Jekyll's Full Statement" - confession, full truth

Effect: Each chapter reveals information gradually, building mystery. The final two chapters provide different perspectives, piecing together the truth.

🔄 Structural Patterns

Parallel Scenes

Scenes that echo each other - creates contrast or development.

Parallel Scenes in "Macbeth"
  • Opening witches vs Act 4 witches - prophecies begin and continue
  • Duncan's murder vs Banquo's murder - Macbeth escalates, loses control
  • Lady Macbeth mocks Macbeth vs Macbeth dismisses Lady Macbeth - power shift

Juxtaposition

Placing contrasting elements together for effect.

Juxtaposition in "A Christmas Carol"
  • Fezziwig's party vs Scrooge's lonely office - contrast in treatment of workers
  • Cratchit family dinner vs Scrooge's cold lodgings - warmth vs isolation
  • Ignorance and Want vs Scrooge's wealth - social responsibility

⏱️ Pacing and Time

Time Compression

Weeks or years pass quickly - summary, not scene.

"Macbeth" - Time Compression

The play covers months, but feels rushed. Time compresses as Macbeth's reign accelerates. This creates a sense of spiralling out of control.

Real Time

Events unfold at the same pace as audience experience.

"An Inspector Calls" - Real Time

The play takes about as long to perform as the events take in-story. This creates:

  • Urgency - no time to think
  • Claustrophobia - trapped in the dining room
  • Intensity - interrogation in real time

📊 Narrative Perspective Shifts

"Jekyll and Hyde" - Perspective Shifts
  1. Utterson - lawyer investigating, limited perspective
  2. Lanyon - scientist witnessing transformation, horror
  3. Jekyll - first-person confession, full truth

Structural Effect: Information revealed gradually; mystery maintained; truth pieced together from multiple sources.

✍️ How to Write About Structure

Golden Rule: Don't just describe the structure - explain WHY the writer chose it and WHAT EFFECT it creates. Ask: "Why THIS order? Why HERE?"

Useful Sentence Starters

  • "The writer positions X at the beginning/end to..."
  • "By placing X before/after Y, the writer creates..."
  • "The cyclical structure suggests..."
  • "The shift in perspective allows..."
  • "Structuring the text in X way means that..."
Good Structural Analysis

Shakespeare positions the witches at the very opening, before we meet Macbeth. This structural choice establishes the supernatural and the theme of appearance vs reality from the start, foreshadowing Macbeth's moral corruption. The witches' final appearance in Act 4 marks the turning point after which Macbeth's downfall accelerates, framing his tragedy within their prophecies.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It's Wrong How to Fix It
Confusing structure with form Form = type; structure = organisation Ask: "What IS it?" vs "How is it ARRANGED?"
Describing structure without effect Examiners know the structure - they want analysis Always explain WHY and EFFECT
Only discussing beginning/end Structure includes all organisational choices Consider ordering, pacing, shifts, patterns
Forgetting structural terminology Shows lack of technical knowledge Learn terms: cyclical, chronological, juxtaposition

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What is the effect of a cyclical structure?

Q2: How does Shakespeare use scene length to control pacing in "Macbeth"?

Q3: Why does "A Christmas Carol" use a non-linear structure with ghosts from past, present, and future?

Q4: What structural technique creates mystery in "Jekyll and Hyde"?

✅ Answers

  1. A cyclical structure (ending where it began) suggests repetition, inevitability, or that nothing has changed. It can create a sense of entrapment or reinforce themes. In "An Inspector Calls," the cyclical ending suggests the Birlings haven't learned, and without social change, tragedy will repeat.
  2. Shakespeare varies scene length: long scenes (Duncan's arrival) slow the pace for development; short scenes (Act 5 battles) accelerate pace for urgency and climax. As Macbeth's situation deteriorates, scenes become shorter, creating a sense of spiralling out of control.
  3. The non-linear structure forces Scrooge (and readers) to see cause and effect. Past explains how he became cold; present shows consequences; future warns of death. This structural journey creates the conditions for transformation - he must see his whole life to change it.
  4. "Jekyll and Hyde" uses multiple narrators (Utterson, Lanyon, Jekyll) to reveal information gradually. Each narrator has limited perspective, so truth is pieced together slowly. The final two chapters provide documents and confessions, structurally delaying full revelation until the end.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Know the overall structure of each text (acts, chapters, stanzas)
  • Consider WHY events are ordered this way
  • Track how information is revealed
  • Analyse openings and endings
  • Look for patterns: parallels, contrasts, cycles
  • Connect structure to themes and character development
  • Use structural vocabulary: cyclical, chronological, juxtaposition, pacing
