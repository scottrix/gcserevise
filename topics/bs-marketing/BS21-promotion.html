Promotion

How businesses promote their products: advertising (TV, print, digital, social media), sales promotion, public relations, personal selling, sponsorship, and the choice of promotional methods for different target markets and budgets.

Key Fact: Advertising: paid communication through media channels (TV, radio, print, online, social media, billboards). It builds awareness and brand image but is expensive and difficult to measure ROI directly.
Key Fact: Sales promotion: short-term incentives to encourage purchase (BOGOF, discounts, coupons, free gifts, competitions). Boosts immediate sales but can damage brand image if overused.
Key Fact: Public relations (PR): managing the business's image through media coverage, press releases, events, and community involvement. It's credible (not paid for) but the business can't control the message.
Key Fact: Personal selling: one-to-one communication (sales reps, demonstrations). Effective for high-value, complex products but expensive per customer and limited reach.
Key Fact: Digital promotion: social media marketing, influencer partnerships, email campaigns, SEO, and content marketing. Cost-effective, highly targeted, measurable, but requires ongoing content creation and platform expertise.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain the difference between advertising and sales promotion, giving one advantage and one disadvantage of each.

Q2: Analyse why a business might choose social media marketing over traditional TV advertising.

Q3: Evaluate which promotional method would be most effective for a new luxury skincare brand targeting 25-35 year-old women.

✅ Answers

  1. Advertising: paid messages through media to build brand awareness and image. Advantage: reaches large audiences, builds long-term brand recognition. Disadvantage: expensive (TV adverts cost thousands), difficult to measure direct impact on sales. Sales promotion: short-term offers like discounts, BOGOF, coupons. Advantage: creates immediate sales boost, easy to measure effectiveness. Disadvantage: may attract bargain hunters who don't become loyal customers, can devalue the brand if used too often.
  2. Social media marketing advantages: highly targeted (demographics, interests, behaviours), lower cost than TV, interactive (customers can comment/share), measurable (clicks, conversions, engagement), builds two-way relationships. TV advertising advantages: mass reach, prestige/credibility, emotional impact through storytelling, reaches older demographics less active on social media. However, TV is expensive and less targeted - a local business would waste money reaching viewers outside its area.
  3. For a luxury skincare brand targeting 25-35 women: social media/influencer marketing is most effective - this demographic spends significant time on Instagram and TikTok, follows beauty influencers, and trusts peer recommendations. Influencer partnerships provide authentic endorsements, targeted reach, and measurable engagement. PR (magazine features, beauty awards) adds credibility. TV advertising would be too expensive and insufficiently targeted. Personal selling isn't practical for consumer skincare. A mix of influencer marketing + social media ads + PR would be most cost-effective and aligned with the target market's media habits.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always match the promotional method to the TARGET MARKET: where do they spend time and who do they trust?
  • When evaluating promotion, consider the BUDGET: small businesses can't afford TV but can afford social media.
  • Remember the AIDA model: Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action. Different promotional methods work at different stages.

📝 Exam Technique

Business Exam Tips:
When evaluating promotional methods, use the TAM framework: Target market reach (does it reach the right people?), Affordability (does it fit the budget?), Measurability (can the business track results?). The best promotional mix combines methods that work at different stages of the AIDA model.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Social media marketing is free, so it's the best option for all businesses. Correct: While creating social media accounts is free, effective social media marketing requires: paid advertising spend (organic reach is very limited), content creation (photos, videos, copywriting), staff time for management and engagement, and potentially influencer fees. A badly managed social media presence can damage a brand. For businesses targeting demographics less active on social media (e.g. over-65s), traditional media may be more effective. The best promotional mix depends on the target market, budget, and product type.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate whether a local plumbing business should invest in social media advertising or local newspaper advertising.

A grade 9 response will: analyse social media (Facebook/Nextdoor can target local postcodes, lower cost per lead, customer reviews build trust, but plumbers' customers may search when they have an emergency rather than scroll social media); local newspaper (reaches local homeowners, traditional audience who may need plumbing, trust in established local paper, but declining readership, expensive, hard to measure ROI); consider the customer journey (most people search Google for a local plumber in an emergency - so Google Ads or SEO may actually be most effective); conclude: a combination approach works best - Google Ads for emergency search, local Facebook/Nextdoor for building awareness and trust, and newspaper only if it offers good value for the local demographic. The key is being visible when the customer needs the service.
