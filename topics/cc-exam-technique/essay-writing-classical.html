Essay Writing Skills

How to structure and write high-scoring essays for OCR J280

Key Fact: A good essay has: introduction, 3-4 developed paragraphs, conclusion
Key Fact: The introduction should define key terms and indicate your line of argument
Key Fact: Use PEEL: Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link back to the question
Key Fact: Evidence must be specific — named characters, events, sources and cultural details
Key Fact: Classical Civilisation essays require evidence from both literary and visual/material sources
Key Fact: Explanation means showing how evidence supports your argument
Key Fact: The conclusion must directly answer the question with a clear, justified judgement
Key Fact: For 'how far' essays, present both sides before reaching a judgement
Key Fact: Signposting language helps the examiner follow your argument
Key Fact: Always plan before writing — even 2 minutes improves coherence
Key Fact: Top-band answers show sustained analysis, precise classical evidence and convincing judgement
Key Fact: Time management: roughly 1 minute per mark

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Essay Writing Skills, you must know:

  • PEEL: Paragraph structure: Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link
  • Line of argument: The overall thesis running through your essay, connecting all paragraphs
  • Judgement: A clear, reasoned decision about the question, supported by evidence
  • Signposting: Using connecting words to guide the reader through your argument
  • Sustained analysis: Consistent examination of evidence throughout the essay
  • Counter-argument: An opposing viewpoint acknowledged before explaining why your position is stronger

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What does PEEL stand for?

Q: What should an introduction include?

Q: Why is a conclusion essential?

Q: How does a 'how far' essay differ from an 'explain' essay?

Q: What distinguishes a top-band answer?

✅ Answers

  1. Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link — ensures each paragraph is focused and developed.
  2. Definition of key terms, understanding of the question, and indication of your line of argument.
  3. Without a conclusion delivering a justified judgement, you cannot access the top mark band.
  4. 'How far' requires presenting both sides before a judgement; 'explain' focuses on giving reasons.
  5. Sustained analysis, precise classical evidence from literary and visual sources, and a convincing judgement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Spend 2-3 minutes planning before writing
  • Start each paragraph with a clear topic sentence
  • Use specific named evidence from the classical world
  • In your conclusion, make a clear definitive judgement
  • If running out of time, write a brief conclusion rather than none

📝 Exam Technique

GCSE Classical Civilisation Exam Tips:
Use precise classical terminology in all answers. When analysing sources, always consider provenance (who created it, when, and why). For literary questions, reference specific episodes and book numbers from set texts. For thematic questions, compare Greek and Roman approaches where relevant. For 'how far' questions, present a balanced argument with a clear judgement. OCR J280 rewards answers that demonstrate understanding of the classical world through use of specific evidence from both literary and visual/material sources.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ A conclusion that just repeats the introduction ✓ The conclusion should deliver your final judgement, synthesising arguments from your paragraphs

✗ Listing facts without explaining how they answer the question ✓ Every piece of evidence needs explanation showing how it supports your argument

✗ One-sided argument for a 'how far' question ✓ Always present evidence for and against before reaching your judgement

✗ Starting to write immediately without planning ✓ Even a brief plan ensures clear structure and covers all required points

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

How far was xenia the most important value in the world of the Odyssey? (15 marks)

Xenia was a central value in the Odyssey, but not clearly more important than loyalty, cunning or family. Xenia structures many episodes: the Phaeacians show ideal hospitality, Eumaeus demonstrates true xenia despite poverty, and the suitors are killed for violating it. The code is divinely enforced by Zeus Xenios, giving it religious authority other values lack. The suitors' deaths show that violating xenia is a capital offence. However, loyalty is equally central — Penelope's faithfulness for twenty years, Eumaeus's devotion and Argos's patient waiting show that loyalty drives the poem's emotional power. Cunning (metis) is fundamental — Odysseus's entire strategy depends on it. Family bonds are the deepest motivation — Odysseus endures twenty years of suffering to return to Penelope and Telemachus. In conclusion, xenia was one of several equally important values. Its divine enforcement makes it uniquely powerful, but loyalty, cunning and family love are equally essential to the poem's meaning and to Odysseus's success.
