Command Words & Question Types

Understanding what OCR J280 exam questions are really asking

Key Fact: OCR J280 uses specific command words that determine the depth and type of response required
Key Fact: 'Describe' means give an account of what happened or what something was like
Key Fact: 'Explain' requires giving reasons or causes — show how and why something happened
Key Fact: 'Explain the significance' demands you show both what happened and why it mattered
Key Fact: 'How far' questions require a balanced argument with a judgement
Key Fact: 'Assess the validity' means evaluate how true a statement is using evidence
Key Fact: 'Compare' requires identifying similarities AND differences
Key Fact: Short-answer questions (2-4 marks) need concise, focused responses with specific evidence
Key Fact: Medium-mark questions (6-8 marks) need developed explanations with precise classical examples
Key Fact: Extended questions (12-15 marks) require sustained argument, analysis and a clear judgement
Key Fact: Paper 1 focuses on thematic analysis; Paper 2 focuses on literary analysis
Key Fact: Always identify the command word before planning your answer

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Command Words & Question Types, you must know:

  • Command word: The instruction word in a question that tells you what kind of response is needed
  • Describe: Give a detailed account of what happened or what something was like
  • Explain: Give reasons for why something happened or why it was significant
  • Assess: Make a judgement about importance or validity, supported by evidence
  • How far: Evaluate the extent to which a statement is true, presenting both sides before a judgement
  • Validity: How truthful or accurate a statement or interpretation is

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What is the key difference between 'describe' and 'explain'?

Q: What must you include in a 'how far' answer?

Q: How should you approach a 'compare' question?

Q: Which paper focuses on literary analysis in OCR J280?

Q: What does 'assess the validity' of a statement mean?

✅ Answers

  1. Describe requires an account of what happened (factual detail), while explain requires reasons why it happened (causation and significance).
  2. A balanced argument showing both sides, supported by specific evidence, leading to a clear judgement.
  3. Identify both similarities and differences using specific evidence, linking the comparison to the question's focus.
  4. Paper 2 (The Homeric World) focuses on literary analysis of Homer's Odyssey.
  5. Evaluate how true it is by presenting evidence for and against, then reaching a judgement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Circle or underline the command word before writing
  • For 'how far' questions, plan both sides for balance
  • Short-answer: one specific point per mark
  • Extended answers need 3-4 developed paragraphs plus a conclusion
  • Always use evidence from classical sources, not just general knowledge

📝 Exam Technique

GCSE Classical Civilisation Exam Tips — Command Words & Question Types:
1. For Command Words & Question Types, use classical terminology and reference specific ancient sources
2. When analysing texts, consider both content and literary techniques
3. Place {topic_name} in its cultural and historical context

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Describing when the question says 'explain why' ✓ Focus on causes and reasons — use 'because', 'due to', 'as a result of'

✗ One-sided answer to a 'how far' question ✓ Present both sides before making your judgement

✗ Writing everything you know regardless of the command word ✓ Tailor your answer to the command word

✗ Ignoring the command word entirely ✓ The command word tells you exactly what the examiner wants

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Explain why understanding command words is essential for success in OCR J280 Classical Civilisation (8 marks)

Understanding command words is essential because they determine the type of response the examiner expects. 'Describe' requires factual detail about what happened, while 'explain' demands reasons and causes. Misreading a command word can lead to a low mark even with strong knowledge. OCR J280 has two papers with different emphases: Paper 1 uses thematic command words, while Paper 2 requires literary analysis. Each paper's command words reflect different skills, so understanding them ensures the candidate applies the correct approach. Ultimately, command words are the key to unlocking what the question demands.
