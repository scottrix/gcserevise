📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Paper 2: Homeric World Technique, you must know:

  • Homeric World: Paper 2 of OCR J280 focusing on Homer's Odyssey
  • Epic simile: An extended comparison used by Homer — often several lines long
  • Nostos: Homecoming — the central theme of the Odyssey
  • Divine intervention: When a god directly influences events — Athena helps Odysseus throughout
  • Foreshadowing: Hints about what will happen later — Homer frequently foreshadows the suitors' fate
  • In medias res: Beginning in the middle of the story — the Odyssey starts with Telemachus, not the Trojan War

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always use specific episode references with book numbers
  • Know key episodes in detail: Cyclops, Circe, Sirens, return to Ithaca, bow contest
  • Character complexity is rewarded — discuss flaws as well as strengths
  • Compare characters when relevant — Eumaeus vs suitors on xenia, Odysseus vs Achilles on heroism
  • Homer's techniques (epic simile, foreshadowing) are often examined — know them with examples

📝 Exam Technique

GCSE Classical Civilisation Exam Tips:
Use precise classical terminology in all answers. When analysing sources, always consider provenance (who created it, when, and why). For literary questions, reference specific episodes and book numbers from set texts. For thematic questions, compare Greek and Roman approaches where relevant. For 'how far' questions, present a balanced argument with a clear judgement. OCR J280 rewards answers that demonstrate understanding of the classical world through use of specific evidence from both literary and visual/material sources.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Writing about the Odyssey in general terms without specific episodes ✓ Always reference specific books and episodes with detailed evidence

✗ Discussing what happens without analysing why it matters ✓ Always explain the significance for character, theme and Homer's purpose

✗ Describing Odysseus as simply a great hero without acknowledging flaws ✓ Odysseus is complex — his hubris, recklessness and deceit are as important as his cunning

✗ Confusing Paper 1 thematic approach with Paper 2 literary approach ✓ Paper 2 requires literary analysis of the Odyssey as a text — focus on Homer's techniques

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

How far is the Odyssey a poem about homecoming rather than adventure? (15 marks)

The Odyssey is fundamentally about homecoming (nostos) rather than adventure, though the adventures are essential to developing its themes. Homecoming is the poem's central purpose — every episode, character and theme serves Odysseus's return to Ithaca. The opening lines declare the subject as the man who strove to win his friends' return. Penelope's faithfulness, Telemachus's journey and the suitors' occupation all revolve around Odysseus's absence. Even the adventures serve homecoming: each monster tests whether his desire for nostos can overcome the temptations of forgetfulness, comfort and knowledge. The second half, set entirely in Ithaca, is purely about homecoming — the disguise, planning and violent reclamation. However, the adventures are not merely obstacles — they are what makes Odysseus the hero who deserves to return. Without the Cyclops, Circe and the Underworld, his homecoming would lack the depth of suffering and demonstration of arete that make it meaningful. The adventures also allow Homer to explore themes that homecoming alone could not: civilisation, heroism and the relationship between mortals and gods. Overall, the Odyssey is primarily about homecoming, but the adventures give that homecoming its significance, making the poem about the journey towards home as much as the destination.
