Odysseus the Hero

Odysseus as an epic hero: his character, cunning and the Greek ideal of heroism

Key Fact: Odysseus is the archetypal Greek hero whose intelligence (metis) is as important as his physical strength
Key Fact: The Odyssey tells the story of Odysseus's ten-year journey home (nostos) from the Trojan War
Key Fact: Odysseus's key qualities: cunning, eloquence, endurance, bravery, curiosity and leadership
Key Fact: Unlike Achilles (who chose glory and early death), Odysseus chooses survival and homecoming
Key Fact: Odysseus's name means 'man of pain' or 'trouble' — his suffering is central to his identity
Key Fact: He invented the Trojan Horse, demonstrating his preference for cunning over brute force
Key Fact: His encounter with Polyphemus shows both his cleverness (the 'Nobody' trick) and his fatal flaw (revealing his true name)
Key Fact: Odysseus is not a perfect hero — he can be arrogant, reckless and deceitful
Key Fact: His disguise as a beggar upon returning to Ithaca shows his mastery of deception and patience
Key Fact: Penelope's faithfulness and cunning (unraveling the shroud) mirrors Odysseus's own intelligence
Key Fact: Telemachus's coming-of-age journey in Books 1-4 parallels his father's heroism
Key Fact: The Odyssey explores what it means to be a hero in peacetime, not just in war

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Odysseus the Hero, you must know:

  • Metis: Cunning intelligence or craft — Odysseus's defining quality, valued as highly as physical prowess
  • Nostos: Homecoming — the central theme of the Odyssey, Odysseus's journey back to Ithaca
  • Kleos: Glory or fame — the heroic reputation earned through great deeds, central to the Iliad
  • Xenia: The sacred code of hospitality between guest and host — constantly tested in the Odyssey
  • Hubris: Excessive pride — Odysseus's shout of his true name to Polyphemus is an act of hubris
  • Aeolia: The island of Aeolus, keeper of the winds — Odysseus's crew opened the bag of winds, blowing them off course

❓ Practice Questions

Q: How does Odysseus differ from Achilles as a hero?

Q: Why was shouting his name to Polyphemus a fatal error?

Q: How does Penelope mirror Odysseus's character?

Q: What does Odysseus's name suggest about his character?

Q: How does the Odyssey redefine heroism for peacetime?

✅ Answers

  1. Achilles represents physical prowess and the pursuit of kleos (glory) through death in battle. Odysseus represents metis (cunning intelligence) and the pursuit of nostos (homecoming) through survival and endurance.
  2. It allowed Polyphemus to call down his father Poseidon's curse on Odysseus, causing the ten-year delay. It was an act of hubris — valuing his reputation over his safety and his crew's lives.
  3. Penelope's trick of unraveling Laertes' shroud shows the same cunning and patience as Odysseus. Like him, she uses intelligence to survive in a hostile situation.
  4. 'Odysseus' means 'man of pain' or 'trouble' — he both suffers and causes suffering, reflecting the complex nature of his heroism.
  5. The Odyssey shows that heroism is not only about battlefield glory — it also requires patience, endurance, cunning and the ability to rebuild a life after war.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always balance Odysseus's positive qualities with his flaws — he is a complex, not idealised, hero
  • The Polyphemus episode is the most commonly tested — know it in detail
  • Compare Odysseus with other characters who show (or lack) xenia, cunning or restraint
  • Penelope and Telemachus are important secondary heroes — don't neglect them
  • Use specific book references when possible to show detailed knowledge of the text

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Describing Odysseus as a perfect hero without flaws ✓ Odysseus is complex — his hubris, deceit andrecklessness cause real harm, especially to his crew

✗ Confusing kleos (glory) with nostos (homecoming) ✓ Kleos is the warrior's glory of the Iliad; nostos is the homecoming of the Odyssey — the two epics prioritise different heroic values

✗ Only discussing Odysseus's physical courage ✓ Metis (cunning intelligence) is his defining quality — the Odyssey values cleverness over brute strength

✗ Forgetting that Penelope is also heroic ✓ Penelope's cunning (the shroud trick) and endurance rival Odysseus's own — she is a heroic figure in her own right

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

How far is Odysseus a more admirable hero than Achilles? (15 marks)

Odysseus is arguably a more admirable hero than Achilles because his qualities serve survival and homecoming rather than personal glory, though both heroes embody different but equally valid Greek ideals. Odysseus's metis (cunning intelligence) is more broadly useful than Achilles's raw martial prowess — Odysseus saves his crew from Polyphemus through wit, resists the Sirens through preparation, and reclaims his home through patience and disguise. His choice of nostos (homecoming) over kleos (glory) shows a hero who values life, family and community over personal fame. He endures twenty years of suffering and temptation without losing his determination to return, showing extraordinary resilience. Furthermore, Odysseus's heroism is relevant to peacetime — his patience, adaptability and emotional endurance are qualities needed in ordinary life. However, Achilles also has admirable qualities: his absolute commitment to his principles, his willingness to die for glory, and his genuine grief for Patroclus show emotional depth and integrity. Achilles's rage at Agamemnon for dishonouring him reflects a genuine concern for justice, while Odysseus sometimes deceives and manipulates for self-serving reasons. Odysseus's hubris in revealing his name to Polyphemus shows poor judgement that costs his crew their lives. Overall, Odysseus is more admirable for his adaptability, endurance and commitment to home, but his moral complexity makes him a more realistic rather than a simply better hero than Achilles.
