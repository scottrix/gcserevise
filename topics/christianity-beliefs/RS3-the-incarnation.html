RS3: The Incarnation

All Boards

Beliefs about the incarnation and Jesus as the Son of God; fully human and fully divine

The Meaning of the Incarnation

Key Fact: The incarnation is the Christian belief that God became human in the person of Jesus Christ. "Incarnation" means "made flesh" — from the Latin "caro" (flesh).

Christians believe that in Jesus, God took on human nature while remaining fully divine. This is one of the most distinctive and important Christian doctrines.

  • Jesus is fully God — he had divine power, authority and knowledge
  • Jesus is fully human — he experienced hunger, thirst, pain, temptation and emotion
  • The incarnation shows God's love — God was willing to enter human experience and suffering

Jesus as the Son of God

The title "Son of God" does not mean God had a biological son. It expresses the unique relationship between God the Father and Jesus, and affirms Jesus' divine nature.

  • At Jesus' baptism, a voice from heaven said "You are my Son, whom I love" (Mark 1:11)
  • Jesus referred to God as "Abba" (Father), indicating an intimate relationship
  • The title affirms that Jesus shares God's nature and authority
  • It distinguishes Jesus from other prophets — he is not just a messenger but God himself
Exam Tip: Be prepared to explain why the incarnation matters — it enables salvation (Jesus could bridge the gap between God and humanity), reveals God's nature, and shows God understands human suffering.

Why the Incarnation Matters

  • Revelation: Through Jesus, humans can see what God is like — "Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father" (John 14:9)
  • Salvation: Only someone both human and divine could bridge the gap between a holy God and sinful humanity
  • Empathy: Because God became human and experienced suffering, Christians believe God truly understands human pain
  • Dignity of humanity: The incarnation affirms that human beings have worth — God chose to become one

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Explain two reasons why the incarnation is important for Christians.

Q: "Jesus was just a good teacher, not the Son of God." Evaluate this statement.

✅ Answers

  1. 1) The incarnation enables salvation — because Jesus is both fully God and fully human, he can bridge the gap between a holy God and sinful humanity. His divine nature gives his sacrifice infinite worth; his human nature means he can represent humanity. 2) The incarnation reveals God's nature — through Jesus' life and teachings, Christians can understand what God is like. As Jesus said, "Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father" (John 14:9).
  2. Some people argue Jesus was merely a moral teacher because his teachings about love, forgiveness and justice are valuable regardless of his divine status. However, Christians would disagree because: Jesus claimed a unique relationship with God (calling him "Abba"); the resurrection confirms his divine identity; his miracles demonstrate supernatural authority; and his death and resurrection would have no saving power if he were only human. The incarnation is central to Christian faith — without it, there is no atonement or salvation.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity beliefs questions: Always use specific biblical references (e.g. Genesis 1, John 1:1-3, John 3:16). Explain different Christian interpretations (literal vs non-literal, Catholic vs Protestant). Use key terms precisely (omnipotent, Trinity, incarnation, atonement). For evaluation questions, consider both religious and secular viewpoints before reaching a justified conclusion.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often confuse the Trinity as three gods rather than one God in three persons. Wrong: "Christians believe in three gods." Correct: "Christians believe in one God who exists as three persons — Father, Son and Holy Spirit."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that beliefs about the incarnation and jesus as the son of god; fully human and fully divine.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
