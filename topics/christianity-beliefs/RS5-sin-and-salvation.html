RS5: Sin and Salvation

All Boards

Original sin; the means of salvation including law, grace and Spirit; atonement

Sin and Original Sin

Key Fact: Sin is any action that goes against God's will. Christians believe that sin separates humans from God and that all people have a tendency to sin (original sin), which stems from Adam and Eve's disobedience in Genesis 3.
  • Original sin — the first sin committed by Adam and Eve; its consequences affect all humanity
  • Some Christians believe original sin is inherited (Augustine's view); others see it as the tendency to sin that all humans share
  • Original sin explains why humans are imperfect and need salvation
  • The doctrine of original sin is particularly important in Catholic theology

Salvation

Salvation means being saved from sin and its consequences. Christians believe salvation is made possible through Jesus Christ.

  • Through law — following God's commandments and living a moral life
  • Through grace — God's unmerited favour; salvation is a gift from God that cannot be earned (Ephesians 2:8-9: "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God")
  • Through the Spirit — the Holy Spirit empowers Christians to live transformed lives
Exam Tip: Different Christian traditions emphasise different aspects of salvation. Protestants tend to emphasise grace and faith alone (sola fide), while Catholics emphasise the importance of both faith and good works cooperating with grace.

Atonement

Atonement means making amends for sin and restoring the relationship between God and humanity. Christians believe Jesus achieved this through his death on the cross.

  • Substitutionary atonement: Jesus died in the place of sinners, taking the punishment that humanity deserved
  • Sacrifice: Jesus is described as the "Lamb of God" (John 1:29) — the perfect sacrifice that ends the need for all other sacrifices
  • Moral influence: Jesus' self-sacrificial love inspires humans to turn to God and live differently
  • Victory: Through the cross and resurrection, Jesus defeated sin and death

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Explain two Christian beliefs about salvation.

Q: "People can be good without God." Evaluate this statement with reference to Christian teaching.

✅ Answers

  1. 1) Salvation through grace — Christians believe that humans cannot earn salvation through their own efforts; it is a free gift from God, received through faith (Ephesians 2:8-9). 2) Salvation through Christ's atonement — Jesus' death on the cross paid the penalty for sin, making it possible for humans to be reconciled with God. Without Jesus' sacrifice, the gap between a holy God and sinful humanity could not be bridged.
  2. Many would agree that non-religious people can live morally good lives, showing kindness, justice and compassion. However, from a Christian perspective, true goodness comes from God, and without God there is no ultimate standard of morality. Christians believe that even "good" actions fall short of God's standard due to sin, and salvation requires God's grace, not just human effort. The question depends on whether "goodness" is defined by human standards or God's standards.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity beliefs questions: Always use specific biblical references (e.g. Genesis 1, John 1:1-3, John 3:16). Explain different Christian interpretations (literal vs non-literal, Catholic vs Protestant). Use key terms precisely (omnipotent, Trinity, incarnation, atonement). For evaluation questions, consider both religious and secular viewpoints before reaching a justified conclusion.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often confuse the Trinity as three gods rather than one God in three persons. Wrong: "Christians believe in three gods." Correct: "Christians believe in one God who exists as three persons — Father, Son and Holy Spirit."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that original sin; the means of salvation including law, grace and spirit; atonement.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
