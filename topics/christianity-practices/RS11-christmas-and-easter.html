RS11: Christmas and Easter

All Boards

The celebrations of Christmas and Easter and their importance for Christians in Britain today

🎄 Christmas

Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ (the incarnation). It is one of the two most important Christian festivals, along with Easter.

  • Advent — the four weeks before Christmas; a time of preparation and reflection
  • Christmas Day (25 December) — celebrates Jesus' birth in Bethlehem (Luke 2:1-20)
  • Nativity scene — depicts Jesus in a manger with Mary, Joseph, shepherds and wise men
  • Midnight Mass — a special service on Christmas Eve, particularly important for Catholics
  • Christmas reminds Christians that God became human — the incarnation is at the heart of the faith
Exam Tip: Some Christians are concerned that Christmas has become too commercialised — the focus on presents and Santa Claus can overshadow the birth of Jesus. Churches work to keep the religious meaning central through nativity services, carol concerts and Advent reflections.

🐣 Easter

Easter is the most important Christian festival, celebrating the resurrection of Jesus from the dead.

  • Lent — 40 days of fasting and reflection before Easter, remembering Jesus' 40 days in the desert
  • Holy Week: Palm Sunday (Jesus enters Jerusalem), Maundy Thursday (Last Supper), Good Friday (crucifixion)
  • Good Friday — a solemn day remembering Jesus' death; churches may be bare and services are sombre
  • Easter Sunday — celebration of the resurrection; churches are decorated with flowers; the paschal candle is lit
  • Without the resurrection, Christianity has no foundation — it proves Jesus' divinity and victory over death

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain why Easter is the most important Christian festival.

Q2: Explain two ways Christians celebrate Christmas.

✅ Answers

  1. Easter is the most important festival because it celebrates the resurrection — the event on which the entire Christian faith depends. Without the resurrection, Jesus would be just a dead teacher, and there would be no salvation. As Paul wrote: "If Christ has not been raised, our preaching is useless and so is your faith" (1 Corinthians 15:14). The resurrection proves Jesus' divinity, demonstrates God's power over death, and gives Christians the hope of eternal life.
  2. 1) Many Christians attend a Midnight Mass or Christmas Day service, which includes nativity readings from Luke's Gospel, carols, and a celebration of Jesus' birth. 2) Christians set up nativity scenes in churches and homes, depicting the birth of Jesus in a manger with Mary, Joseph and the shepherds, to remind themselves and their families of the true meaning of Christmas amid commercialisation.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity practices questions: Name specific sacraments and explain their significance. Compare different Christian traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant). Use real examples (e.g. Lourdes, Iona, CAFOD, street pastors). For worship questions, distinguish between liturgical, non-liturgical and informal worship with specific examples.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think all Christians celebrate the Eucharist in the same way. Wrong: "All Christians believe the bread and wine literally become Jesus' body and blood." Correct: "Catholics believe in transubstantiation; Protestants have different views including consubstantiation and memorial symbolism."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that the celebrations of christmas and easter and their importance for christians in britain today.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.

Detailed Notes

Understanding Christmas and Easter in Christian Practice

Christmas and Easter is a key aspect of Christian practice that GCSE Religious Studies students must understand in depth. Christianity is the world’s largest religion with over 2 billion followers, and its practices shape the daily lives of believers. The AQA specification requires you to explain Christian practices, analyse their significance, and evaluate different Christian perspectives with reference to biblical and theological sources.

When writing about christmas and easter, always refer to specific biblical teachings, denominational differences (Catholic, Anglican, Orthodox, Protestant), and the lived experience of Christians today. Examiners reward answers that show understanding of diversity within Christianity — not all Christians interpret or practice their faith in the same way.

Christian practices are rooted in the life and teachings of Jesus Christ as recorded in the Bible. The New Testament, particularly the Gospels and the Acts of the Apostles, provides the primary sources for understanding how Christians are called to live. When discussing christmas and easter, connect the practice to its biblical foundation.

GCSE Example: Understanding Christmas and Easter in Christian Practice

A strong GCSE RS answer about christmas and easter would: explain the practice with reference to biblical teaching, describe how it is observed by different Christian denominations, analyse its significance for Christians, and evaluate different perspectives with justified reasoning.

Biblical and Theological Foundations of Christmas and Easter

Christian practices are grounded in the Bible and developed through church tradition. When discussing christmas and easter, identify the relevant biblical passages: the Old Testament provides the Jewish context that Christianity grew from; the Gospels record Jesus’ life and teachings; the Epistles (letters) of Paul and others explain theological significance; and the Acts of the Apostles describes the early church’s practices.

Key Christian beliefs that inform christmas and easter include: the nature of God (loving, just, omnipotent), the incarnation (God becoming human in Jesus), the atonement (Jesus’ death reconciling humanity with God), the resurrection (Jesus rising from the dead), and the Holy Spirit (God’s active presence in the world and in believers). These doctrines shape how Christians understand and practice their faith.

Different denominations interpret biblical teaching differently. Catholic Christians give authority to both Scripture and Tradition (the teaching authority of the Church). Protestant Christians emphasise sola scriptura (Scripture alone). Orthodox Christians emphasise the continuity of apostolic tradition. These differences affect how christmas and easter is understood and practiced.

GCSE Example: Biblical and Theological Foundations of Christmas and Easter

When explaining christmas and easter, reference specific Bible passages: for worship, cite John 4:24 (‘God is spirit, and his worshippers must worship in the Spirit and in truth’); for prayer, cite Matthew 6:9-13 (the Lord’s Prayer). Always explain what the passage means in context and how it influences Christian practice today.

The Impact of Christmas and Easter on Christians Today

Understanding christmas and easter in the modern world means considering how Christians practice their faith in contemporary Britain and globally. Christianity in the UK is diverse: the Church of England (Anglican) is the established church, but there are also Catholic, Methodist, Baptist, Pentecostal and Orthodox communities, each with distinct approaches to christmas and easter.

Christian practices like christmas and easter have both personal and communal dimensions. Personally, they shape individual spiritual life, ethical decision-making and relationship with God. Communally, they build church community, strengthen social bonds, and provide a witness to wider society. Understanding both dimensions is essential for GCSE.

When evaluating the significance of christmas and easter for Christians today, consider: why do some Christians find this practice more meaningful than others? How does the practice express Christian beliefs about God, Jesus and the Holy Spirit? What challenges do Christians face in maintaining this practice in contemporary secular society?

GCSE Example: The Impact of Christmas and Easter on Christians Today

A balanced evaluation of christmas and easter might argue: for many Christians, this practice is central to their faith because [reason with biblical reference]. However, other Christians may prioritise different aspects of their faith because [reason]. Ultimately, the significance of christmas and easter depends on [factor] because [justification].

Additional Practice Questions

Q: Explain the importance of christmas and easter for Christians, with reference to biblical teachings and denominational differences.

A: Christmas and Easter is important for Christians because [specific biblical teaching]. For example, [specific Bible passage] teaches that [specific principle]. Catholics approach christmas and easter by [specific Catholic practice], while Protestants [specific Protestant approach]. This denominational difference exists because [theological reason]. Despite differences, all Christians value christmas and easter because [shared reason].

Q: Evaluate the significance of christmas and easter for Christians living in Britain today.

A: Christmas and Easter remains significant for many Christians in Britain today because [reason with evidence]. However, in a increasingly secular society, some Christians find it challenging to maintain this practice because [specific challenge]. Different denominations respond differently: [specific examples]. Overall, while christmas and easter remains important for committed Christians, its expression may adapt to contemporary British life because [justified conclusion].
