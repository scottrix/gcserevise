RS11: Christmas and Easter

All Boards

The celebrations of Christmas and Easter and their importance for Christians in Britain today

🎄 Christmas

Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ (the incarnation). It is one of the two most important Christian festivals, along with Easter.

  • Advent — the four weeks before Christmas; a time of preparation and reflection
  • Christmas Day (25 December) — celebrates Jesus' birth in Bethlehem (Luke 2:1-20)
  • Nativity scene — depicts Jesus in a manger with Mary, Joseph, shepherds and wise men
  • Midnight Mass — a special service on Christmas Eve, particularly important for Catholics
  • Christmas reminds Christians that God became human — the incarnation is at the heart of the faith
Exam Tip: Some Christians are concerned that Christmas has become too commercialised — the focus on presents and Santa Claus can overshadow the birth of Jesus. Churches work to keep the religious meaning central through nativity services, carol concerts and Advent reflections.

🐣 Easter

Easter is the most important Christian festival, celebrating the resurrection of Jesus from the dead.

  • Lent — 40 days of fasting and reflection before Easter, remembering Jesus' 40 days in the desert
  • Holy Week: Palm Sunday (Jesus enters Jerusalem), Maundy Thursday (Last Supper), Good Friday (crucifixion)
  • Good Friday — a solemn day remembering Jesus' death; churches may be bare and services are sombre
  • Easter Sunday — celebration of the resurrection; churches are decorated with flowers; the paschal candle is lit
  • Without the resurrection, Christianity has no foundation — it proves Jesus' divinity and victory over death

📊 Christmas vs Easter

FeatureChristmasEaster
What it celebratesJesus' birth (incarnation)Jesus' resurrection

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain why Easter is the most important Christian festival.

Q2: Explain two ways Christians celebrate Christmas.

✅ Answers

  1. Easter is the most important festival because it celebrates the resurrection — the event on which the entire Christian faith depends. Without the resurrection, Jesus would be just a dead teacher, and there would be no salvation. As Paul wrote: "If Christ has not been raised, our preaching is useless and so is your faith" (1 Corinthians 15:14). The resurrection proves Jesus' divinity, demonstrates God's power over death, and gives Christians the hope of eternal life.
  2. 1) Many Christians attend a Midnight Mass or Christmas Day service, which includes nativity readings from Luke's Gospel, carols, and a celebration of Jesus' birth. 2) Christians set up nativity scenes in churches and homes, depicting the birth of Jesus in a manger with Mary, Joseph and the shepherds, to remind themselves and their families of the true meaning of Christmas amid commercialisation.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity practices questions: Name specific sacraments and explain their significance. Compare different Christian traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant). Use real examples (e.g. Lourdes, Iona, CAFOD, street pastors). For worship questions, distinguish between liturgical, non-liturgical and informal worship with specific examples.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think all Christians celebrate the Eucharist in the same way. Wrong: "All Christians believe the bread and wine literally become Jesus' body and blood." Correct: "Catholics believe in transubstantiation; Protestants have different views including consubstantiation and memorial symbolism."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that the celebrations of christmas and easter and their importance for christians in britain today.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
