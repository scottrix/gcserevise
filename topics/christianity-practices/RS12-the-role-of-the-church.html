RS12: The Role of the Church

All Boards

The Church in the local community; food banks and street pastors; mission and evangelism

✝️ The Role of the Church in the Local Community

The Church is not just a building — it is the community of Christians working together to live out Jesus' teaching. The local church plays a vital role in serving the community.

  • Pastoral care — visiting the sick, supporting the bereaved, counselling those in need
  • Food banks — many churches run or support food banks to help those in poverty
  • Street pastors — trained volunteers who go out at night to help vulnerable people on the streets
  • Parent and toddler groups — providing community support for young families
  • Youth work — youth clubs, holiday clubs and mentoring schemes for young people
  • Debt advice and homelessness support — Christians Against Poverty (CAP) and similar organisations
Key Fact: Jesus taught "Love your neighbour as yourself" (Mark 12:31) and the Parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25-37) shows that Christians must help anyone in need, regardless of who they are. The local church puts this teaching into practice.

🍞 Food Banks and Street Pastors

Food banks provide emergency food to people in crisis. The Trussell Trust runs over 1,200 food banks in the UK, many based in churches. They provide three days' worth of nutritionally balanced food to people referred by professionals.

Street pastors are trained church volunteers who patrol city streets at night, helping people who are vulnerable — those who are drunk, lost, distressed or at risk. They offer practical help, a listening ear and a safe presence. Street pastors operate in over 100 towns and cities across the UK.

📢 Mission and Evangelism

  • Mission — sharing God's love through action and service (not just words)
  • Evangelism — sharing the Christian message to encourage people to become Christians
  • Jesus' Great Commission: "Go and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:19)
  • Some churches focus on social action (food banks, homeless shelters) as their primary mission
  • Others focus on proclamation (Alpha Course, street preaching, sharing the Gospel)
  • Most churches do both — serving the community AND sharing their faith

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain two ways the local church serves the community.

Q2: "The Church should focus on worship, not social action." Evaluate this statement.

✅ Answers

  1. 1) Food banks — many churches run food banks that provide emergency food supplies to people in poverty. The Trussell Trust, a Christian charity, runs over 1,200 food banks. This puts Jesus' teaching to "love your neighbour" into practical action. 2) Street pastors — trained church volunteers go out at night to help vulnerable people on the streets, offering practical help, listening and a safe presence. They demonstrate Christian care for the marginalised.
  2. Some Christians argue the Church's primary role is worship and spiritual guidance — social action is the government's job. However, Jesus himself healed the sick, fed the hungry and cared for the outcast. The Parable of the Good Samaritan teaches that loving your neighbour requires practical action. Worship and social action are not alternatives — worship inspires service, and service demonstrates worship. Churches that serve their communities often find this is the most effective form of evangelism.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity practices questions: Name specific sacraments and explain their significance. Compare different Christian traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant). Use real examples (e.g. Lourdes, Iona, CAFOD, street pastors). For worship questions, distinguish between liturgical, non-liturgical and informal worship with specific examples.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think all Christians celebrate the Eucharist in the same way. Wrong: "All Christians believe the bread and wine literally become Jesus' body and blood." Correct: "Catholics believe in transubstantiation; Protestants have different views including consubstantiation and memorial symbolism."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that the church in the local community; food banks and street pastors; mission and evangelism.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
