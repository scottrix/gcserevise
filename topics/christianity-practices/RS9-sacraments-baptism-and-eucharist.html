RS9: Sacraments: Baptism and Eucharist

All Boards

The meaning of sacrament; infant and believers' baptism; Holy Communion and its significance

✝️ Sacraments: Baptism and Eucharist

Key Fact: A sacrament is an outward sign of an inward grace — a visible ceremony that Christians believe channels God's grace (favour) to the believer. Most Christians recognise two sacraments (Baptism and Eucharist) but Catholics and Orthodox recognise seven.

💧 Baptism

Baptism is the sacrament of initiation into the Christian faith. It involves water and the words "I baptise you in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."

FeatureInfant BaptismBelievers' Baptism
WhoBabies, on behalf of parents' faithAdults/young people who choose it themselves
DenominationsCatholic, Anglican, Methodist, OrthodoxBaptist, Pentecostal, some evangelical
MethodSprinkling with water or pouringFull immersion in a pool or river
GodparentsYes — make promises on behalf of the childNo — the person makes their own promises
MeaningCleansing from original sin; welcome into the ChurchPublic declaration of personal faith; following Jesus' example

🍞 The Eucharist (Holy Communion)

The Eucharist commemorates the Last Supper, when Jesus shared bread and wine with his disciples before his crucifixion. "Do this in remembrance of me" (1 Corinthians 11:24-25).

ViewDenominationBelief about Bread and Wine
TransubstantiationCatholicThe bread and wine literally become the body and blood of Christ (though they still look and taste like bread and wine)
ConsubstantiationLutheranChrist's body and blood are present alongside the bread and wine
Memorial/SymbolicBaptist, ZwingliThe bread and wine are symbols that remind believers of Christ's sacrifice
Spiritual presenceMethodist, ReformedChrist is spiritually present but the elements remain bread and wine
Exam Tip: The Eucharist is the most important act of worship for Catholics — they believe they receive Christ himself. For Protestants, it is a memorial that strengthens faith and unity. Be ready to explain both views with specific theological terms.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain two different Christian views about the Eucharist.

Q2: Explain why baptism is important for Christians.

✅ Answers

  1. 1) Catholics believe in transubstantiation — the bread and wine literally become the body and blood of Christ during the Mass, even though they still appear as bread and wine. This is based on Jesus' words "This is my body" at the Last Supper. 2) Baptists and many Protestants see the Eucharist as a memorial — the bread and wine are symbols that remind believers of Christ's sacrifice on the cross. They follow Jesus' instruction to "do this in remembrance of me" but do not believe the elements change.
  2. Baptism is important because: it follows Jesus' own baptism by John (Mark 1:9-11); Jesus commanded his disciples to baptise (Matthew 28:19); it symbolises the washing away of sin and new life in Christ; it initiates the person into the Christian community; and Catholics believe it removes original sin. Different denominations practise baptism differently but all see it as a key Christian rite.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Christianity practices questions: Name specific sacraments and explain their significance. Compare different Christian traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant). Use real examples (e.g. Lourdes, Iona, CAFOD, street pastors). For worship questions, distinguish between liturgical, non-liturgical and informal worship with specific examples.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think all Christians celebrate the Eucharist in the same way. Wrong: "All Christians believe the bread and wine literally become Jesus' body and blood." Correct: "Catholics believe in transubstantiation; Protestants have different views including consubstantiation and memorial symbolism."

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
