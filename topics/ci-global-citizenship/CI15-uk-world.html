The UK & the World

The UK's role in international relations: diplomacy, trade, military alliances, soft power, and how globalisation affects UK citizens.

Key Fact: The UK is a permanent member of the UN Security Council with veto power, giving it significant influence over international responses to conflicts and crises.
Key Fact: The UK is a member of NATO (military alliance), the G7 (major economies), the Commonwealth (56 nations), and the Council of Europe (human rights).
Key Fact: Globalisation means decisions made in one country affect others — economic crises, climate change, pandemics, and migration all cross borders. No nation can solve these alone.
Key Fact: The UK government's foreign policy is decided by the Prime Minister and Foreign Secretary, with limited parliamentary control. However, Parliament must approve military action.
Key Fact: The UK spends approximately 0.5% of GNI on international aid (reduced from the 0.7% target in 2021). This provides humanitarian assistance and supports development in poorer countries.
Key Fact: Soft power is the ability to influence through culture, values, and persuasion rather than military force. The UK has significant soft power through the English language, BBC, British Council, and world-leading universities.
Key Fact: Hard power uses military force or economic sanctions. The UK exercises hard power through its armed forces and through sanctions regimes.
Key Fact: Trade agreements affect citizens' daily lives through prices, job opportunities, and product availability. Post-Brexit, the UK negotiates its own trade deals independently of the EU.
Key Fact: The UK's colonial history continues to shape its international relationships — the Commonwealth, migration patterns, and ongoing debates about reparations and historical injustice.
Key Fact: The British Council promotes UK culture, education, and English language teaching worldwide, operating in over 100 countries.
Key Fact: International treaties commit the UK to certain standards: the European Convention on Human Rights, the Paris Climate Agreement, and various UN conventions.
Key Fact: The UK provides humanitarian aid and disaster relief through FCDO (Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office) funding and military logistics capabilities.

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What role does the UK play in the United Nations?

Q: Explain the difference between hard power and soft power.

Q: How does globalisation affect UK citizens?

✅ Answers

  1. The UK is a permanent member of the UN Security Council with veto power over resolutions. It contributes to peacekeeping operations, provides significant funding (around 5% of the UN budget), and uses its position to influence international responses to conflicts and humanitarian crises.
  2. Hard power uses military force or economic sanctions to compel other nations to act in a certain way. Soft power uses cultural influence, diplomacy, and shared values to persuade. The UK exercises hard power through its military and soft power through the BBC, British Council, and English language.
  3. Globalisation affects UK citizens through: trade (imported goods and export jobs), migration (diverse communities but political debate), shared environmental challenges like climate change, cultural exchange (food, music, media), and economic interconnection (financial crises spreading across borders).

🎯 Exam Tips

  • When discussing the UK's global role, use specific organisations (UN Security Council, NATO, Commonwealth)
  • Distinguish between hard and soft power with concrete examples
  • Globalisation questions require you to consider both benefits and drawbacks

📝 Exam Technique

Citizenship Studies Exam Tips:
Use precise legal and political terminology. Always support arguments with specific examples (e.g. named laws, cases, or organisations). For evaluation questions, present both sides before reaching a reasoned conclusion. Active citizenship questions require you to demonstrate practical understanding, not just theoretical knowledge.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ The UK has no influence in international affairs since Brexit. ✓ The UK remains a permanent UN Security Council member, a NATO member, and a major economy. While Brexit reduced EU influence, the UK still has significant global influence through soft power and military strength.

✗ Globalisation only affects the economy. ✓ Globalisation affects culture, politics, the environment, migration, technology, and health as well as the economy.

✗ Parliament decides UK foreign policy. ✓ The government (Prime Minister and Foreign Secretary) decides foreign policy. Parliament has limited powers but must approve military action and can debate issues.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the UK's role as a global citizen. [12 marks]

The UK plays a significant role as a global citizen through its membership of international organisations and contributions to international development. As a permanent member of the UN Security Council, the UK influences responses to global conflicts. It is a leading NATO member, committing to collective defence. The UK's aid budget, though reduced from 0.7% to 0.5% of GNI, still provides vital humanitarian assistance. The UK also exercises substantial soft power. The English language, BBC World Service, British Council, and world-leading universities give the UK cultural influence disproportionate to its size. This supports diplomatic relationships and trade. However, the UK's global role has been questioned. The aid reduction broke a manifesto commitment and drew criticism from aid organisations. The Iraq War damaged the UK's international reputation. Post-Brexit, the UK has had to renegotiate trade relationships and its European influence has diminished. Overall, the UK remains a significant global player, but its credibility depends on maintaining commitments to international law, aid, and cooperation.
