❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in French about food and drink culture.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on food and drink culture.

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary, with correct adjective agreement, articles and verb conjugation. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph with: introduction (à mon avis...), opinion with reason (je pense que... parce que...), a different viewpoint (cependant...), and a conclusion (en conclusion...). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Je pense que [topic] est important(e) parce que..." — ensure adjective agreement (important vs importante depending on noun gender), correct verb form, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (je pense que... parce que...)
  • Check adjective agreement (gender and number) before finishing
  • Use complex structures: negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais), pronouns (le, la, les), conjunctions (cependant, donc, en plus)
  • Include cultural references to French-speaking countries
  • For writing: plan your response, check verb conjugations, re-read for errors

📝 Exam Technique

French Exam Tips:
For customs and festivals questions: Use the perfect tense to describe what happened at a festival. Include cultural knowledge about French traditions (not just British ones). Use the imperfect to describe what used to happen. Include dates and time expressions. Show awareness of la Francophonie beyond France.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often use the present tense instead of the perfect for describing past festivals. Wrong: Pour le 14 juillet, on danse dans les rues (present — implies habit). Correct: Pour le 14 juillet, on a dansé dans les rues (perfect — specific past event). Students also confuse quand (when) with quant (how much/many).

La Gastronomie Française et l'UNESCO

In 2010, le repas gastronomique des Français was added to UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list. This recognises the ritual importance of French meals: choosing products, pairing food with wine, setting the table, and the specific sequence of courses. A traditional French meal follows a strict order: l'apéritif (pre-meal drinks), l'entrée (starter), le plat principal (main course), le fromage (cheese course), le dessert, and le digestif (post-meal drink).

French cuisine is regional. In la Provence, la bouillabaisse (fish stew) and la ratatouille (vegetable stew) are iconic. In l'Alsace, la choucroute (sauerkraut with sausages) reflects Germanic influence. In la Bourgogne, le bœuf bourguignon (beef stew in red wine) is a classic. In le Sud-Ouest, le cassoulet (bean and meat stew) and le foie gras are specialities. Knowing regional dishes shows cultural depth.

For the exam, discuss food preferences using the conditional: Je voudrais goûter la bouillabaisse parce que j'adore les fruits de mer (I would like to try bouillabaisse because I love seafood). Express opinions about French cuisine: À mon avis, la gastronomie française est la meilleure du monde, cependant c'est vrai qu'elle peut être riche et calorique.

Exemple

Le repas gastronomique français est classé par l'UNESCO. Il suit un ordre précis: l'apéritif, l'entrée, le plat principal, le fromage et le dessert. C'est un art de vivre! — The French gastronomic meal is classified by UNESCO. It follows a precise order: the apéritif, the starter, the main course, the cheese and the dessert. It's an art of living!

Le Fromage, le Pain et le Vin

Three pillars of French food culture: le fromage, le pain, and le vin. France has over 1,000 varieties of cheese, from le camembert (Normandie) to le roquefort (Aveyron) to le comté (Franche-Comté). General de Gaulle famously said: « Comment voulez-vous gouverner un pays qui a 246 variétés de fromage? » (How can you govern a country that has 246 varieties of cheese?).

Le pain — specifically la baguette — is so important that the artisanal baguette was added to UNESCO's heritage list in 2022. A traditional baguette must be made on-site with only four ingredients: flour, water, salt, and yeast. The average French person eats half a baguette per day! La croissant, despite its Austrian origins, is also a French icon. Le vin is integral to French meals — France is the world's second-largest wine producer, with famous regions like Bordeaux, Bourgogne, Champagne, and la Vallée du Rhône.

For Higher tier, discuss food and identity: Le fromage et le pain sont des symboles de l'identité française. Cependant, les habitudes alimentaires changent — les jeunes mangent moins de fromage et plus de fast-food.

Exemple

La baguette artisanale est classée par l'UNESCO depuis 2022. Elle ne contient que quatre ingrédients: la farine, l'eau, le sel et la levure. Un Français mange une demi-baguette par jour en moyenne! — The artisanal baguette has been classified by UNESCO since 2022. It contains only four ingredients: flour, water, salt and yeast. A French person eats half a baguette per day on average!

La Culture Culinaires Francophones

Francophone food culture extends well beyond France. In Sénégal, le thiéboudienne (rice with fish, tomatoes and vegetables) is the national dish, traditionally served in a large communal bowl. Le yassa (chicken or fish marinated in lemon and onions) and le mafé (peanut stew) are beloved across West Africa. Eating from a shared plate symbolises community and la teranga.

In Québec, la poutine (fries, cheese curds, gravy) is iconic, though its origins were humble. Le pâté chinois (shepherd's pie) and la tourtière (meat pie) reflect French-Canadian heritage. Le sirop d'érable is produced from March to April in les érablières — visiting a cabane à sucre is a springtime tradition where families enjoy la tire sur la neige (hot maple syrup poured on snow). In Belgium, les moules-frites (mussels and chips) is the national dish, and les gaufres (waffles) come in two types: de Bruxelles (light and crispy) and de Liège (dense and sweet).

Compare culinary traditions: Tandis qu'en France, le fromage est le symbole culinaire, au Sénégal, c'est le thiéboudienne, au Québec, c'est la poutine, et en Belgique, ce sont les moules-frites.

Exemple

Au Sénégal, on mange le thiéboudienne dans un grand bol communautaire. Au Québec, on visite le cabane à sucre pour la tire sur la neige. En Belgique, les moules-frites sont le plat national. — In Sénégal, they eat thiéboudienne from a large communal bowl. In Québec, they visit the sugar shack for maple taffy on snow. In Belgium, moules-frites is the national dish.
