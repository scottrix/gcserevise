Dance Structure & Form

Binary, ternary, rondo, narrative, abstract and thematic structures

Key Fact: Dance structure is how a piece is organised — the overall shape and order of sections
Key Fact: Binary form has two contrasting sections: A then B (e.g. fast then slow)
Key Fact: Ternary form has three sections: ABA — the opening section returns after a contrast
Key Fact: Rondo form alternates a main theme with different episodes: ABACADA
Key Fact: Narrative structure tells a story with a beginning, middle and end in chronological order
Key Fact: Abstract structure is organised around movement ideas without a storyline
Key Fact: Thematic structure develops and varies one or more movement themes throughout
Key Fact: The choice of structure affects how the audience experiences and understands the dance
Key Fact: Choreographers choose a structure that best supports their intention
Key Fact: Some professional works combine more than one structural form
Key Fact: In AQA 8236 NEA, you must identify and justify your chosen structure
Key Fact: Structure is often compared to musical form — many dance structures borrow from music

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Dance Structure & Form, you must know:

  • Dance structure: The overall organisation and arrangement of sections within a dance piece
  • Binary form: A two-part structure with contrasting sections, A and B
  • Ternary form: A three-part structure, ABA, where the first section returns after a contrasting middle
  • Rondo form: A structure with a recurring main theme alternating with different episodes: ABACADA
  • Narrative structure: A structure that tells a story following a chronological sequence of events
  • Abstract structure: A structure organised around movement ideas and qualities rather than a storyline

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What is the difference between binary and ternary form?

Q: Describe the structure of a rondo and give its pattern.

Q: How does an abstract structure differ from a narrative structure?

Q: Why might a choreographer choose ternary form for their dance?

Q: Explain what a thematic structure is and how it works.

✅ Answers

  1. Binary form has two sections (A B), while ternary form has three (A B A) where the opening section returns after the contrast.
  2. A rondo has a main theme that keeps returning, alternating with different contrasting episodes, following the pattern ABACADA.
  3. A narrative structure tells a story with characters and events in order; an abstract structure is organised around movement ideas and qualities without a storyline.
  4. Ternary form gives a sense of balance and resolution because the opening section returns, providing familiarity. The middle section offers contrast before the return creates a satisfying conclusion.
  5. A thematic structure is built around one or more movement themes that are developed and varied throughout the piece. The themes reappear in different forms, creating unity while maintaining interest.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use the correct letter patterns (AB, ABA, ABACADA) when describing structures
  • Always explain why a choreographer chose that structure — link it to intention
  • In NEA written tasks, name your structure and justify how it supports your idea
  • For professional works, identify the structure and explain how it shapes the audience experience
  • Remember that structure and form are used interchangeably in AQA 8236

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Binary form means two dancers performing together. ✓ Binary form is a two-part structure with contrasting sections labelled A and B — it is about the organisation of the piece, not the number of dancers.

✗ Ternary form is ABC — three different sections. ✓ Ternary form is ABA — the A section returns after the B contrast, creating a three-part structure with repetition.

✗ Abstract structure means the dance has no structure. ✓ Abstract structure is still carefully organised — it is structured around movement ideas and qualities rather than a narrative storyline.

✗ Rondo form only has two sections that alternate. ✓ Rondo form has a recurring main theme (A) alternating with different episodes (B, C, D, etc.), giving the pattern ABACADA.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Compare binary form and rondo form, explaining how each structures a dance and the effect it creates for the audience. [6 marks]

Binary form is a two-part structure with contrasting sections, A and B. Section A establishes one mood or movement style, then section B provides contrast — for example, A might be slow and sustained while B is fast and dynamic. This creates a clear sense of contrast and can represent opposing ideas or emotions. The audience experiences a straightforward shift between two worlds. Rondo form has a recurring main theme (A) that alternates with different episodes (B, C, D), giving the pattern ABACADA. Each return of the A section provides familiarity and a sense of home, while each new episode offers fresh contrast. This creates a journey-like experience where the audience can anchor themselves in the returning theme while being taken to new places. Rondo form is more complex than binary and suits dances that need variety while maintaining a strong central idea. Binary is simpler and more direct, ideal for short pieces or clear contrasts. Both structures help the audience follow and engage with the choreography, but rondo provides more variety while binary provides sharper contrast.
