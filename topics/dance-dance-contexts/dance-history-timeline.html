Dance History & Timeline

Key developments from ballet to contemporary: tracing the evolution of dance through major historical periods and influential figures

Key Fact: Ballet originated in the courts of Renaissance Italy and France in the 15th and 16th centuries
Key Fact: King Louis XIV of France established the Académie Royale de Danse in 1661, professionalising ballet
Key Fact: Romantic ballet emerged in the 1830s with ethereal themes and pointe work, such as La Sylphide
Key Fact: Classical ballet peaked in late 19th-century Russia with works like Swan Lake and The Sleeping Beauty
Key Fact: Isadora Duncan rebelled against ballet's rigidity in the early 1900s, pioneering free expressive movement
Key Fact: Martha Graham developed a technique based on contraction and release, becoming a pillar of modern dance
Key Fact: Merce Cunningham introduced chance procedures and separated dance from music in the mid-20th century
Key Fact: Contemporary dance blends elements of ballet, modern and postmodern dance from the 1980s onwards
Key Fact: Hip-hop dance emerged from African American and Latino communities in the Bronx during the 1970s
Key Fact: World dance traditions such as Bharatanatyam, Kathak and Flamenco have influenced contemporary choreography
Key Fact: The UK contemporary dance scene flourished with choreographers like Christopher Bruce and Matthew Bourne
Key Fact: Understanding dance history helps contextualise professional works studied in AQA 8236

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Dance History & Timeline, you must know:

  • Renaissance ballet: Early court dance from 15th-century Italy and France that laid the foundations for classical ballet
  • Romantic ballet: 19th-century ballet style characterised by ethereal themes, pointe work and supernatural characters
  • Modern dance: Early 20th-century movement rejecting ballet's formality in favour of expressive, grounded movement
  • Contraction and release: Martha Graham's core technique based on the cycle of muscular tension and relaxation in the torso
  • Contemporary dance: A blend of ballet, modern and postmodern techniques that emerged in the late 20th century
  • Chance choreography: Merce Cunningham's method of using random procedures to determine movement order and structure

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Name two key developments in ballet during the Renaissance period.

Q: How did Isadora Duncan challenge the traditions of classical ballet?

Q: What was significant about Merce Cunningham's approach to choreography?

Q: Describe how hip-hop dance originated and why it matters in dance history.

Q: Why is understanding dance history important for AQA 8236?

✅ Answers

  1. Ballet originated in the Italian and French courts as court entertainment, and King Louis XIV established the Académie Royale de Danse in 1661, which professionalised ballet training and performance.
  2. Duncan rejected ballet's rigid technique, corsets and pointe shoes, instead performing barefoot in loose tunics and using natural, expressive movement inspired by ancient Greek art and nature.
  3. Cunningham used chance procedures such as coin tosses to determine movement order, and he separated dance from music so each could exist independently, breaking the traditional relationship between the two.
  4. Hip-hop dance emerged in the 1970s in the Bronx, New York, among African American and Latino communities as part of hip-hop culture. It introduced breaking, locking and popping, expanding dance beyond studio-based traditions.
  5. Dance history provides context for the professional works studied in the course, helping students understand choreographic intentions, stylistic influences and how dance has evolved, which supports analysis and appreciation.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • When discussing dance history, always link the period or figure to a specific professional work you have studied
  • Use precise dates and names — examiners reward accurate historical knowledge
  • Explain how historical developments influenced choreographic style, not just what happened
  • Compare at least two different periods or choreographers when asked about dance evolution
  • Remember that dance history questions often require you to analyse, not just describe

📝 Exam Technique

GCSE Dance Exam Tips:
Use precise classical terminology in all answers. When analysing sources, always consider provenance (who created it, when, and why). For literary questions, reference specific episodes and book numbers from set texts. For thematic questions, compare Greek and Roman approaches where relevant. For 'how far' questions, present a balanced argument with a clear judgement. AQA 8236 rewards answers that demonstrate understanding of the classical world through use of specific evidence from both literary and visual/material sources.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Modern dance and contemporary dance are the same thing. ✓ Modern dance refers to the early 20th-century movement (Graham, Cunningham); contemporary dance is a later blend of ballet, modern and postmodern styles.

✗ Ballet began in Russia. ✓ Ballet originated in the courts of Renaissance Italy and France; it later flourished in Russia during the classical period.

✗ Isadora Duncan founded contemporary dance. ✓ Duncan pioneered free expressive movement in the early 1900s, which was part of the modern dance movement; contemporary dance developed decades later.

✗ Dance history is just a list of dates and names. ✓ Dance history is about understanding how social, cultural and artistic changes shaped the development of dance styles and techniques.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Explain how dance has evolved from classical ballet to contemporary dance, referring to key figures and developments. [6 marks]

Dance has evolved significantly from classical ballet to contemporary dance through a series of revolutionary changes. Classical ballet, formalised in the courts of Renaissance France and perfected in 19th-century Russia with works like Swan Lake, emphasised turned-out legs, pointe work and rigid technique. In the early 1900s, Isadora Duncan challenged ballet's constraints by performing barefoot with free, expressive movement, pioneering modern dance. Martha Graham further developed this with her contraction-and-release technique, creating a grounded, emotionally powerful style. Merce Cunningham then broke conventions by using chance procedures and separating dance from music. Contemporary dance, emerging in the late 20th century, synthesises elements from ballet, modern and postmodern dance, allowing choreographers like Christopher Bruce to combine technical virtuosity with expressive freedom. Each stage built upon and reacted against what came before, demonstrating dance's continual evolution.
