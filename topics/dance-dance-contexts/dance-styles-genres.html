Ballet, contemporary, jazz, hip-hop, street and world dance: identifying key features, techniques and characteristics of major dance styles

Dance Styles & Genres

Ballet, contemporary, jazz, hip-hop, street and world dance: identifying key features, techniques and characteristics of major dance styles

Key Fact: Ballet is characterised by turnout, pointe work, elevation and precise classical technique
Key Fact: Contemporary dance blends ballet and modern techniques with an emphasis on floor work and weight
Key Fact: Jazz dance combines African rhythms, theatrical performance and syncopated, energetic movement
Key Fact: Hip-hop dance includes breaking, locking, popping and krumping, rooted in urban street culture
Key Fact: Street dance encompasses a range of informal dance styles developed outside dance studios
Key Fact: World dance includes traditional forms such as Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Flamenco and African dance
Key Fact: Each style has distinct technique, posture, dynamics and use of music that define its identity
Key Fact: Choreographers often blend styles to create fusion work, such as Matthew Bourne's contemporary ballet
Key Fact: Understanding style features is essential for accurately describing and analysing professional works
Key Fact: AQA 8236 requires students to recognise and articulate the stylistic features of prescribed works
Key Fact: Dance styles evolve over time as choreographers innovate and cross boundaries between genres
Key Fact: Cultural context shapes each style — ballet reflects courtly formality while hip-hop reflects street culture

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Dance Styles & Genres, you must know:

  • Turnout: The outward rotation of the legs from the hip socket, fundamental to ballet technique
  • Syncopation: A rhythmic pattern where emphasis falls on normally weak beats, common in jazz dance
  • Breaking: An acrobatic hip-hop dance style involving floor work, spins and power moves such as headspins
  • Release technique: A contemporary dance approach using momentum and gravity to move efficiently through space
  • Kathak: A classical Indian dance form known for intricate footwork, spins and storytelling gestures
  • Fusion: The blending of two or more dance styles to create a new hybrid form of movement

✅ Answers

  1. Ballet features turnout, pointe work and lifted posture with an emphasis on defying gravity. Contemporary dance uses release technique, floor work and weight into the ground, allowing a more grounded, fluid quality.
  2. Hip-hop dance encompasses breaking (floor work and spins), locking (sharp freezes between fluid movement), popping (quick muscle contractions) and krumping (intense, expressive chest-pumping movements).
  3. Jazz dance blends African rhythmic traditions with American theatrical performance, using syncopation, isolations, high energy and kicks. It has roots in African American social dance and Broadway musical theatre.
  4. Identifying style features allows students to accurately describe and analyse prescribed professional works, demonstrating understanding of how choreographers use style to communicate intention and create meaning.
  5. Matthew Bourne's Swan Lake blends classical ballet technique with contemporary and theatrical storytelling, replacing the traditional female corps with male dancers. This fusion creates a fresh interpretation that challenges conventions while retaining ballet's technical demands.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always name the specific style and describe its technical features when analysing a professional work
  • Use style-specific vocabulary such as 'turnout', 'release', 'syncopation' rather than generic terms
  • When comparing styles, discuss at least two contrasting features such as posture, dynamics or use of space
  • Link style features directly to the choreographer's intention — do not just list characteristics
  • Remember that many professional works blend styles; be precise about which elements come from which tradition

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Contemporary dance is just ballet done slowly. ✓ Contemporary dance is a distinct style using release technique, floor work and weight; it is fundamentally different from ballet in posture, use of gravity and movement quality.

✗ Street dance and hip-hop are the same style. ✓ Hip-hop is a specific culture that includes breaking, locking and popping; street dance is a broader term covering informal urban dance styles, some of which overlap with hip-hop.

✗ World dance is not relevant to AQA 8236. ✓ World dance styles influence many contemporary choreographers and may feature in prescribed works; understanding them supports analysis and comparison.

✗ Jazz dance is just dancing to jazz music. ✓ Jazz dance is a technical style with its own vocabulary of isolations, syncopation, kicks and contractions; it can be performed to many genres of music, not just jazz.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Compare the key features of ballet and contemporary dance, explaining how each style communicates meaning differently. [6 marks]

Ballet and contemporary dance differ fundamentally in technique, use of the body and how they communicate meaning. Ballet uses turnout, pointe work and a lifted, upright posture to create an impression of weightlessness and aspiration. Meaning is conveyed through codified gestures, mime and formal choreographic structures such as pas de deux. In contrast, contemporary dance uses release technique, floor work and shifts of weight, creating a grounded, fluid quality. The body moves into and out of the floor, suggesting a connection to gravity and human experience. Contemporary choreographers communicate meaning through more open, expressive movement vocabulary that can directly represent emotions or ideas. For example, in Christopher Bruce's Swan Song, contemporary technique conveys vulnerability and oppression through curled, protective body shapes, whereas ballet's Swan Lake uses classical technique to convey ethereal beauty. Both styles communicate powerfully, but ballet does so through formal elegance while contemporary uses raw physicality and weight.
