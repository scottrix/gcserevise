Physical Skills in Dance

Flexibility, strength, stamina, coordination and balance for dance performance

Key Fact: Physical skills are the foundation of all dance performance and underpin technical and expressive work
Key Fact: Flexibility is the range of movement at a joint and allows dancers to achieve extended lines and shapes
Key Fact: Strength is the ability of muscles to exert force and is needed for lifts, jumps and controlled movements
Key Fact: Stamina (cardiovascular endurance) enables dancers to sustain energy throughout an entire performance
Key Fact: Coordination is the ability to use different body parts together smoothly and accurately
Key Fact: Balance is the ability to maintain a stable position, whether static or moving through space
Key Fact: A dancer must develop all five physical skills together as they are interdependent
Key Fact: Flexibility reduces the risk of injury and allows greater range of movement in choreography
Key Fact: Muscular strength supports control in slow movements and power in explosive actions
Key Fact: Stamina ensures the quality of movement does not deteriorate as the performance progresses
Key Fact: Coordination between limbs, torso and head is essential for performing complex movement phrases
Key Fact: GCSE Dance assessors look for evidence of well-developed physical skills in performance

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Physical Skills in Dance, you must know:

  • Flexibility: The range of movement available at a joint, allowing extended lines and shapes in dance
  • Strength: The ability of a muscle or muscle group to exert force against resistance
  • Stamina: Cardiovascular endurance that allows a dancer to maintain energy over a full performance
  • Coordination: The ability to use different parts of the body together smoothly and accurately
  • Balance: The ability to maintain a stable position, either still or while moving
  • Muscular endurance: The ability of muscles to repeat contractions over time without fatigue

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Name the five physical skills a dancer needs for performance.

Q: Why is flexibility important for a dancer?

Q: How does stamina affect dance performance quality?

Q: Explain the relationship between strength and balance in dance.

Q: Why must physical skills be developed together rather than in isolation?

✅ Answers

  1. Flexibility, strength, stamina, coordination and balance.
  2. Flexibility increases the range of movement at joints, allowing dancers to create extended lines, achieve required shapes and reduce the risk of injury.
  3. Stamina allows the dancer to maintain energy, technique and expression throughout the entire performance without deterioration.
  4. Core and leg strength support the ability to hold stable positions and control weight shifts, which are essential for maintaining balance in static and moving actions.
  5. Physical skills are interdependent — for example, coordination relies on balance, and balance relies on strength. Developing only one skill limits overall performance quality.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use the terms flexibility, strength, stamina, coordination and balance precisely — do not confuse them in written answers
  • When describing a performer's physical skills, always link the skill to the effect on the audience or the quality of the dance
  • Remember that stamina is about sustaining the whole performance, not just being fit in general
  • In 6-mark questions, aim to cover at least three physical skills with clear explanations and dance examples
  • Physical skills are often assessed alongside technical and expressive skills — show how they connect

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Flexibility just means being able to do the splits. ✓ Flexibility refers to the range of movement at any joint — it includes shoulders, spine, hips and ankles, not just the legs.

✗ Stamina and strength are the same thing. ✓ Stamina is cardiovascular endurance for sustaining energy over time; strength is the force muscles can exert at one time.

✗ Coordination only means using arms and legs together. ✓ Coordination involves integrating all body parts — limbs, torso and head — as well as timing movements with music and other dancers.

✗ Balance is only about standing still on one leg. ✓ Balance applies to both static holds and dynamic movement, including control during turns, leaps and weight shifts.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Explain how two physical skills contribute to a successful dance performance. Give dance examples to support your answer. [6 marks]

Flexibility and strength are two physical skills that are essential for a successful dance performance. Flexibility allows a dancer to achieve a wide range of movement at the joints, creating extended lines and dynamic shapes. For example, a dancer with good hip and leg flexibility can perform high développés and grand battements, making the performance visually impressive and enabling them to fulfil the choreographic intention. Strength enables a dancer to exert force through the muscles, supporting powerful jumps and controlled landings. For instance, in a performance involving repeated sautés and lifts, leg and core strength allow the dancer to push off the floor with power and land safely with control, maintaining the quality of the movement throughout. Together, these skills ensure the dancer can perform the choreography with accuracy, safety and expressive range.
