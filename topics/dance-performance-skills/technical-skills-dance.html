Technical Dance Skills

Posture, alignment, control, balance, coordination and spatial awareness

Key Fact: Technical skills are the specific physical competences that enable a dancer to perform movement accurately and safely
Key Fact: Posture is the way the body is held and provides the base from which all movement begins
Key Fact: Alignment refers to the correct positioning of body parts in relation to one another, ensuring efficient movement
Key Fact: Control is the ability to manage the body's movement with precision, including speed, direction and dynamics
Key Fact: Balance in a technical sense means maintaining equilibrium during both stillness and motion
Key Fact: Coordination links different body parts and timing to produce clean, accurate movement phrases
Key Fact: Spatial awareness is understanding where the body is in relation to the performance space, other dancers and the audience
Key Fact: Good posture and alignment reduce the risk of injury and improve the aesthetic quality of movement
Key Fact: Control allows a dancer to perform slow, sustained movements and fast, sharp actions with equal clarity
Key Fact: Spatial awareness ensures formations, pathways and spacing are maintained throughout group work
Key Fact: Technical skills are assessed in both the performance and choreography components of AQA 8236
Key Fact: Without solid technical skills, expressive and physical skills cannot be communicated effectively to the audience

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Technical Dance Skills, you must know:

  • Posture: The way the body is held, providing a stable base for all dance movement
  • Alignment: The correct positioning of body parts in relation to each other for safe and efficient movement
  • Control: The ability to manage the body's movement precisely, including speed, direction and dynamics
  • Spatial awareness: An understanding of the body's position in relation to the space, other dancers and the audience
  • Turnout: The external rotation of the leg from the hip joint, used in many dance styles for aesthetic line
  • Placement: The accurate positioning of a body part in space during a movement or stillness

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What is the difference between posture and alignment?

Q: Why is spatial awareness important in group dance?

Q: How does control affect the quality of a slow, sustained movement?

Q: Explain how poor alignment can lead to injury.

Q: Why are technical skills necessary for expressive performance?

✅ Answers

  1. Posture is the overall way the body is held; alignment is the specific positioning of body parts in relation to each other for efficient movement.
  2. Spatial awareness allows dancers to maintain correct formations, avoid collisions, use the performance space effectively and ensure the audience can see the choreography.
  3. Control enables the dancer to move with even speed and steady dynamics, creating a smooth, continuous line without wobbling or losing balance.
  4. Poor alignment places stress on joints and muscles incorrectly, for example landing a jump with misaligned knees can strain ligaments and cause long-term damage.
  5. Without technical accuracy, the dancer cannot communicate the intended mood or meaning clearly — for example, poor control undermines the quality of a soft, lyrical phrase.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Distinguish clearly between posture (how the body is held overall) and alignment (how body parts relate to each other)
  • When writing about control, always link it to a specific movement quality such as sustained or sudden dynamics
  • Spatial awareness questions often require you to discuss formations, levels, directions and use of stage area
  • Use dance terminology rather than everyday language — say 'alignment' not 'standing up straight'
  • In performance analysis, mention at least two technical skills and connect them to the choreographic intention

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Posture and alignment mean the same thing. ✓ Posture is the overall way the body is held; alignment is the specific relationship between body parts such as shoulders over hips and knees over toes.

✗ Control only applies to slow movements. ✓ Control applies to all speeds — it means the dancer manages the movement precisely whether slow and sustained or fast and sudden.

✗ Spatial awareness is just about not bumping into other dancers. ✓ Spatial awareness includes understanding the performance space, maintaining formations, using pathways and directions, and positioning relative to the audience.

✗ Technical skills are only relevant in the studio. ✓ Technical skills are essential in all performance contexts — they ensure safe, accurate and expressive dancing on any stage or in any venue.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe two technical skills and explain how they improve dance performance. Use examples to support your answer. [6 marks]

Alignment and control are two technical skills that significantly improve dance performance. Alignment is the correct positioning of body parts in relation to each other, such as stacking the head over the shoulders, ribs over hips and knees over toes. Good alignment ensures movements are efficient and safe; for example, when a dancer performs a plié, correct knee-over-toe alignment prevents joint strain and allows the movement to look clean and balanced. Control is the ability to manage the body's movement with precision, regulating speed, direction and dynamics. For instance, a dancer performing a sustained developpé to the front must control the extension of the leg slowly and evenly, maintaining balance and poise throughout. Without control, the movement would rush, wobble or lose its intended dynamic quality. Together, alignment provides the structural foundation for safe technique, while control allows the dancer to perform with accuracy and expressiveness, communicating the choreographic intention clearly to the audience.
