Emancipation of Expressionism — Kenrick H2O Sandy

Kenrick H2O Sandy/Boy Blue Entertainment: features and analysis

Key Fact: 'Emancipation of Expressionism' was choreographed by Kenrick H2O Sandy for Boy Blue Entertainment and premiered in 2017
Key Fact: Boy Blue Entertainment is a hip-hop dance company founded by Kenrick H2O Sandy and Michael Asante
Key Fact: The choreographic intention is to demonstrate the power of expression through hip-hop dance and show how movement can communicate freedom
Key Fact: The stimulus was the concept of emancipation — liberation through expressive movement — and the role of hip-hop as an art form for self-expression
Key Fact: Movement vocabulary includes hip-hop foundations such as popping, locking, breaking, krumping and house
Key Fact: The work blends hip-hop with contemporary influences, creating a unique fusion style
Key Fact: Dynamics are a major feature — the work contrasts explosive, high-energy moments with controlled, still sections
Key Fact: The structure builds from a single dancer to the full ensemble, symbolising the spread of expression from individual to collective
Key Fact: The aural setting features a powerful score by Michael Asante mixing beats, music and the spoken word 'emancipation'
Key Fact: Costume is urban streetwear — hoodies, tracksuits and trainers — reflecting hip-hop culture and the dancers' identity
Key Fact: Lighting is bold and theatrical, using spotlights, silhouettes and colour to highlight individuals and the group
Key Fact: The work celebrates hip-hop as a legitimate art form worthy of theatrical presentation and academic study

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Emancipation of Expressionism — Kenrick H2O Sandy, you must know:

  • Hip-hop dance: A range of street dance styles including breaking, popping, locking and krumping, rooted in African American and Latino culture
  • Boy Blue Entertainment: A hip-hop dance company founded by Kenrick H2O Sandy and Michael Asante, dedicated to elevating hip-hop as a theatrical art form
  • Popping: A hip-hop style involving quick muscle contractions creating a sharp, jerking effect in the body
  • Locking: A hip-hop style featuring sudden freezes or 'locks' between fast, funky movements, creating a distinctive rhythm
  • Krumping: An expressive, aggressive street dance style involving fast, powerful chest pops, arm swings and stomps
  • Emancipation: The act of being set free from restrictions — in this work, liberation through the expressive power of hip-hop dance

✅ Answers

  1. To demonstrate the power of expression through hip-hop dance, showing how movement can communicate freedom and serve as a form of artistic liberation.
  2. Popping, locking and krumping — though breaking and house are also featured in the movement vocabulary.
  3. The structure builds from a single dancer to the full ensemble, symbolising how expression spreads from the individual to the collective — one voice becoming many.
  4. Michael Asante's score mixes beats, music and the spoken word 'emancipation', creating a powerful, driving atmosphere that energises the dancers and reinforces the theme of liberation.
  5. Urban streetwear — hoodies, tracksuits and trainers — reflects hip-hop culture and the dancers' authentic identity, grounding the work in its cultural roots.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ 'Emancipation of Expressionism' is purely a contemporary dance work. ✓ The work is rooted in hip-hop dance styles including popping, locking, krumping and breaking — it blends hip-hop with contemporary influences but hip-hop is the foundation.

✗ The work has a narrative storyline. ✓ The work does not tell a story — it is structured around the idea of expression growing from individual to collective, building energy and intensity rather than following a plot.

✗ Kenrick H2O Sandy choreographed the work alone. ✓ Kenrick H2O Sandy is the choreographer, but the work was created for Boy Blue Entertainment, the company he co-founded with Michael Asante who composed the score.

✗ The costume is traditional dancewear. ✓ The costume is urban streetwear — hoodies, tracksuits and trainers — chosen to reflect hip-hop culture and the dancers' authentic identity.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the key choreographic features of 'Emancipation of Expressionism' and explain how they communicate the intention of liberation through expression. [6 marks]

The key choreographic features of 'Emancipation of Expressionism' are hip-hop movement vocabulary, dynamic contrast, building structure and powerful design elements, all of which communicate liberation through expression. The hip-hop vocabulary — popping, locking, krumping and breaking — is the foundation of the work. These styles are inherently expressive: krumping's aggressive chest pops and arm swings release raw emotion, while popping's sharp muscle contractions show precise control over the body. The dynamic contrast between explosive, high-energy ensemble sections and moments of controlled stillness creates dramatic tension, symbolising the struggle and release that emancipation involves. The structure builds from a single dancer to the full ensemble, representing how individual expression becomes collective power — one voice inspires many until the whole group moves with unified energy. The aural setting by Michael Asante drives the work forward with powerful beats and the spoken word 'emancipation', reinforcing the theme. Urban streetwear costume connects the dancers to hip-hop culture, showing this is authentic self-expression, not theatrical costume. Bold lighting highlights individuals breaking free and the group moving as one. Together, these features make the intention unmistakable — hip-hop dance is presented as a powerful form of liberation, where the body expresses what words cannot.
