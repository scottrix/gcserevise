Devising Process and Stimulus

Responding to stimuli (images, text, music, objects, themes); research and exploration; brainstorming and mind-mapping; initial ideas to structured drama; collaborative creation.

Key Fact: A stimulus can be anything: an image, a song, a news story, a poem, an object — it's the starting point for devising.
Key Fact: Research: explore the stimulus from multiple angles — personal, social, historical, political — to find depth.
Key Fact: Brainstorming: generate as many ideas as possible without judging them — quantity leads to quality.
Key Fact: Collaborative creation: everyone contributes — share ideas physically and verbally, build on each other's suggestions.
Key Fact: From stimulus to structure: move from exploration (what does this make us think/feel?) to selection (which ideas are strongest?) to structuring (how do we shape this into drama?).

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe the process you would use to develop a piece of drama from a stimulus. Use a specific example.

Q2: How can research enhance a devised piece? What types of research might you do?

Q3: Explain how collaborative devising is different from working with a script. What are the challenges and benefits?

✅ Answers

  1. Full process: receive stimulus → initial reactions → research → brainstorm → improvise → select strongest ideas → structure → rehearse → refine. Give a specific stimulus example showing each stage.
  2. Research adds depth and authenticity: factual research (statistics, history), personal research (interviews, experiences), artistic research (other plays, art works). It prevents cliches and enriches content.
  3. Collaborative: everyone contributes ideas, ownership is shared, multiple perspectives enrich the work. Challenges: compromise, conflicting visions, unequal contribution. Benefits: creativity, investment, diverse ideas.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always describe the FULL devising journey — don't just talk about the final piece
  • Show how the STIMULUS led to SPECIFIC dramatic choices
  • In your portfolio, document EVERY stage with evidence
  • Collaborative means shared ownership — show how everyone contributed

📝 Exam Technique

Drama Exam Tips:
For devising process questions, use a chronological structure: Stimulus → Response → Research → Exploration → Selection → Structure → Rehearsal → Performance. At each stage, describe what you DID, WHY you did it, and what EFFECT it had on the final piece.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Devising is just making up a play on the spot — you don't need to plan or research. Correct: Devising is a structured creative process. It begins with careful stimulus analysis and research, moves through collaborative exploration and improvisation, and requires deliberate selection and structuring. The best devised work combines creative freedom with rigorous development — it is never just improvised on the spot.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

You are given the stimulus of a photograph showing a divided community. Describe how you would use this stimulus to devise a piece of drama, explaining your process from initial response to final performance.

A grade 9 response will: describe the initial response to the stimulus; show detailed research plans; explain how exploration techniques (improvisation, hot-seating) develop ideas; describe the selection process; show how the piece is structured; justify each creative decision with reference to intended effect on the audience.

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

Devising is Component 2 (30% NEA). You must submit a devised performance and a portfolio. The portfolio must document your process (AO4: 30% — evaluation and analysis of your own work). You must show how practitioner ideas influenced your work, how you collaborated, and how you refined your piece. The portfolio is as important as the performance.
