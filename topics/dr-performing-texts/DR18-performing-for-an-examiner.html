Performing for an Examiner

Component 3 practical exam preparation; performing two extracts; working with a group; maintaining focus under pressure; examiner visit protocol; performance etiquette and preparation.

Key Fact: Component 3: perform two extracts from ONE play — assessed by a visiting examiner.
Key Fact: Group work: you are assessed INDIVIDUALLY even in a group performance — you must maintain character and focus throughout.
Key Fact: Preparation: full dress and technical rehearsals before the exam day — no surprises on the day.
Key Fact: Focus under pressure: if something goes wrong, STAY IN CHARACTER and adapt — the examiner assesses how you handle the moment.
Key Fact: Performance etiquette: silence backstage, full costume and make-up, clear entrances and exits, commitment from the first second.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What should you do if something goes wrong during a performance for the examiner? Why is this important?

Q2: Describe how you would prepare for a visiting examiner assessment from one week before to the performance day.

Q3: How are individual performers assessed within a group performance? What does the examiner look for?

✅ Answers

  1. Stay in character and adapt — never break character or apologise. The examiner wants to see how you handle the unexpected. A covered mistake shows professionalism; a broken character shows immaturity. Adapt and carry on.
  2. One week before: full run-throughs with notes. Three days before: technical and dress rehearsals. Day before: light rehearsal, rest, prepare costume. Day of: warm-up, focus, silence backstage, full commitment from the first moment.
  3. Each performer is marked individually on: vocal skills, physical skills, characterisation, interaction with others, and overall dramatic effectiveness. The group context matters but your personal choices, commitment and truthfulness earn your marks.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • The examiner assesses from the MOMENT you enter the performance space
  • Individual marks — your group's weakness should not drag you down if you perform well
  • Cover mistakes IN CHARACTER — never acknowledge them
  • Full costume, make-up and props show professionalism and commitment

📝 Exam Technique

Drama Exam Tips:
For the practical exam, the examiner assesses: 1) Vocal skills (clarity, projection, pace, pitch), 2) Physical skills (movement, gesture, facial expression, spatial awareness), 3) Characterisation (understanding, truthfulness, development), 4) Interaction (listening, responding, ensemble awareness), 5) Overall effectiveness (impact, engagement, dramatic shape).

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: If someone in your group forgets their lines or makes a mistake, your mark will be affected too. Correct: You are assessed INDIVIDUALLY. If another performer makes a mistake, the examiner watches how YOU respond — staying in character, adapting and supporting the scene shows professionalism and can actually enhance your mark. Breaking character or panicking hurts YOUR mark, not theirs.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe how you would prepare for and perform in a practical exam with a visiting examiner. Explain what the examiner will be looking for and how you would demonstrate your best work.

A grade 9 response will: describe thorough preparation (rehearsal schedule, technical/dress rehearsals); explain performance etiquette (costume, focus, professionalism); detail how to demonstrate each assessed skill; show understanding of individual assessment within group context; explain how to handle unexpected situations; demonstrate awareness of what the examiner values.

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

Component 3 (Texts in Practice, 30%) is assessed by a visiting examiner on AO3 (perform with understanding). The two extracts must come from the same play. The examiner assesses each performer individually on vocal skills, physical skills, characterisation, interaction and overall effectiveness. Maximum marks require exceptional commitment, truthfulness and dramatic impact.
