Frantic Assembly and Physical Theatre

Physical theatre conventions; Frantic Assembly's techniques: chair work, push hands, lift and lean, hymns hands, unison, counterpoint; devising through movement; DV8 Physical Theatre.

Key Fact: Physical theatre: the body is the primary means of expression — movement communicates meaning beyond words.
Key Fact: Frantic Assembly's chair work: using chairs as the central prop — creates dynamic, rhythmic, often aggressive sequences.
Key Fact: Push hands: two performers pushing against each other's hands — creates tension, balance and relational dynamics.
Key Fact: Lift and lean: one performer supports another's weight — requires trust, shows relationship and dependency.
Key Fact: Unison and counterpoint: moving together (unison) shows unity; moving in opposition (counterpoint) shows conflict or individuality.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe three Frantic Assembly techniques and explain the dramatic effect each can create.

Q2: How can physical theatre communicate emotions and relationships more effectively than dialogue alone?

Q3: You are creating a devised piece about conflict. How would you use physical theatre techniques to explore this theme?

✅ Answers

  1. Three named techniques with dramatic effect: chair work (rhythm, aggression, status); push hands (tension, balance of power); lift and lean (trust, dependency, vulnerability). Each linked to specific dramatic meaning.
  2. Physical theatre bypasses language to communicate directly through the body — proximity, touch, weight and rhythm reveal relationship dynamics instantly. It can show conflict, intimacy or power shifts without a single word.
  3. Practical response showing: push hands to show conflict escalation; unison breaking into counterpoint to show unity fracturing; chair work to create aggressive confrontations; lift/lean to show shifting power. Each linked to the theme of conflict.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Name Frantic Assembly techniques precisely — 'chair duet', not 'chairs scene'
  • Physical theatre is about MEANING through MOVEMENT — not just 'it looks cool'
  • Always link physical choices to the emotional/relational content
  • Safety and trust are essential when discussing contact work

📝 Exam Technique

Drama Exam Tips:
For physical theatre questions, describe movement PRECISELY: who moves, how they move, what it represents, and the effect. 'Two performers stand close, hands pressed together, pushing rhythmically — the counter-tension represents their competing desires, the increasing force shows escalating conflict.'

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Physical theatre is just dancing — it doesn't have the same dramatic depth as scripted dialogue. Correct: Physical theatre uses movement as a primary language for dramatic meaning. Frantic Assembly's techniques communicate complex emotions and relationships through the body. Movement can be MORE powerful than words for showing conflict, intimacy and power because it bypasses intellectual processing and hits the audience viscerally.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

How would you use Frantic Assembly techniques to devise a piece about a relationship breaking down? Describe and justify at least three physical theatre sequences.

A grade 9 response will: name three specific techniques; describe each sequence precisely (who, what, how); explain the dramatic meaning of each; show how the sequences develop the theme; consider the audience's experience; evaluate the effectiveness of physical theatre for this subject matter.
