DT18: Finishing Techniques

Foundation Higher All Boards

Finishing Techniques

Surface finishes for metals, polymers, timber and textiles; painting, powder coating, anodising, plating, varnishing, laminating; functional and decorative purposes.

Key Fact: Metal finishes - functional: galvanising (zinc coating prevents rust), anodising (thickened aluminium oxide layer - harder, can be dyed), plating (chromium/nickel for hardness). Decorative: powder coating (baked polymer - durable, many colours), painting (versatile but less durable).
Key Fact: Timber finishes - functional: preservative (prevents rot), fire retardant. Decorative: varnish (clear, highlights grain, waterproof), oil (penetrates wood, natural), wax (soft sheen, less durable), paint (opaque, hides grain), stain (changes colour, shows grain).
Key Fact: Polymer finishes - self-finishing (coloured in the mould). Surface: printing (pad, screen, hot-foil), texturing (etched mould surfaces), vacuum metallising.
Key Fact: Textile finishes - functional: waterproofing (DWR coating), flame retardancy, stain resistance. Decorative: dyeing, printing, embroidery, applique.
Key Fact: Finishing purpose: protection (corrosion, UV, abrasion, moisture), identification (branding, safety warnings), aesthetics (colour, texture, sheen).

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain why galvanising is used on steel gate hinges but not on stainless steel hinges.

Q2: Compare varnish and oil as finishes for an oak dining table, justifying your recommendation for a family home.

Q3: A polymer product needs the company logo applied. Compare pad printing and screen printing.

✅ Answers

  1. Steel contains iron which rusts. Galvanising coats with zinc - zinc corrodes sacrificially, protecting exposed iron even if scratched. Stainless steel has 10.5%+ chromium forming a self-healing oxide layer - does not rust, so galvanising is unnecessary.
  2. Varnish: hard clear film - excellent waterproof protection, resists scratches and heat, easy to wipe clean. Oil: penetrates wood, warm natural look, easy to repair, water-resistant but not waterproof, needs regular reapplication. For a busy family home: varnish for superior protection against spills and scratches.
  3. Pad printing: transfers ink via silicone pad - excellent for curved/irregular surfaces, small to medium logos, fine detail. Screen printing: ink through mesh stencil - best for flat surfaces, large areas, bold colours. For curved polymer casing: pad printing. For flat panel: screen printing.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • State WHY the finish is needed (protection/aesthetics/identification) before choosing
  • Link the finish to the material AND the use environment
  • Know which finishes are decorative vs functional vs both

📝 Exam Technique

D&T Exam Tips:
For finishing questions: 1) Identify the material, 2) State requirements, 3) Propose an appropriate finish, 4) Explain how it works, 5) Justify the choice.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Painting is always the best finish because it gives the most colour options and covers any surface. Correct: Paint is versatile but has limitations: it chips and scratches easily, hides the material beneath, requires surface preparation and regular maintenance. For a garden gate, galvanising + powder coating is far more durable. For an oak table, varnish protects while showing the grain.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

A manufacturer produces aluminium outdoor furniture. Evaluate THREE appropriate finishing techniques and recommend the most suitable for a coastal environment.

A grade 9 response will: identify coastal challenges (salt spray, UV, moisture); evaluate anodising (excellent corrosion resistance, UV stable, oxide layer is integral to aluminium - cannot chip, self-healing); powder coating (good but can chip on edges); painting (least durable - salt penetrates cracks, paint peels). Recommendation: anodising is most suitable for coastal environments.
