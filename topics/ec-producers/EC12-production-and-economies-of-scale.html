Production and Economies of Scale

Production, productivity, and economies of scale: the difference between production and productivity, and how economies and diseconomies of scale affect firms.

Key Fact: Production is total output. Productivity is output per unit of input (e.g. output per worker per hour). Higher productivity = more from the same resources.
Key Fact: Factors affecting productivity: technology, training, motivation, capital investment, and management.
Key Fact: Economies of scale: as firms grow, average costs fall — purchasing (bulk discounts), technical (specialist machinery), managerial (specialist managers), financial (lower borrowing rates), risk-bearing (diversification).
Key Fact: Diseconomies of scale: beyond a certain size, average costs rise — communication problems, coordination difficulties, demotivation, bureaucracy.
Key Fact: Minimum efficient scale (MES) is the smallest output at which a firm minimises long-run average costs.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain the difference between production and productivity, and why productivity matters more for living standards.

Q2: Describe three types of economies of scale and how each reduces average costs.

Q3: Evaluate whether a firm should always grow as large as possible.

✅ Answers

  1. Production = total output (1,000 cars/week). Productivity = output per input (10 cars per worker/week). Productivity matters more because it measures efficiency — higher productivity enables higher wages without inflation, raising living standards.
  2. Purchasing: bulk-buying discounts (Tesco vs corner shop). Technical: specialist machinery that small firms cannot justify. Financial: large firms borrow at lower interest rates.
  3. For growth: economies of scale reduce costs, market power increases, diversification reduces risk. Against: diseconomies raise costs (poor communication, bureaucracy), less innovation, slower response to change. Conclusion: growth is beneficial up to where diseconomies outweigh economies.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Production = total output; Productivity = output per input. Always distinguish.
  • List five types of economies: purchasing, technical, managerial, financial, risk-bearing.
  • Diseconomies are about MANAGEMENT problems, not production costs.

📝 Exam Technique

Economics Exam Tips:
When evaluating firm growth, use the EDC framework: Economies of scale, Diseconomies of scale, Competitive effect. Optimal size balances economies against diseconomies.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Bigger firms are always more efficient than smaller firms. Correct: Economies of scale apply UP TO A POINT — beyond MES, costs may level off or rise. Small firms can be efficient through: lower overheads, flexibility, personal service, and niche focus. In some industries (professional services, creative), small firms outperform large ones by avoiding bureaucracy.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate whether small firms can survive in industries dominated by large firms with economies of scale.

A grade 9 response will: acknowledge large firms' cost advantages; identify small firm advantages (flexibility, niche, personal service, innovation); consider industry type (scale essential in car manufacturing, less so in services); conclude: small firms survive by competing where scale is a disadvantage — quality, customisation, speed.
