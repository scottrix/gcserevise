🎮 Hobbies and Interests

Leisure activities, reading, music, films; hobbies in different tenses; opinions and reasons

Key Fact: This topic covers key vocabulary and structures for hobbies and interests. You need to know the topic vocabulary, be able to use it in all four skills (listening, speaking, reading, writing), and express opinions with reasons.

📋 Key Vocabulary and Structures

For Hobbies and Interests, you must know:

  • Topic-specific vocabulary (nouns, verbs, adjectives)
  • Key grammar structures for this topic area
  • How to express and justify opinions
  • How to describe past, present and future related to this topic
  • Cultural knowledge about French-speaking countries and communities

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in French about hobbies and interests.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on hobbies and interests.

Q3: Translate into French: "I think hobbies and interests is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary, with correct adjective agreement, articles and verb conjugation. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph with: introduction (à mon avis...), opinion with reason (je pense que... parce que...), a different viewpoint (cependant...), and a conclusion (en conclusion...). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Je pense que [topic] est important(e) parce que..." — ensure adjective agreement (important vs importante depending on noun gender), correct verb form, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (je pense que... parce que...)
  • Check adjective agreement (gender and number) before finishing
  • Use complex structures: negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais), pronouns (le, la, les), conjunctions (cependant, donc, en plus)
  • Include cultural references to French-speaking countries
  • For writing: plan your response, check verb conjugations, re-read for errors

📝 Exam Technique

French Exam Tips — Hobbies and Interests:
1. For Hobbies and Interests, use a range of vocabulary and complex grammatical structures to access higher marks
2. Check verb tenses, agreements and word order carefully in your written work
3. In translation tasks, translate meaning rather than word-for-word
4. When writing about Hobbies and Interests, include opinions and justifications, not just descriptions
5. For listening and reading, focus on key words and cognates to extract meaning

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget to use the perfect tense for completed past activities. Wrong: Hier, je joue au foot. Correct: Hier, j'ai joué au foot (perfect tense for completed past actions). Students also mix up à and en with countries. Wrong: Je vais à France. Correct: Je vais en France (en with feminine countries starting with consonant).

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Writing task: Write about leisure activities, reading, music, films; hobbies in different tenses; opinions and reasons.

A grade 9 answer will: use at least three tenses accurately; include complex structures (subordinate clauses, pronouns, negation); show precise grammar (agreement, conjugation, spelling); express and justify opinions; include cultural knowledge; use a wide range of vocabulary; be well-organised with connecting words (cependant, en revanche, de plus, en conclusion).

Mark scheme: Content (relevance and detail) + Quality of language (accuracy and range) = total marks

Notes Détaillées

Les Loisirs et les Passions

Hobbies vocabulary: la lecture (reading), la musique (music), le dessin (drawing), la peinture (painting), la cuisine (cooking), le jardinage (gardening), la photographie (photography), les jeux vidéo (video games), la danse (dance), le théâtre (theatre/drama). Use faire de for activities: je fais de la danse, je fais du théâtre. Use jouer de for instruments: je joue du piano, je joue de la guitare — note this is jouer de, not jouer à.

Discussing hobbies requires expressing opinions with reasons: J'adore la lecture parce que ça me détend (I love reading because it relaxes me). Je préfère les jeux vidéo — c'est plus amusant (I prefer video games — it's more fun). For Higher tier, use nuanced opinions: Ce qui me passionne, c'est la photographie (What fascinates me is photography). Je suis passionné(e) par la musique (I am passionate about music).

French teenagers enjoy many of the same hobbies as British teens, but cultural differences exist. La BD (bande dessinée — comic books) is hugely popular in France — it's considered an art form, not just for children. France is the second-largest comic market after Japan. Le Festival d'Angoulême is the world's biggest comic festival.

Exemple

Je suis passionné par la musique — je joue de la guitare depuis cinq ans. Le week-end, je fais aussi de la photographie et j'aime lire des bandes dessinées françaises. — I am passionate about music — I have been playing the guitar for five years. At the weekend, I also do photography and I like reading French comic books.

La Culture des Loisirs en France

French cultural life is rich and heavily subsidised. Le pass Culture, introduced in 2019, gives every 18-year-old 300€ to spend on cultural activities — cinema tickets, books, concerts, museum entries, music lessons. This pass reflects France's commitment to cultural access for all. Young people can use it to buy des livres (books), go to des concerts, visit des musées, or even buy un instrument de musique.

France has a strong tradition of les associations (clubs and societies). Many towns have la maison des associations — a building housing multiple clubs. Common activities include le club de lecture (book club), la chorale (choir), le club de jeux de société (board game club), l'atelier de peinture (painting workshop). Joining an association is a very French way to pursue hobbies.

In Sénégal, hobbies often have a communal focus. Le football de rue (street football) is ubiquitous. Le sabar — traditional drumming and dancing — is both a hobby and a cultural practice. In Belgium, les bandes dessinées are also hugely popular — Belgium is the birthplace of Tintin (Hergé) and Les Schtroumpfs (The Smurfs, by Peyo).

Exemple

Le pass Culture donne 300 euros aux jeunes de 18 ans pour des activités culturelles. C'est une initiative fantastique pour rendre la culture accessible à tous. — The pass Culture gives 300 euros to 18-year-olds for cultural activities. It's a fantastic initiative to make culture accessible to everyone.

Jouer d'un Instrument et la Musique Francophone

To talk about playing instruments: jouer du piano (to play the piano), jouer de la flûte (to play the flute), jouer de la batterie (to play the drums), jouer du violon (to play the violin). Remember: jouer de + article (du/de la/des) for instruments, but jouer à + article (au/à la/aux) for sports. This distinction is a classic GCSE test point.

Francophone music is incredibly diverse. From Édith Piaf and Johnny Hallyday to modern artists like Aya Nakamura (Mali-French), Stromae (Belgian), and Cœur de Pirate (Québécois), French-language music spans many genres. Le rap français is one of the most popular genres among young people — artists like Nekfeu, Orelsan, and PNL dominate the charts.

Discussing music preferences: Je préfère écouter du rap français parce que les paroles sont poétiques (I prefer listening to French rap because the lyrics are poetic). Ma chanson préférée est « Formidable » de Stromae (My favourite song is « Formidable » by Stromae). Mentioning specific francophone artists shows cultural knowledge.

Exemple

J'écoute surtout du rap français — j'adore Orelsan et Nekfeu. Stromae, qui est belge, est aussi un artiste incroyable. Sa chanson « Alors on danse » est connue dans le monde entier. — I mostly listen to French rap — I love Orelsan and Nekfeu. Stromae, who is Belgian, is also an incredible artist. His song « Alors on danse » is known worldwide.

Questions Supplémentaires

Q4: Quels sont vos loisirs préférés et pourquoi? (What are your favourite hobbies and why?)

Q5: Comparez les loisirs des jeunes en France et dans un autre pays francophone. (Compare young people's hobbies in France and another francophone country.)

Réponses Supplémentaires

  1. Mes loisirs préférés sont la musique et la photographie. Je joue de la guitare depuis quatre ans et je suis dans un groupe avec mes amis. La musique me détend et me permet d'exprimer ma créativité. J'aime aussi la photographie parce que c'est un moyen de capturer des moments importants. Le week-end, je sors prendre des photos dans ma ville. En plus, j'utilise mon pass Culture pour aller au concert. — My favourite hobbies are music and photography. I've been playing guitar for four years and I'm in a band with my friends. Music relaxes me and allows me to express my creativity. I also like photography because it's a way to capture important moments. At the weekend, I go out to take photos in my town. Also, I use my pass Culture to go to concerts.
  2. En France, les jeunes aiment écouter du rap français, jouer aux jeux vidéo et aller au ciné. Le pass Culture les aide à accéder à la culture. Au Sénégal, les jeunes jouent souvent au football de rue et participent au sabar, qui est à la fois un loisir et une tradition culturelle. En Belgique, la bande dessinée est une passion nationale — Tintin est né en Belgique! Tandis qu'en France les loisirs sont plus individualistes, au Sénégal, ils sont souvent communautaires. — In France, young people like listening to French rap, playing video games and going to the cinema. The pass Culture helps them access culture. In Sénégal, young people often play street football and participate in the sabar, which is both a hobby and a cultural tradition. In Belgium, comic books are a national passion — Tintin was born in Belgium! Whereas in France hobbies are more individualistic, in Sénégal they are often communal.
