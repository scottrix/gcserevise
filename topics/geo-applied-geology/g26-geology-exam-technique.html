Geology Exam Technique

Strategies for answering GCSE Geology exam questions, including mathematical skills, fieldwork, and extended writing.

Key Fact: AO1: Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of geological ideas and methods (35%)
Key Fact: AO2: Apply knowledge and understanding of geology to familiar and unfamiliar contexts (35%)
Key Fact: AO3: Analyse, interpret and evaluate geological information and methods (30%)
Key Fact: Mathematical skills: calculating gradients, using standard form, ratios, percentages, interpreting graphs
Key Fact: Fieldwork: describe, explain and evaluate field observations using correct terminology
Key Fact: Extended writing requires structured answers using geological terminology and specific examples

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Geology Exam Technique, you must know:

  • AO1: Assessment Objective 1, demonstrate knowledge and understanding of geological principles and methods
  • AO2: Assessment Objective 2, apply knowledge and understanding to familiar and unfamiliar geological contexts
  • AO3: Assessment Objective 3, analyse, interpret and evaluate geological information, data and methods
  • Command word: The instruction in a question telling you what to do (e.g. describe, explain, compare, evaluate)
  • Field sketch: A simplified drawing made in the field highlighting key geological features, labelled with annotations

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What is the difference between describe and explain?

Q: How should you approach a 6-mark extended writing question?

Q: What mathematical skills are required in GCSE Geology?

Q: How do you make a good field sketch?

Q: What does evaluate mean in an exam question?

✅ Answers

  1. Describe = state what you observe (facts, features, data). Explain = say why or how it happens (mechanism, cause, process).
  2. 1) Identify the command word and focus. 2) Plan to cover all aspects. 3) Write in structured paragraphs with geological terminology. 4) Include specific examples or case studies. 5) If evaluating, consider both sides and reach a justified conclusion.
  3. Calculating gradients, using standard form, calculating ratios and percentages, interpreting data from tables and graphs, and calculating true thickness from apparent thickness and dip angle.
  4. 1) Choose a clear viewpoint. 2) Draw the outline of major features. 3) Add geological details (bedding, folds, faults). 4) Label and annotate key features. 5) Include a scale, direction and title.
  5. Weigh up strengths and weaknesses, consider both sides of evidence, and reach a supported conclusion.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Command words: describe = what; explain = why/how; evaluate = weigh and conclude
  • OCR J357 AO weightings: AO1 35%, AO2 35%, AO3 30%
  • In maths questions: write formula, substitute, calculate, include units
  • Fieldwork answers must use specific observations and correct terminology

📝 Exam Technique

GCSE Geology Exam Tips:
Use precise geological terminology in all answers. For calculation questions, always show your working and include units. When describing field observations, reference specific evidence and measurements. For extended questions, use structured paragraphs with clear scientific reasoning. OCR J357 rewards answers that demonstrate understanding of geology as a practical, observational science with appropriate use of field evidence and named examples.

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ Just writing the final answer gives full marks ✓ You must show your working; method marks are awarded even if the final answer is wrong

✗ Describe and explain need the same type of answer ✓ Describe = what happens; explain = why/how it happens. Many students lose marks by describing when asked to explain

✗ You don't need units in calculations ✓ Omitting units loses marks; always include the correct unit with numerical answers

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Explain how to approach different question types in the GCSE Geology exam and describe effective revision strategies. [6 marks]

For describe questions, state what you observe: features, patterns, data trends. For explain questions, give reasons and mechanisms: why features form, how processes work. For compare questions, state similarities AND differences using comparative language. For evaluate questions, present evidence for and against, then reach a justified conclusion. For calculations, always write the formula, substitute values, show working and include units. For fieldwork questions, use precise geological terminology and specific observations. For extended writing (6-mark), plan first, use structured paragraphs, include named examples and correct vocabulary. Effective revision strategies include: creating summary grids for rock types (composition, texture, origin), practising map interpretation with past papers, drawing annotated field sketches from photographs, making glossary cards for key terms, and timing yourself on past questions. The OCR J357 exam tests three AOs: AO1 (recall, 35%), AO2 (application, 35%) and AO3 (analysis and evaluation, 30%). Target each AO specifically: AO1 needs accurate factual knowledge; AO2 needs applying concepts to unfamiliar contexts; AO3 needs critical analysis of data and evidence.

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

AO1 (Knowledge & Understanding): Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of geology exam technique, including key geological concepts, observational data and theoretical models relevant to OCR J357.

AO2 (Application of Knowledge): Apply knowledge and understanding of geology exam technique to both familiar and unfamiliar geological contexts, using field evidence and theoretical principles to explain phenomena.

AO3 (Analysis & Evaluation): Analyse geological data related to geology exam technique, evaluate evidence from observations and investigations, and construct reasoned arguments using scientific methodology.
