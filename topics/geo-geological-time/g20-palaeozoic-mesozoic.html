Palaeozoic & Mesozoic Eras

Key events of the Palaeozoic and Mesozoic eras: the Cambrian explosion, colonisation of land, major extinction events, and the age of reptiles.

Key Fact: The Palaeozoic Era (541-252 Ma): Cambrian explosion of hard-bodied life, Silurian marine life, Devonian age of fish and early land plants, Carboniferous coal forests, Permian mass extinction
Key Fact: The Cambrian explosion (~541 Ma): rapid diversification of life with hard shells and skeletons, making fossilisation much more common
Key Fact: The Carboniferous (359-299 Ma): warm, humid climate; vast swamp forests of ferns, horsetails and clubmosses formed today's coal deposits
Key Fact: The Permian-Triassic mass extinction (~252 Ma): the 'Great Dying' eliminated ~96% of marine species, caused by massive volcanism (Siberian Traps) and climate change
Key Fact: The Mesozoic Era (252-66 Ma): Triassic (recovery and early dinosaurs), Jurassic (age of dinosaurs and ammonites), Cretaceous (flowering plants, T-rex)
Key Fact: Ammonites are key Mesozoic index fossils: they evolved rapidly and were globally distributed, allowing precise correlation of Jurassic and Cretaceous rocks

📋 Key Vocabulary and Concepts

For Palaeozoic & Mesozoic Eras, you must know:

  • Cambrian explosion: The rapid diversification of life at the start of the Cambrian period (~541 Ma), when most major animal groups first appeared in the fossil record
  • Mass extinction: An event where a large proportion of species become extinct in a geologically short time; five major events are recognised
  • Ammonite: An extinct marine mollusc with a coiled, chambered shell; excellent Mesozoic index fossil due to rapid evolution and wide distribution
  • Coal measures: Carboniferous sedimentary rocks containing coal seams formed from compressed tropical swamp vegetation
  • Siberian Traps: A vast area of flood basalt in Russia, erupted at the end of the Permian; linked to the Permian-Triassic mass extinction through climate change

❓ Practice Questions

Q: What was the Cambrian explosion and why is it important?

Q: How did Coal Measures form in the Carboniferous?

Q: What caused the Permian-Triassic mass extinction?

Q: Why are ammonites useful index fossils?

Q: Name the three periods of the Mesozoic Era in order.

✅ Answers

  1. The rapid diversification of life ~541 million years ago, when organisms evolved hard shells and skeletons for the first time. This made fossilisation much more likely, producing an apparent 'explosion' of life in the fossil record. It marks the start of the Palaeozoic Era.
  2. Vast swamp forests of ferns, horsetails and clubmosses grew in warm, humid conditions. When plants died, they fell into waterlogged swamps where oxygen was absent, preventing decay. Over millions of years, the accumulated plant material was buried, compressed and heated, forming coal seams within sedimentary rock sequences.
  3. Massive volcanic eruptions of the Siberian Traps released enormous volumes of CO2 and other gases, causing extreme global warming, ocean acidification and anoxia. These environmental changes eliminated ~96% of marine species and ~70% of land species, making it the most severe extinction event in Earth's history.
  4. They evolved rapidly (new species appeared every 1-2 million years), were geographically widespread in marine environments, and had distinctive coiled shells that are easily recognised. Their short time ranges allow precise dating and correlation of Mesozoic rocks worldwide.
  5. Triassic (252-201 Ma), Jurassic (201-145 Ma), Cretaceous (145-66 Ma).

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Cambrian explosion = hard shells appear, fossil record suddenly rich
  • Carboniferous = coal from tropical swamp forests
  • Permian-Triassic = biggest extinction, Siberian Traps volcanism
  • Ammonites = key Mesozoic index fossils, rapid evolution, wide distribution

⚠️ Common Errors

✗ The Cambrian explosion was the origin of life ✓ Life existed for ~3 billion years before the Cambrian; the 'explosion' was the rapid evolution of hard-bodied organisms, which greatly increased fossilisation potential

✗ Coal formed from dinosaurs ✓ Coal formed from compressed ancient plant material (ferns, horsetails, clubmosses) in Carboniferous swamps, millions of years before dinosaurs existed

✗ The dinosaurs were killed by the Permian extinction ✓ Dinosaurs went extinct at the Cretaceous-Paleogene (K-Pg) boundary ~66 Ma; the Permian extinction (~252 Ma) occurred before dinosaurs evolved

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the key events of the Palaeozoic and Mesozoic eras and explain the significance of the Permian mass extinction. [6 marks]

The Palaeozoic Era (541-252 Ma) began with the Cambrian explosion, when organisms evolved hard shells and skeletons, dramatically increasing the fossil record. The Silurian saw diverse marine life with coral reefs and jawless fish. The Devonian ('Age of Fishes') saw the evolution of bony fish and the first amphibians and land plants colonising the continents. The Carboniferous featured vast tropical swamp forests of ferns and horsetails that formed today's coal measures; amphibians thrived and early reptiles appeared. The Permian ended with the greatest mass extinction in Earth's history (~252 Ma), when ~96% of marine species and ~70% of land vertebrates were eliminated. This was caused by massive volcanic eruptions (Siberian Traps) that released huge volumes of CO2, causing extreme global warming, ocean acidification and widespread marine anoxia. The Mesozoic Era (252-66 Ma) saw recovery and diversification. The Triassic was a recovery period with early dinosaurs and marine reptiles. The Jurassic was the 'Age of Dinosaurs' with abundant ammonites (key index fossils), and the first birds. The Cretaceous saw flowering plants evolve and dinosaurs reach their peak diversity (T-rex, Triceratops). The era ended with the K-Pg extinction caused by an asteroid impact, wiping out the non-avian dinosaurs.
