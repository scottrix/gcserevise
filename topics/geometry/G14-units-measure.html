G14: Units of Measure

Foundation Higher All Boards

Use standard units of measure and related concepts; length, area, volume, capacity, mass, time, money

📋 Key Concepts

Units of measure are standard quantities used to measure physical quantities like length, mass, time, etc.

📝 Length

Metric units: km (kilometre), m (metre), cm (centimetre), mm (millimetre)
1 km = 1000 m
1 m = 100 cm
1 cm = 10 mm
1 km = 100,000 cm
Example 1

Convert 3.5 km to metres.

Solution: 3.5 × 1000 = 3500 m

Example 2

Convert 4500 mm to metres.

Solution: 4500 ÷ 1000 = 4.5 m

📝 Area

Metric units: km², m², cm², mm², hectares (ha)
1 km² = 1,000,000 m²
1 m² = 10,000 cm²
1 cm² = 100 mm²
1 hectare = 10,000 m²
Important: Area units are squared, so conversion factors are also squared!
Example 3

Convert 5 m² to cm².

Solution: 5 × 10,000 = 50,000 cm²

Example 4

Convert 30,000 cm² to m².

Solution: 30,000 ÷ 10,000 = 3 m²

📝 Volume

Metric units: km³, m³, cm³, mm³, litres (L), millilitres (mL)
1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³
1 cm³ = 1000 mm³
1 litre = 1000 mL
1 cm³ = 1 mL
Example 5

Convert 2.5 m³ to cm³.

Solution: 2.5 × 1,000,000 = 2,500,000 cm³

Example 6

Convert 3500 mL to litres.

Solution: 3500 ÷ 1000 = 3.5 L

📝 Mass

Metric units: tonne (t), kilogram (kg), gram (g), milligram (mg)
1 tonne = 1000 kg
1 kg = 1000 g
1 g = 1000 mg
Example 7

Convert 0.5 tonnes to grams.

Solution:

0.5 tonnes = 500 kg

500 kg = 500,000 g

📝 Time

Time units: hours (h), minutes (min), seconds (s)
1 hour = 60 minutes
1 minute = 60 seconds
1 hour = 3600 seconds
Example 8

Convert 2.5 hours to minutes.

Solution: 2.5 × 60 = 150 minutes

Example 9

Convert 90 minutes to hours.

Solution: 90 ÷ 60 = 1.5 hours

📝 Imperial-Metric Conversions

Approximate conversions:
ImperialMetric
1 inch≈ 2.5 cm
1 foot≈ 30 cm
1 mile≈ 1.6 km
1 pound (lb)≈ 450 g
1 gallon≈ 4.5 litres

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Convert 7500 m to km.

Q2: Convert 0.8 m² to cm².

Q3: Convert 3 litres to mL.

Q4: Convert 2.4 kg to grams.

Q5: Convert 135 minutes to hours.

✅ Answers

  1. 7.5 km
  2. 8000 cm²
  3. 3000 mL
  4. 2400 g
  5. 2.25 hours (or 2 hours 15 minutes)

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Learn the conversion factors by heart
  • Remember: area units are squared, volume units are cubed
  • 1 cm³ = 1 mL is very useful for volume problems
  • For time, use fractions: 45 min = 3/4 hour = 0.75 hours
  • Check your answer is sensible - should it be bigger or smaller?
  • Practise both metric-metric and imperial-metric conversions
