G5: Congruence Criteria

Foundation Higher All Boards

Know and use the criteria for congruent triangles: SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS

📋 Key Concepts

Definition: Two shapes are congruent if they have the same shape and size - they can be exactly superimposed on each other.
Important: Congruent shapes have all corresponding sides and angles equal. The areas are also equal.

📝 The Four Congruence Criteria

SSS (Side-Side-Side): If three sides of one triangle are equal to three sides of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
SAS (Side-Angle-Side): If two sides and the included angle of one triangle are equal to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): If two angles and the included side of one triangle are equal to two angles and the included side of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
RHS (Right-angle-Hypotenuse-Side): If the hypotenuse and one side of a right-angled triangle are equal to the hypotenuse and one side of another right-angled triangle, the triangles are congruent.

📝 Using SSS

Example 1

Triangle ABC has sides AB = 5 cm, BC = 7 cm, AC = 4 cm. Triangle DEF has sides DE = 5 cm, EF = 7 cm, DF = 4 cm. Prove the triangles are congruent.

Solution:

AB = DE = 5 cm

BC = EF = 7 cm

AC = DF = 4 cm

All three corresponding sides are equal, so by SSS, triangles ABC and DEF are congruent.

📝 Using SAS

Example 2

In triangles PQR and XYZ: PQ = XY = 6 cm, QR = YZ = 8 cm, and angle PQR = angle XYZ = 45°. Prove the triangles are congruent.

Solution:

PQ = XY = 6 cm (first side)

Angle PQR = Angle XYZ = 45° (included angle)

QR = YZ = 8 cm (second side)

Two sides and the included angle are equal, so by SAS, triangles PQR and XYZ are congruent.

📝 Using ASA

Example 3

In triangles ABC and DEF: AB = DE = 5 cm, angle A = 50°, angle B = 60°, angle D = 50°, angle E = 60°. Prove congruence.

Solution:

Angle A = Angle D = 50°

AB = DE = 5 cm (included side)

Angle B = Angle E = 60°

Two angles and the included side are equal, so by ASA, triangles ABC and DEF are congruent.

📝 Using RHS

Example 4

Triangle ABC is right-angled at B. Triangle DEF is right-angled at E. AC = DF = 10 cm, AB = DE = 6 cm. Prove congruence.

Solution:

Both triangles are right-angled

AC = DF = 10 cm (hypotenuses are equal)

AB = DE = 6 cm (one pair of corresponding sides equal)

By RHS, triangles ABC and DEF are congruent.

📝 Non-Congruence Cases

AAA is NOT a congruence criterion: Knowing three angles only tells you the shape, not the size. The triangles could be similar but different sizes.
ASS is NOT a congruence criterion: The angle must be between the two sides (SAS), not just any angle.
Example 5

Two triangles have angles of 40°, 60°, 80°. Are they congruent?

Solution: Not necessarily. They are similar (same angles) but could have different sizes. We need information about the sides to prove congruence.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What does SSS stand for?

Q2: State the congruence criterion used when you know: two sides and the angle between them.

Q3: Why is AAA not a congruence criterion?

Q4: What does the H stand for in RHS?

Q5: Two right-angled triangles have hypotenuses of 13 cm and one side of 5 cm each. Are they congruent?

✅ Answers

  1. Side-Side-Side
  2. SAS (Side-Angle-Side)
  3. Three equal angles gives similar triangles (same shape), not congruent triangles (same size)
  4. Hypotenuse (the longest side of a right-angled triangle)
  5. Yes, by RHS criterion

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always state the criterion you are using (SSS, SAS, ASA, or RHS)
  • Make sure the angle in SAS is the included angle (between the two sides)
  • Remember: AAA gives similarity, not congruence
  • For RHS, both triangles must be right-angled
  • Write a clear conclusion: "Therefore, triangles ABC and DEF are congruent by [criterion]"
