❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in German about the influence of celebrities.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on the influence of celebrities.

Q3: Translate into German: "I think the influence of celebrities is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass the influence of celebrities wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget to use the correct case after prepositions. Wrong: Er ist berühmt für seinen Stimme. Correct: Er ist berühmt für seine Stimme (für takes accusative; Stimme is feminine → seine).

Detailed Notes

Celebrity Culture and Social Media

Celebrity culture (die Promikultur) in German-speaking countries is heavily influenced by social media. Key vocabulary: der Influencer / die Influencerin (influencer), die Werbekampagne (advertising campaign), die Markenbotschafterin (brand ambassador), die Follower (followers), viral gehen (to go viral), der Trend (trend), nachmachen (to imitate/copy), der Lebensstil (lifestyle). German influencers like Bibi H. (Bibi blocksberg) and Dagi Bee have millions of followers.

In Germany, there is ongoing debate about the influence of social media stars on young people. Critics argue that influencers promote unrealistic beauty standards (unschöne Schönheitsideale) and consumerism (der Konsumismus). Supporters say they provide entertainment and can raise awareness of important issues. In Austria, the Österreichische Werberat (Austrian Advertising Council) requires influencers to clearly label sponsored content (als Werbung kennzeichnen).

For the exam, discuss both sides using einerseits ... andererseits: Einerseits können Influencer junge Menschen inspirieren, andererseits schaffen sie unrealistische Erwartungen (On the one hand influencers can inspire young people, on the other hand they create unrealistic expectations). Use ich bin der Meinung, dass... for your own view.

Beispiel

Einerseits sind Influencer unterhaltsam und können junge Menschen inspirieren. Andererseits vermitteln sie oft ein unrealistisches Bild des Lebens. Ich finde, man sollte kritisch sein.
On the one hand influencers are entertaining and can inspire young people. On the other hand they often convey an unrealistic picture of life. I think one should be critical.

Are Celebrities Good Role Models?

The question Sind Prominente gute Vorbilder? (Are celebrities good role models?) is a classic GCSE discussion topic. Positive arguments: celebrities can inspire achievement (Leistung inspirieren), raise money for charity (Geld für wohltätige Zwecke sammeln), and raise awareness of important issues (auf wichtige Probleme aufmerksam machen). For example, German footballer Mesut Özil donated his World Cup bonus to charity.

Negative arguments: some celebrities set a bad example through bad behaviour (schlechtes Verhalten), promote materialism (den Materialismus fördern), or create pressure to look perfect (Druck, perfekt auszusehen). The German concept of der Schaden (damage/harm) is relevant — some argue that celebrity culture does mehr Schaden als Nutzen (more harm than good) to young people's mental health.

In the DACH countries, there is a cultural preference for substance over style. Germans tend to respect people who have achieved something meaningful (etwas Bedeutsames erreicht haben) rather than those who are simply famous for being famous (berühmt für berühmt sein). This contrasts with some aspects of Anglo-American celebrity culture.

Beispiel

Meiner Meinung nach sind manche Promis gute Vorbilder, zum Beispiel Sportler, die für wohltätige Zwecke spenden. Aber andere Promis sind nur berühmt, weil sie reich sind. Das ist kein gutes Vorbild für Jugendliche.
In my opinion some celebrities are good role models, for example athletes who donate to charity. But other celebrities are only famous because they are rich. That is not a good role model for young people.

German Attitudes to Fame and Privacy

Germans generally value privacy (die Privatsphäre) more highly than people in some other cultures. The concept of die Privatsphäre is legally protected — Germany has some of the strictest privacy laws in the world. Paparazzi photos of celebrities' private lives are more restricted than in the UK or US. The Recht am eigenen Bild (right to one's own image) means you cannot publish someone's photo without their consent.

The German tradition of die Zurückhaltung (restraint/reserve) means that overt self-promotion is sometimes viewed negatively. The phrase Wir sind nicht hier, um uns zu produzieren (We are not here to show off) captures this attitude. In contrast, Austria has a more flamboyant cultural tradition — the Schmäh (Viennese black humour and charm) is a celebrated art form that often involves self-deprecating wit.

In Switzerland, the culture is similarly reserved. Swiss celebrities tend to keep a low profile, and the Swiss media is generally more respectful of privacy than British tabloids. For the exam, comparing German and British attitudes to celebrity privacy demonstrates intercultural understanding.

Beispiel

In Deutschland ist die Privatsphäre sehr wichtig. Es gibt strenge Gesetze gegen Paparazzi. Deutsche Promis sind oft zurückhaltender als britische Promis. Ich finde das besser.
In Germany privacy is very important. There are strict laws against paparazzi. German celebrities are often more reserved than British celebrities. I think that is better.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Haben Prominente einen positiven oder negativen Einfluss auf junge Leute? Gib deine Meinung mit Gründen. (Do celebrities have a positive or negative influence on young people? Give your opinion with reasons.)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: Celebrities have a big influence on young people. On the one hand they can inspire, but on the other hand they promote consumerism. I think one should be critical.

Model Answers

  1. Meiner Meinung nach haben Prominente sowohl einen positiven als auch einen negativen Einfluss. Einerseits können sie junge Menschen inspirieren — zum Beispiel Sportler, die für wohltätige Zwecke spenden und Disziplin zeigen. Andererseits vermitteln viele Influencer auf Social Media ein unrealistisches Bild des Lebens. Sie zeigen nur die schönen Seiten und das kann zu Problemen mit dem Selbstwertgefühl führen. In Deutschland ist die Privatsphäre wichtiger als in Großbritannien, und Promis sind oft zurückhaltender. Ich finde, man sollte kritisch sein und nicht alles nachmachen, was Promis tun.

    (In my opinion celebrities have both a positive and negative influence. On the one hand they can inspire young people — for example athletes who donate to charity and show discipline. On the other hand many influencers on social media convey an unrealistic picture of life. They only show the beautiful sides and that can lead to problems with self-esteem. In Germany privacy is more important than in Great Britain, and celebrities are often more reserved. I think one should be critical and not imitate everything that celebrities do.)
  2. Prominente haben einen großen Einfluss auf junge Leute. Einerseits können sie inspirieren, aber andererseits fördern sie den Konsumismus. Ich finde, man sollte kritisch sein.
