⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often use the present tense instead of the perfect for describing past festivals. Wrong: Letztes Jahr feiere ich Weihnachten zu Hause. Correct: Letztes Jahr habe ich Weihnachten zu Hause gefeiert (perfect tense for completed past actions).

Detailed Notes

Weihnachten: German Christmas Traditions

Weihnachten (Christmas) is the most important festival in Germany, and German Christmas traditions have influenced celebrations worldwide. Key vocabulary: der Weihnachtsbaum (Christmas tree), der Weihnachtsmarkt (Christmas market), der Adventskranz (advent wreath), der Adventskalender (advent calendar), das Christkind (the Christ child — brings presents in southern Germany and Austria), der Weihnachtsmann (Father Christmas — in northern Germany), die Bescherung (the giving of presents on Christmas Eve).

In Germany, Christmas Eve (der Heilige Abend, 24 December) is the main celebration day. Families gather, sing Weihnachtslieder (Christmas carols), and exchange presents (die Bescherung). Many attend die Christmette (midnight mass). The Weihnachtsmarkt tradition is uniquely German — the Nürnberger Christkindlesmarkt and Dresdner Striezelmarkt are among the oldest and most famous. Austrian Christkindlmärkte are equally charming, especially in Salzburg and Vienna.

The Adventskranz (advent wreath) with four candles was invented in Hamburg in 1839. Each Sunday of Advent, one more candle is lit. The Adventskalender (advent calendar) also originated in Germany in the 19th century. In the exam, these cultural details demonstrate excellent knowledge of DACH traditions.

Beispiel

In Deutschland wird Weihnachten am 24. Dezember gefeiert. Am Heiligen Abend gibt es die Bescherung unter dem Weihnachtsbaum. Wir singen Weihnachtslieder und essen Plätzchen.
In Germany Christmas is celebrated on 24 December. On Christmas Eve there is the giving of presents under the Christmas tree. We sing Christmas carols and eat biscuits.

Karneval and Oktoberfest

der Karneval / die Fasching (carnival) is celebrated with enormous enthusiasm in the Rhineland and southern Germany. Key vocabulary: der Umzug (procession), das Kostüm (costume), die Karnevalssitzung (carnival session/show), Kamelle! (sweets thrown from floats), helau! / alaaf! (carnival cries). The Kölner Karneval is the largest in Germany, with over a million participants. In Switzerland, die Fasnacht in Basel is a UNESCO-recognised cultural event, starting at 4 AM on the Monday after Ash Wednesday.

das Oktoberfest in Munich is the world's largest folk festival, running for 16–18 days from mid-September. Over 6 million visitors attend, consuming over 7 million litres of beer. Key foods include: die Brezel (pretzel), das Hendl (roast chicken), die Wurst (sausage), der Steckerlfisch (grilled fish on a stick). Visitors wear traditional costume: der Dirndl for women and die Lederhosen for men.

In Austria, der Krampus tradition on 5 December is unique — men dress as horned demons and parade through streets to frighten naughty children. This contrasts with the friendly Nikolaus (St Nicholas) who visits on 6 December. The Perchtenlauf in the Alpine regions is a similar tradition with wild masks and costumes to drive away winter spirits.

Beispiel

Der Kölner Karneval ist die fünfte Jahreszeit! Die Menschen tragen Kostüme und es gibt große Umzüge mit Wagen, von denen man Kamelle wirft. Man ruft 'Karneval, helau!'
The Cologne Carnival is the fifth season! People wear costumes and there are large processions with floats from which sweets are thrown. People shout 'Karneval, helau!'

Tag der Deutschen Einheit and National Holidays

der Tag der Deutschen Einheit (German Unity Day, 3 October) is Germany's national holiday, commemorating the reunification of Germany in 1990. Unlike other countries, Germany has only one national holiday — all other public holidays (die Feiertage) are determined by individual Bundesländer (federal states). The Bürgerfest (citizens' festival) is held each year in a different host city.

Other important German holidays include: der 1. Mai (Labour Day, 1 May — also Maifeiertag), Ostern (Easter), Pfingsten (Pentecost/Whitsun), der Martinstag (St Martin's Day, 11 November — children carry lanterns in processions), der Heilige Abend (Christmas Eve). In Bavaria and Baden-Württemberg, der Feiertag of Fronleichnam (Corpus Christi) is also observed, but not in Berlin.

In Austria, the Nationalfeiertag (National Day) is 26 October, commemorating the declaration of permanent neutrality in 1955. Switzerland's Bundesfeier (National Day) is 1 August, celebrating the founding of the Swiss Confederation in 1291. On this day, Bundesfeuer (bonfires) and fireworks are common. Understanding the different national holidays of the DACH countries shows strong cultural awareness for the exam.

Beispiel

Der Tag der Deutschen Einheit am 3. Oktober ist der einzige nationale Feiertag in Deutschland. Er erinnert an die Wiedervereinigung im Jahr 1990. Es gibt ein großes Bürgerfest.
German Unity Day on 3 October is the only national holiday in Germany. It commemorates reunification in 1990. There is a large citizens' festival.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Beschreibe ein deutsches Fest, das du kennst. Was macht man dort und warum ist es wichtig? (Describe a German festival you know. What do people do there and why is it important?)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: Christmas is the most important festival in Germany. On Christmas Eve, families exchange presents under the Christmas tree. The advent wreath has four candles.

Model Answers

  1. Ich kenne das Oktoberfest in München sehr gut. Es ist das größte Volksfest der Welt und findet jedes Jahr im September statt. Über sechs Millionen Besucher kommen dorthin. Man trägt oft traditionelle Kleidung wie Lederhosen und Dirndl. Man isst Brezeln und Hähnchen und trinkt Bier aus einem Maßkrug. Es gibt auch Achterbahnen und andere Fahrgeschäfte. Ich finde das Oktoberfest wichtig, weil es eine alte bayerische Tradition ist und die Kultur des Landes zeigt. Ähnliche Feste gibt es auch in Österreich und der Schweiz.

    (I know the Oktoberfest in Munich very well. It is the largest folk festival in the world and takes place every year in September. Over six million visitors come there. People often wear traditional clothing like Lederhosen and Dirndl. You eat pretzels and roast chicken and drink beer from a litre mug. There are also rollercoasters and other rides. I think the Oktoberfest is important because it is an old Bavarian tradition and shows the culture of the country. Similar festivals also exist in Austria and Switzerland.)
  2. Weihnachten ist das wichtigste Fest in Deutschland. Am Heiligen Abend tauschen die Familien Geschenke unter dem Weihnachtsbaum aus. Der Adventskranz hat vier Kerzen.
