❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in German about food and drink culture.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on food and drink culture.

Q3: Translate into German: "I think food and drink culture is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass food and drink culture wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often use the present tense instead of the perfect for describing past festivals. Wrong: Letztes Jahr feiere ich Weihnachten zu Hause. Correct: Letztes Jahr habe ich Weihnachten zu Hause gefeiert (perfect tense for completed past actions).

Detailed Notes

Traditional German Food and Drink

German food culture is rich and regionally diverse. Key vocabulary: die Bratwurst (grilled sausage), der Sauerkraut (fermented cabbage), der Kartoffelsalat (potato salad), die Brezel (pretzel), der Kuchen (cake), der Apfelstrudel (apple strudel), das Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte (Black Forest gateau), der Senf (mustard), das Bier (beer), der Wein (wine), der Schnaps (spirit/liquor). Each region has specialities: die Weißwurst in Bavaria, der Labskaus in Hamburg, der Sauerbraten in the Rhineland.

The Wirtshaus (inn/tavern) is a traditional German dining institution where locals gather for hearty food and beer. The Biergarten (beer garden) tradition started in Bavaria — originally, brewers planted chestnut trees over their underground cellars to keep the beer cool. Today, Biergärten are social spaces where families and friends meet. In Austria, the Heuriger (wine tavern) is a similar tradition where winemakers serve their own wine and local food.

Breakfast in Germany (das Frühstück) typically includes fresh bread rolls (die Brötchen or die Semmeln in Austria), butter, cold meats (der Aufschnitt), cheese, jam and boiled eggs. On Sundays, many families enjoy a special breakfast with croissants and fresh orange juice. The Kaffee und Kuchen (coffee and cake) tradition in the afternoon is similar to British afternoon tea and is especially strong in northern Germany.

Beispiel

Typisch deutsch ist das Frühstück mit frischen Brötchen, Käse und Aufschnitt. Am Nachmittag gibt es Kaffee und Kuchen. In Bayern isst man Weißwurst mit süßem Senf.
Typical German is breakfast with fresh rolls, cheese and cold meats. In the afternoon there is coffee and cake. In Bavaria people eat white sausage with sweet mustard.

Regional Specialities Across the DACH Countries

Each DACH region has unique culinary traditions. In northern Germany, seafood is popular: der Fischbrötchen (fish sandwich) and der Hering (herring). In Bavaria, der Knödel (dumpling) — either Kartoffelknödel (potato) or Semmelknödel (bread) — accompanies many dishes. In Swabia, die Maultasche (similar to ravioli) is the regional speciality, sometimes called Herrgottsbescheißerle (little God-cheaters, because they hid meat during Lent).

Austrian cuisine is heavily influenced by the former Austro-Hungarian Empire. der Wiener Schnitzel (breaded veal cutlet) is the national dish, traditionally made with veal (Kalbsschnitzel) though pork versions are common. der Apfelstrudel and die Sachertorte (chocolate cake with apricot jam) are world-famous desserts. The Tafelspitz (boiled veal) was reportedly Emperor Franz Joseph's favourite dish.

Swiss food centres on cheese and potatoes. das Käsefondue (melted cheese dip) and die Raclette (melted cheese over potatoes) are iconic winter dishes. die Rösti (grated and fried potato) is a staple, and das Zürcher Geschnetzelte (veal in cream sauce) is a Zurich classic. Swiss chocolate brands like Lindt, Toblerone and Sprüngli are globally recognised.

Beispiel

In Schwaben sind Maultaschen eine Spezialität. In Österreich isst man Wiener Schnitzel und Sachertorte. In der Schweiz ist Käsefondue im Winter sehr beliebt.
In Swabia Maultaschen are a speciality. In Austria people eat Wiener Schnitzel and Sachertorte. In Switzerland cheese fondue is very popular in winter.

Food Etiquette and Table Customs

German dining etiquette has specific customs. Before eating, everyone says Guten Appetit (enjoy your meal) or simply Mahlzeit (literally "mealtime" — used as a lunch greeting, especially in workplaces). It is polite to wait until everyone has been served before starting. Keep your hands on the table (not in your lap) — this comes from the medieval tradition of showing you were not holding a weapon.

When toasting, say Prost! (cheers!) with beer or Zum Wohl! (to your health!) with wine. Crucially, you must make eye contact when clinking glasses — failing to do so is said to bring seven years of bad luck (sieben Jahre Pech). This superstition is taken surprisingly seriously in Germany. Another rule: never cross arms when clinking glasses with a group.

Tipping in Germany is different from the UK. A service charge is usually included, but it is customary to round up the bill (aufrunden). For example, if the bill is €28.50, you might say dreißig (thirty) and the server keeps the change. You do not leave the tip on the table — instead, you tell the server the total amount you wish to pay when they come to collect payment.

Beispiel

Beim Essen sagt man 'Guten Appetit'. Beim Anstoßen muss man Augenkontakt halten, sonst hat man sieben Jahre Pech! Das Trinkgeld rundet man auf — man sagt einfach 'dreißig Euro, bitte'.
At mealtimes one says 'Guten Appetit'. When clinking glasses one must maintain eye contact, otherwise one has seven years of bad luck! One rounds up the tip — one simply says 'thirty euros, please'.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Beschreibe die Esskultur in Deutschland und vergleiche sie mit der in Großbritannien. (Describe the food culture in Germany and compare it with that in Great Britain.)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: At breakfast I eat bread rolls with cheese. When toasting in Germany, you must make eye contact. The Sacher torte comes from Vienna and is world-famous.

Model Answers

  1. Die deutsche Esskultur ist sehr vielfältig. Zum Frühstück isst man frische Brötchen mit Käse und Aufschnitt, während man in England oft Toast mit Baked Beans isst. In Deutschland gibt es die Tradition von Kaffee und Kuchen am Nachmittag, was es in England als Afternoon Tea gibt. Beim Essen sagt man 'Guten Appetit' und beim Anstoßen 'Prost' — man muss Augenkontakt halten! In Bayern gibt es Biergärten, wo Familien zusammen essen und trinken. In Österreich ist die Kaffeehauskultur sehr wichtig. Ich finde die deutsche Esskultur sehr gesellig, weil das Essen oft ein soziales Ereignis ist.

    (The German food culture is very diverse. For breakfast one eats fresh rolls with cheese and cold meats, whereas in England one often eats toast with baked beans. In Germany there is the tradition of coffee and cake in the afternoon, which exists in England as afternoon tea. At mealtimes one says 'Guten Appetit' and when toasting 'Prost' — one must maintain eye contact! In Bavaria there are beer gardens where families eat and drink together. In Austria the coffee house culture is very important. I find the German food culture very sociable because eating is often a social event.)
  2. Zum Frühstück esse ich Brötchen mit Käse. Beim Anstoßen in Deutschland muss man Augenkontakt halten. Die Sachertorte kommt aus Wien und ist weltberühmt.
