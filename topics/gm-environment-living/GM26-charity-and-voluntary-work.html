❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in German about charity and voluntary work.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on charity and voluntary work.

Q3: Translate into German: "I think charity and voluntary work is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass charity and voluntary work wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often use the wrong case after prepositions. Wrong: In meiner Stadt gibt es einen Park (in + dative for location is correct). Correct: In meiner Stadt gibt es einen Park — in takes DATIVE for location. BUT: Ich gehe in die Stadt — in takes ACCUSATIVE for movement towards.

Detailed Notes

Charity and Volunteering in DACH Countries

Charity and volunteering (die Wohltätigkeit und das Ehrenamt) are important topics in GCSE German. Key vocabulary: die Wohltätigkeit (charity), die Spende (donation), spenden (to donate), das Ehrenamt (voluntary work), ehrenamtlich arbeiten (to volunteer), der Freiwillige / die Freiwillige (volunteer), die Hilfsorganisation (aid organisation), der_obdachlose (homeless person), das Tierheim (animal shelter), die Suppenküche (soup kitchen).

Volunteering (das Ehrenamt) is deeply rooted in German society. Over 30% of Germans do some form of voluntary work, often through der Verein (club/association). The Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr (FSJ — voluntary social year) allows young people aged 16–27 to spend a year working in social, ecological or cultural organisations while receiving a small allowance. This is a uniquely German institution — there is no exact equivalent in the UK.

Major German charities include die Caritas (Catholic charity), das Diakonische Werk (Protestant charity), das Rote Kreuz (Red Cross), and UNICEF Deutschland. In Austria, die Volkshilfe and die Caritas are prominent. In Switzerland, die Caritas and das Schweizerische Rote Kreuz play similar roles. Understanding these organisations shows cultural knowledge for the exam.

Beispiel

In Deutschland machen über 30 Prozent der Menschen ehrenamtlich mit. Das Freiwillige Soziale Jahr ist eine gute Möglichkeit für junge Leute, Erfahrungen zu sammeln und anderen zu helfen.
In Germany over 30% of people do voluntary work. The Voluntary Social Year is a good opportunity for young people to gain experience and help others.

The Freiwilliges Soziales Year and Other Programmes

The Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr (FSJ) and Freiwilliges Ökologisches Jahr (FÖJ — voluntary ecological year) are important German social programmes. They allow young people to spend 6–18 months working in areas like healthcare, education, environmental protection or culture. Participants receive Taschengeld (pocket money), accommodation and social insurance. Over 30,000 young Germans participate annually.

The Bundesfreiwilligendienst (BFD — Federal Voluntary Service) is similar but available to all ages, not just young people. It was created in 2011 to replace the former Zivildienst (civilian service), which was compulsory for men who objected to military service. The end of conscription in 2011 meant the Zivildienst also ended, and the BFD was created as a voluntary replacement.

In Austria, the Gedenkdienst (Holocaust memorial service) is unique — young Austrians can do their alternative civilian service at Holocaust memorials worldwide instead of military service. This programme reflects Austria's commitment to remembering and atoning for its Nazi past. In Switzerland, the Zivildienst (civilian service) is still an alternative to military service for conscientious objectors.

Beispiel

Das Freiwillige Soziale Jahr ist eine gute Möglichkeit für junge Menschen. Man arbeitet in einer sozialen Einrichtung und bekommt Taschengeld. Es ist eine sinnvolle Erfahrung.
The Voluntary Social Year is a good opportunity for young people. You work in a social institution and receive pocket money. It is a meaningful experience.

Expressing Opinions About Voluntary Work

For the exam, express and justify opinions about charity and volunteering. Positive views: Ich finde ehrenamtliche Arbeit wichtig, weil man anderen Menschen helfen kann (I find voluntary work important because you can help other people). Es gibt einem ein gutes Gefühl (It gives you a good feeling). Man sammelt wertvolle Lebenserfahrung (You gain valuable life experience).

Counter-arguments: Manche Jugendliche haben keine Zeit für Ehrenamt, weil sie für die Schule lernen müssen (Some young people have no time for volunteering because they must study for school). Die Bezahlung ist sehr niedrig (The pay is very low). Es ist schwer, einen Platz zu bekommen (It is hard to get a place).

For Higher tier, use complex structures: Obwohl das FSJ nicht gut bezahlt ist, ist es eine wertvolle Erfahrung, weil man neue Fähigkeiten lernt und gleichzeitig der Gesellschaft hilft (Although the FSJ is not well paid, it is a valuable experience because you learn new skills and at the same time help society). The structure obwohl + subordinate clause + main clause demonstrates high-level grammar.

Beispiel

Obwohl ehrenamtliche Arbeit nicht bezahlt wird, finde ich sie sehr wichtig. Man lernt neue Fähigkeiten und hilft anderen. Ich möchte nächstes Jahr ein FSJ im Tierheim machen.
Although voluntary work is not paid, I find it very important. You learn new skills and help others. I would like to do an FSJ in the animal shelter next year.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Warum ist ehrenamtliche Arbeit wichtig? Was würdest du gern machen? (Why is voluntary work important? What would you like to do?)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: I think voluntary work is important because you help other people. In Germany, many people volunteer for clubs. I would like to do a Voluntary Social Year after school.

Model Answers

  1. Ich finde ehrenamtliche Arbeit sehr wichtig, weil man anderen Menschen helfen und gleichzeitig wertvolle Erfahrungen sammeln kann. In Deutschland machen über 30 Prozent der Bevölkerung ehrenamtlich mit. Ich würde gern im Tierheim arbeiten, weil ich Tiere liebe. Ich möchte auch Hunde ausführen und beim Füttern helfen. Nach dem Abitur möchte ich ein Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr machen, bevor ich an die Universität gehe. Obwohl man nicht viel Geld bekommt, ist es eine sinnvolle Erfahrung, die einem hilft, persönlich zu wachsen.

    (I find voluntary work very important because you can help other people and at the same time gain valuable experience. In Germany over 30% of the population does voluntary work. I would like to work in the animal shelter because I love animals. I also want to walk dogs and help with feeding. After my Abitur I would like to do a Voluntary Social Year before I go to university. Although one does not get much money, it is a meaningful experience that helps you to grow personally.)
  2. Ich finde ehrenamtliche Arbeit wichtig, weil man anderen Menschen hilft. In Deutschland machen viele Menschen ehrenamtlich in Vereinen mit. Ich möchte nach der Schule ein Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr machen.
