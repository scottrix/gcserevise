GM30: Word Order and Conjunctions

Foundation Higher All Boards

Main clause word order; subordinate clauses (verb to end); coordinating vs subordinating conjunctions; dass, weil, wenn, obwohl

Main Clause Word Order

In German main clauses, the verb is always in second position (V2 rule):

  • Subject first: Ich gehe heute ins Kino.
  • Time first: Heute gehe ich ins Kino. (verb still second, subject after verb)
  • Other element first: Ins Kino gehe ich heute.
Key Fact: Whatever comes first in the sentence, the conjugated verb MUST be second. If something other than the subject comes first, the subject swaps places with the verb (verb-subject inversion).

Subordinating Conjunctions

Subordinating conjunctions send the verb to the end of the clause:

ConjunctionMeaningExample
weilbecauseIch bleibe zu Hause, weil ich krank bin
dassthatIch denke, dass Deutsch wichtig ist
wennwhen/ifWenn ich Zeit habe, gehe ich schwimmen
obwohlalthoughObwohl es regnet, gehen wir aus
währendwhile/duringWährend ich esse, lese ich
obwhether/ifIch weiß nicht, ob er kommt
dasince/becauseDa ich müde bin, gehe ich ins Bett

Word Order After Subordinate Clauses

Exam Tip: When a subordinate clause comes FIRST in the sentence, the main clause that follows has VERB-FIRST-SUBJECT order: "Weil ich krank bin, bleibe ich zu Hause" (NOT "ich bleibe"). The subordinate clause counts as position 1, so the verb of the main clause must be in position 2.

Coordinating Conjunctions

Coordinating conjunctions do NOT change word order (verb stays second):

  • und (and), oder (or), aber (but), denn (because)
  • Ich bin müde, aber ich muss arbeiten. (normal word order after aber)
  • Compare: Ich bin müde, weil ich viel arbeiten muss. (verb to end after weil)

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Rewrite with correct word order: Ich denke (dass / Deutsch / wichtig / sein). Ich bleibe zu Hause (weil / ich / krank / sein).

Q: Rewrite starting with the subordinate clause: Ich gehe nicht aus, weil das Wetter schlecht ist. → Weil...

✅ Answers

  1. Ich denke, dass Deutsch wichtig ist. Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil ich krank bin. (Verb goes to the END after dass and weil.)
  2. Weil das Wetter schlecht ist, gehe ich nicht aus. (Subordinate clause first = position 1; main clause verb must be in position 2 = gehe ich, NOT ich gehe.)

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

📝 Exam Technique

German Exam Tips:
For grammar: Show you understand the rules by applying them accurately. Use a range of tenses (at least 3) to access higher marks. Check cases (nominative, accusative, dative). Get word order right — verb second in main clauses, verb at end in subordinate clauses. Include complex structures. Accuracy in grammar is worth up to 40% of writing and speaking marks.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget word order in subordinate clauses. Wrong: Weil ich krank bin, ich bleibe zu Hause. Correct: Weil ich krank bin, bleibe ich zu Hause (in the MAIN clause after a subordinate clause, the verb comes FIRST, then the subject).

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Writing task: Write about main clause word order; subordinate clauses (verb to end); coordinating vs subordinating conjunctions; dass, weil, wenn, obwohl.

A grade 9 answer will: use at least three tenses accurately; include complex structures (subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs); show precise grammar (cases, word order, adjective endings); express and justify opinions; include cultural knowledge about DACH countries; use a wide range of vocabulary; be well-organised with connecting words (außerdem, jedoch, trotzdem, zusammenfassend).

Mark scheme: Content (relevance and detail) + Quality of language (accuracy and range) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE German tests four equally-weighted AOs: AO1 Listening (25%) — understand spoken German across all three themes; AO2 Reading (25%) — understand written German including translation; AO3 Speaking (25%) — communicate effectively in conversation and role-play; AO4 Writing (25%) — produce accurate German including translation into German. For grade 9, use a wide range of vocabulary and complex structures, demonstrate consistent accuracy across all tenses and cases, and include cultural knowledge. The difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the range and accuracy of language — including correct case usage, word order and verb placement.
