GM32: Separable and Modal Verbs

Foundation Higher All Boards

Separable verb prefixes (an-, aus-, mit-, zu-); modal verbs (können, müssen, dürfen, sollen, wollen, möchten); word order with modals

Separable Verbs

Many German verbs have a prefix that separates from the verb and goes to the end of the clause:

VerbMeaningPresentPerfect
anfangento start/beginIch fange um 8 Uhr anIch habe angefangen
ausgehento go outIch gehe heute ausIch bin ausgegangen
mitkommento come alongKommst du mit?Bist du mitgekommen?
zurückkommento come backIch komme morgen zurückIch bin zurückgekommen
aufstehento get upIch stehe um 7 Uhr aufIch bin aufgestanden
einkaufento shopIch kaufe im Supermarkt einIch habe eingekauft
Key Fact: Common separable prefixes: an-, aus-, auf-, ein-, mit-, nach-, vor-, zu-, zurück-, zusammen-, weg-. In subordinate clauses, the prefix stays attached: "...weil ich um 8 Uhr anfange" (NOT anfange ich).

Modal Verbs

Modal verbs express ability, obligation, permission or desire. They are conjugated and followed by an infinitive at the end:

könnencan / to be ablekannkannstkannkönnen

Exam Tip: "Ich möchte" is technically the subjunctive of mögen but functions as a modal verb. It is the standard way to express "I would like" in German. Note: ich kann, ich muss, ich darf, ich will, ich soll — these all have NO -e ending in the ich form.

❓ Practice Questions

Q: Rewrite with correct word order: Ich (einkaufen / gehen / am Samstag).

Q: Translate: I must do my homework. I would like to go to the cinema.

✅ Answers

  1. Ich gehe am Samstag einkaufen. (The separable prefix "ein-" goes to the END of the clause: ich gehe... ein.)
  2. Ich muss meine Hausaufgaben machen. (Modal + infinitive at end.) Ich möchte ins Kino gehen. (Modal + infinitive at end.)

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

📝 Exam Technique

German Exam Tips:
For grammar: Show you understand the rules by applying them accurately. Use a range of tenses (at least 3) to access higher marks. Check cases (nominative, accusative, dative). Get word order right — verb second in main clauses, verb at end in subordinate clauses. Include complex structures. Accuracy in grammar is worth up to 40% of writing and speaking marks.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget word order in subordinate clauses. Wrong: Weil ich krank bin, ich bleibe zu Hause. Correct: Weil ich krank bin, bleibe ich zu Hause (in the MAIN clause after a subordinate clause, the verb comes FIRST, then the subject).

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Writing task: Write about separable verb prefixes (an-, aus-, mit-, zu-); modal verbs (können, müssen, dürfen, sollen, wollen, möchten); word order with modals.

A grade 9 answer will: use at least three tenses accurately; include complex structures (subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs); show precise grammar (cases, word order, adjective endings); express and justify opinions; include cultural knowledge about DACH countries; use a wide range of vocabulary; be well-organised with connecting words (außerdem, jedoch, trotzdem, zusammenfassend).

Mark scheme: Content (relevance and detail) + Quality of language (accuracy and range) = total marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE German tests four equally-weighted AOs: AO1 Listening (25%) — understand spoken German across all three themes; AO2 Reading (25%) — understand written German including translation; AO3 Speaking (25%) — communicate effectively in conversation and role-play; AO4 Writing (25%) — produce accurate German including translation into German. For grade 9, use a wide range of vocabulary and complex structures, demonstrate consistent accuracy across all tenses and cases, and include cultural knowledge. The difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the range and accuracy of language — including correct case usage, word order and verb placement.
