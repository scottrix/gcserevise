❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in German about describing yourself and others.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on describing yourself and others.

Q3: Translate into German: "I think describing yourself and others is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass describing yourself and others wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

📝 Exam Technique

German Exam Tips:
For identity and relationships: Use correct adjective endings (der/die/das + adjective). Use possessive adjectives correctly (mein/meine, dein/deine). Use reflexive verbs with correct pronouns (ich wasche mich, ich freue mich). Express opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil...). Use a range of tenses.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget adjective endings. Wrong: Ich bin groß und dünn (no ending after bin). Correct: Ich bin groß und dünn — predicate adjectives have NO ending after sein/werden/bleiben. BUT: Der große Mann — attributive adjectives BEFORE nouns DO need endings.

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE German tests four equally-weighted AOs: AO1 Listening (25%) — understand spoken German across all three themes; AO2 Reading (25%) — understand written German including translation; AO3 Speaking (25%) — communicate effectively in conversation and role-play; AO4 Writing (25%) — produce accurate German including translation into German. For grade 9, use a wide range of vocabulary and complex structures, demonstrate consistent accuracy across all tenses and cases, and include cultural knowledge. The difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the range and accuracy of language — including correct case usage, word order and verb placement.

Detailed Notes

Physical and Character Descriptions

To describe yourself and others in GCSE German, you need a solid range of adjectives and the correct verb forms. For physical appearance, common adjectives include groß (tall), klein (short), schlank (slim), lockiges Haar (curly hair) and blonde Haare (blonde hair). Remember that German nouns have genders, so you must use the correct article: der Mann, die Frau, das Kind. After the verb sein (to be), adjectives are predicate adjectives and have no ending: Ich bin freundlich (I am friendly).

For character descriptions, useful adjectives include freundlich (friendly), lustig (funny), schüchtern (shy), intelligent, geduldig (patient) and egoistisch (selfish). In southern Germany and Austria, people are often described as gesellig (sociable), reflecting the strong café and Wirtshaus (inn) culture. In Switzerland, the concept of Grüezi (a friendly greeting) reflects a polite and reserved character.

When placing an adjective before a noun (attributive position), you must add endings that agree with the noun's gender, number and case. For example: ein kleiner Hund (a small dog), eine schöne Frau (a beautiful woman), ein großes Haus (a big house). The endings change depending on whether you use der/die/das (strong ending) or ein/eine (weak ending).

Beispiel

Meine Schwester ist groß und schlank. Sie hat lange braune Haare und grüne Augen. Sie ist sehr freundlich und hilfsbereit, aber manchmal ein bisschen ungeduldig.
My sister is tall and slim. She has long brown hair and green eyes. She is very friendly and helpful, but sometimes a bit impatient.

Expressing Opinions About People

At GCSE level, expressing and justifying opinions about people is essential for both the speaking and writing exams. Use ich finde (I find/think) + accusative, or meiner Meinung nach (in my opinion) to introduce your view. Always follow with a reason using weil (because) or denn (because) — note that weil sends the verb to the end, while denn keeps normal word order.

To compare people, use the comparative form: add -er to the adjective and use als (than). For example: Ich bin größer als mein Bruder (I am taller than my brother). The superlative uses am ... -sten: Sie ist am Intelligentesten (She is the most intelligent). Some adjectives have irregular forms: gut → besser → am besten, viel → mehr → am meisten.

In German-speaking cultures, directness is often valued more than in British culture. A German speaker might say Ich finde ihn unmöglich (I find him impossible) without it being considered rude. This cultural difference is useful to mention in speaking exams to show intercultural understanding.

Beispiel

Ich finde meinen besten Freund sehr zuverlässig, weil er immer für mich da ist, wenn ich Probleme habe. Er ist auch lustiger als andere Leute in meiner Klasse.
I find my best friend very reliable because he is always there for me when I have problems. He is also funnier than other people in my class.

Using Reflexive Verbs for Self-Description

Reflexive verbs are frequently used when talking about yourself and daily routines. In German, reflexive verbs require a reflexive pronoun (mich, dich, sich, uns, euch, sich) that matches the subject. Common reflexive verbs for this topic include: sich vorstellen (to introduce oneself), sich freuen (to be pleased), sich langweilen (to be bored), sich interessieren (to be interested) and sich ärgern (to be annoyed).

The accusative reflexive pronouns are used for most situations: Ich freue mich (I am pleased), Du langweilst dich (You are bored). However, some verbs take the dative reflexive pronoun when another accusative object is present: Ich stelle mir vor (I imagine), Ich wasche mir die Hände (I wash my hands — dative because die Hände is accusative).

For the exam, using reflexive verbs correctly demonstrates grammatical range. Try to include at least one reflexive verb in your speaking or writing responses. For Higher tier, combine with subordinating conjunctions: Wenn ich mich vorstelle, sage ich immer... (When I introduce myself, I always say...).

Beispiel

Ich interessiere mich für Musik und Sport. Am Wochenende freue ich mich darauf, mit meinen Freunden auszugehen. Manchmal langweile ich mich zu Hause.
I am interested in music and sport. At the weekend I look forward to going out with my friends. Sometimes I get bored at home.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Schreib einen kurzen Absatz über dich selbst und eine andere Person aus deiner Familie. Beschreibe das Aussehen und den Charakter. (Write a short paragraph about yourself and another person in your family. Describe appearance and character.)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: My best friend is called Lukas. He is taller than me and has green eyes. I find him very reliable because he always helps me. Sometimes he can be serious, but he is also the funniest person I know.

Model Answers

  1. Ich bin mittelgroß und schlank. Ich habe kurze braune Haare und braune Augen. Ich bin ziemlich freundlich und lustig, aber manchmal bin ich auch ein bisschen ungeduldig. Mein Bruder ist größer als ich und er hat lange Haare. Er ist schüchtern, aber sehr intelligent und hilfsbereit. Meiner Meinung nach sind wir beide ganz unterschiedlich, aber wir verstehen uns gut.

    (I am medium-height and slim. I have short brown hair and brown eyes. I am quite friendly and funny, but sometimes I am also a bit impatient. My brother is taller than me and he has long hair. He is shy but very intelligent and helpful. In my opinion we are both quite different, but we get on well.)
  2. Mein bester Freund heißt Lukas. Er ist größer als ich und hat grüne Augen. Ich finde ihn sehr zuverlässig, weil er mir immer hilft. Manchmal kann er ernst sein, aber er ist auch die lustigste Person, die ich kenne.
