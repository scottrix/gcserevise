❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in German about family and friends.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on family and friends.

Q3: Translate into German: "I think family and friends is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass family and friends wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil... / meiner Meinung nach...)
  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

📝 Exam Technique

German Exam Tips:
For identity and relationships: Use correct adjective endings (der/die/das + adjective). Use possessive adjectives correctly (mein/meine, dein/deine). Use reflexive verbs with correct pronouns (ich wasche mich, ich freue mich). Express opinions with reasons (ich finde... weil...). Use a range of tenses.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget adjective endings. Wrong: Ich bin groß und dünn (no ending after bin). Correct: Ich bin groß und dünn — predicate adjectives have NO ending after sein/werden/bleiben. BUT: Der große Mann — attributive adjectives BEFORE nouns DO need endings.

Detailed Notes

Family Vocabulary and Possessive Adjectives

To talk about family at GCSE level, you need a wide range of family vocabulary and the correct possessive adjectives. Key family terms include: die Mutter (mother), der Vater (father), der Bruder (brother), die Schwester (sister), die Großeltern (grandparents), der Onkel (uncle), die Tante (aunt), der Cousin / die Cousine (cousin). Note that in German, der Cousin is masculine and die Cousine is feminine — German makes this gender distinction.

Possessive adjectives in German must agree with the noun that follows them, just like ein/eine. The main possessive adjectives are: mein/meine (my), dein/deine (your, informal), sein/seine (his), ihr/ihre (her), unser/unsere (our), euer/eure (your, plural informal), Ihr/Ihre (your, formal). For example: mein Bruder (my brother — masculine), meine Schwester (my sister — feminine), mein Kind (my child — neuter).

German family structure has changed significantly in recent decades. In modern Germany, blended families (die Patchwork-Familie) are common, and many couples choose not to marry. In Austria and Switzerland, similar trends exist, though rural areas tend to maintain more traditional family structures. Understanding these cultural differences can earn marks in the speaking exam.

Beispiel

Meine Mutter heißt Anna und mein Vater heißt Thomas. Ich habe einen älteren Bruder und eine jüngere Schwester. Unsere Großeltern wohnen in Bayern.
My mother is called Anna and my father is called Thomas. I have an older brother and a younger sister. Our grandparents live in Bavaria.

Describing Relationships

To describe how you get on with family and friends, use verbs like auskommen mit (to get on with), streiten (to argue), verstehen (to understand) and vertrauen (to trust). The verb auskommen is a separable verb: Ich komme gut mit meiner Schwester aus (I get on well with my sister). The prefix aus goes to the end of the clause in a main sentence.

Useful phrases for relationships include: sich gut verstehen (to get on well), sich streiten um (to argue about), einen Streit haben (to have an argument), enge Freunde sein (to be close friends). To express contrast, use aber (but) or obwohl (although): Obwohl wir uns oft streiten, kommen wir gut miteinander aus (Although we often argue, we get on well with each other).

In Germany, the concept of die Familie is often more nuclear than in some cultures. Many young Germans leave home at 18 to study or work. In Austria, the Familienbande (family ties) can be stronger, especially in rural areas. In Switzerland, multilingual families are common due to the four national languages (German, French, Italian, Romansh).

Beispiel

Ich komme gut mit meiner Mutter aus, weil sie sehr verständnisvoll ist. Aber mit meinem Bruder streite ich mich oft um den Computer.
I get on well with my mother because she is very understanding. But I often argue with my brother about the computer.

Talking About Friendship

At GCSE level, you should be able to describe friendships in detail using a range of tenses. Use the present tense for general statements: Wir treffen uns jeden Freitag (We meet every Friday). Use the perfect tense for past events: Wir haben uns letztes Jahr kennengelernt (We met last year). Use the future tense for plans: Wir werden zusammen in den Urlaub fahren (We will go on holiday together).

Important friendship vocabulary includes: der Freund / die Freundin (friend — note: mein Freund can mean boyfriend), der Kumpel (mate/pal — informal), der Bekannte / die Bekannte (acquaintance), die Freundschaft (friendship), der Klassenkamerad (classmate). To distinguish between a platonic friend and a boyfriend/girlfriend, Germans often say ein Freund von mir (a friend of mine) vs mein Freund (my boyfriend).

In the DACH countries, friendship norms differ from the UK. Germans tend to have fewer but deeper friendships (enge Freundschaften). Making friends takes longer, but friendships are typically very loyal. In Austria, the Stammtisch tradition — a regular table at a pub where friends meet weekly — is an important social institution.

Beispiel

Mein bester Freund und ich kennen uns seit der Grundschule. Wir treffen uns jeden Samstag im Park und spielen Fußball zusammen. Letzten Sommer sind wir nach München gefahren.
My best friend and I have known each other since primary school. We meet every Saturday in the park and play football together. Last summer we went to Munich.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Beschreibe deine Familie und deine Beziehung zu jedem Familienmitglied. (Describe your family and your relationship with each family member.)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: I get on well with my sister, although we sometimes argue about clothes. My best friend is called Sophie and we have known each other for five years.

Model Answers

  1. Ich lebe mit meiner Mutter, meinem Vater und meinem jüngeren Bruder in einem Haus in Berlin. Ich komme sehr gut mit meiner Mutter aus, weil sie geduldig und verständnisvoll ist. Mit meinem Vater habe ich manchmal Streit, weil er streng sein kann, aber ich respektiere ihn. Mein Bruder und ich streiten uns oft, aber wir sind trotzdem enge Freunde. Meine Großeltern wohnen in Österreich und wir besuchen sie jedes Jahr zu Weihnachten.

    (I live with my mother, father and younger brother in a house in Berlin. I get on very well with my mother because she is patient and understanding. I sometimes argue with my father because he can be strict, but I respect him. My brother and I often argue, but we are still close friends. My grandparents live in Austria and we visit them every year at Christmas.)
  2. Ich komme gut mit meiner Schwester aus, obwohl wir uns manchmal um Kleidung streiten. Meine beste Freundin heißt Sophie und wir kennen uns seit fünf Jahren.
