✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary with correct case endings, verb position and adjective endings. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph: Meiner Meinung nach... (in my opinion), ich finde... weil... (I think... because...), aber/allerdings... (however), zusammenfassend... (in summary). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Ich finde, dass marriage and partnerships wichtig ist, weil..." — ensure correct word order (verb at end after dass), correct case endings, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks

  • Check case endings (nominative, accusative, dative) before finishing
  • Get word order right: verb-second in main clauses, verb-at-end after dass/weil/wenn
  • Use complex structures: subordinate clauses, modal verbs, separable verbs
  • Include cultural references to German-speaking countries (DACH)

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget adjective endings. Wrong: Ich bin groß und dünn (no ending after bin). Correct: Ich bin groß und dünn — predicate adjectives have NO ending after sein/werden/bleiben. BUT: Der große Mann — attributive adjectives BEFORE nouns DO need endings.

Detailed Notes

Talking About Marriage and Relationships

At GCSE level, you need vocabulary for discussing marriage, partnerships and relationships. Key terms include: die Ehe (marriage), der Ehepartner / die Ehepartnerin (spouse), heiraten (to marry), sich verloben (to get engaged), die Verlobung (the engagement), sich scheiden lassen (to get divorced), die Scheidung (the divorce), der Partner / die Partnerin (partner).

In modern Germany, many couples live together without marrying — this is called die eheähnliche Gemeinschaft or simply zusammenleben (living together). Germany introduced die eingetragene Lebenspartnerschaft (registered civil partnership) for same-sex couples in 2001, and same-sex marriage (die Ehe für alle) was legalised in 2017. Austria legalised same-sex marriage in 2019, and Switzerland followed in 2022.

When discussing relationships in the exam, use a range of tenses. Present for general statements: Viele junge Leute heiraten später (Many young people marry later). Perfect for past: Sie haben im letzten Jahr geheiratet (They got married last year). Future for plans: Wir werden nächstes Jahr heiraten (We will get married next year).

Beispiel

Meine Eltern haben vor zwanzig Jahren geheiratet. Sie haben eine glückliche Ehe. In Deutschland heiraten viele Leute heute später als früher.
My parents got married twenty years ago. They have a happy marriage. In Germany many people today marry later than before.

Wedding Traditions in DACH Countries

German wedding traditions are rich and varied, making excellent cultural content for the exam. A famous tradition is Polterabend — the evening before the wedding, guests smash old porcelain and ceramics outside the couple's door to bring good luck. The couple must then clean up the pieces together, symbolising teamwork. Another tradition is Baumstamm sägen, where the newlyweds saw a log together.

In German weddings, the groom traditionally carries the bride over the threshold. Guests throw coins at the couple (Münzen werfen) for prosperity. The wedding feast often features a special Hochzeitssuppe (wedding soup). In Bavaria, weddings may include traditional clothing: der Dirndl for women and die Lederhosen for men. In Austria, the Brautwaltzer (bridal waltz) is an important part of the celebration.

In Switzerland, wedding customs vary by canton. In some regions, the couple plants a tree (den Baum pflanzen) after the ceremony. The Steinstuben tradition in rural Switzerland involves friends blocking the road with stones, and the groom must pay a toll to pass — similar to the German Sträßchen ziehen (blocking the street).

Beispiel

Auf einem deutschen Polterabend werfen die Gäste altes Porzellan auf den Boden. Das bringt Glück für die Ehe. Am nächsten Tag ist die Hochzeit.
At a German Polterabend, guests throw old porcelain on the ground. This brings luck for the marriage. The next day is the wedding.

Expressing Opinions on Modern Relationships

For the speaking and writing exams, you should be able to express and justify opinions about modern relationships. Useful structures include: Ich finde, dass... (I think that...), Meiner Meinung nach... (In my opinion...), Ich bin der Ansicht, dass... (I am of the view that...). For Higher tier, try using man sollte (one should) or es wäre besser, wenn... (it would be better if...).

Debatable topics include whether marriage is still important today: Ist die Ehe heute noch wichtig? Many young Germans think marriage is less important than commitment: Die Liebe ist wichtiger als die Ehe (Love is more important than marriage). Others believe marriage provides stability: Die Ehe gibt Sicherheit (Marriage provides security).

Statistics show that the average age of marriage in Germany has risen to around 33 for men and 31 for women. The birth rate in Germany is relatively low compared to other European countries. In the exam, quoting statistics or cultural facts can demonstrate intercultural understanding and earn higher marks.

Beispiel

Meiner Meinung nach ist die Ehe nicht mehr so wichtig wie früher. Viele Paare leben gerne zusammen, ohne zu heiraten. Ich finde, dass die Liebe wichtiger ist als ein Stück Papier.
In my opinion marriage is no longer as important as before. Many couples like living together without getting married. I think that love is more important than a piece of paper.

Additional Practice Questions

Q1: Was denkst du über die Ehe heute? Ist sie noch wichtig? Gib deine Meinung mit Gründen. (What do you think about marriage today? Is it still important? Give your opinion with reasons.)

Q2: Übersetze ins Deutsche: In Germany, many couples live together before getting married. The wedding traditions are interesting, for example the Polterabend where guests break porcelain for good luck.

Model Answers

  1. Meiner Meinung nach ist die Ehe heute nicht mehr so wichtig wie früher. Viele junge Leute in Deutschland leben zusammen, ohne zu heiraten, weil sie die Freiheit bevorzugen. Außerdem ist eine Hochzeit sehr teuer — sie kostet durchschnittlich 15 000 Euro in Deutschland. Ich finde jedoch, dass die Ehe Sicherheit gibt, besonders wenn man Kinder hat. Meine Großeltern sind seit vierzig Jahren verheiratet und sie sagen, dass die Ehe eine lebenslange Verpflichtung ist.

    (In my opinion marriage is not as important today as before. Many young people in Germany live together without marrying because they prefer freedom. Besides, a wedding is very expensive — it costs on average 15,000 euros in Germany. However, I think marriage provides security, especially when you have children. My grandparents have been married for forty years and they say that marriage is a lifelong commitment.)
  2. In Deutschland leben viele Paare zusammen, bevor sie heiraten. Die Hochzeitstraditionen sind interessant, zum Beispiel der Polterabend, wo die Gäste Porzellan für Glück zerschlagen.
