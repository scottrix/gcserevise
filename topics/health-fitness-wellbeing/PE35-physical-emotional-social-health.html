PE35: Physical, Emotional and Social Health

Foundation Higher All Boards Paper 2

Understanding the physical, emotional/mental and social health benefits of regular exercise and physical activity, and how each contributes to overall wellbeing.

💪 The Three Dimensions of Health

Health: A state of complete physical, mental and social wellbeing, not merely the absence of disease or infirmity (WHO definition). Health has three interconnected dimensions: physical, emotional/mental, and social.
The Health Triangle:
       PHYSICAL
       /     \
   EMOTIONAL ——— SOCIAL

All three dimensions are connected. Poor physical health can lead to poor mental health. Good social health supports emotional wellbeing. Exercise positively affects all three.

🏃 Physical Health Benefits

Regular physical activity has numerous physical health benefits that reduce the risk of chronic diseases and improve bodily function.

Reduced Risk of Heart Disease (CHD)

  • Exercise strengthens the heart muscle, increasing stroke volume and cardiac output
  • Reduces resting heart rate (bradycardia) - the heart works more efficiently
  • Lowers blood pressure, reducing strain on blood vessels
  • Increases HDL (good) cholesterol and reduces LDL (bad) cholesterol
  • Reduces the build-up of fatty deposits (atheroma) in coronary arteries
  • Improves circulation and reduces the risk of blood clots

Reduced Risk of Obesity

  • Exercise burns calories, helping maintain a healthy energy balance
  • Builds muscle mass, which increases basal metabolic rate
  • Reduces body fat percentage when combined with proper nutrition
  • Prevents weight gain and helps with weight management long-term
  • Maintains a healthy BMI (Body Mass Index between 18.5 and 24.9)

Reduced Risk of Type 2 Diabetes

  • Exercise improves the body's sensitivity to insulin
  • Muscles use glucose for energy during activity, lowering blood sugar levels
  • Regular activity helps maintain stable blood glucose levels
  • Reduces the risk of developing insulin resistance
  • Helps manage weight, which is a key risk factor for type 2 diabetes

Improved Cardiovascular Fitness

  • Increased VO₂ max (maximum oxygen uptake)
  • Larger stroke volume and lower resting heart rate
  • More efficient oxygen delivery to working muscles
  • Increased capillarisation around muscles and alveoli
  • Greater endurance capacity for everyday activities

Additional Physical Benefits

  • Stronger bones (weight-bearing exercise reduces osteoporosis risk)
  • Stronger muscles and improved posture
  • Better immune system function - moderate exercise reduces infection risk
  • Improved flexibility and joint mobility
  • Better sleep quality and energy levels
  • Reduced risk of certain cancers (bowel, breast)
Key Statistics

The NHS recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week, plus strength exercises on two or more days. Regular physical activity can reduce the risk of heart disease by up to 35%, type 2 diabetes by up to 50%, and some cancers by up to 20%.

🧠 Emotional and Mental Health Benefits

Exercise has powerful effects on emotional and mental wellbeing, making it one of the most effective treatments for mild to moderate depression and anxiety.

Stress Relief

  • Exercise reduces levels of stress hormones (cortisol and adrenaline)
  • Physical activity provides a healthy outlet for tension and frustration
  • Rhythmic, repetitive exercises (running, swimming) have a meditative, calming effect
  • Time away from daily worries and pressures
  • Reduces the physical symptoms of stress (muscle tension, headaches)

Serotonin Release

  • Exercise stimulates the release of serotonin, a neurotransmitter that improves mood
  • Low serotonin levels are linked to depression
  • Physical activity also releases endorphins - the body's natural painkillers
  • Endorphins create the "runner's high" - a feeling of euphoria after exercise
  • Dopamine release creates a sense of reward and motivation

Self-Esteem and Confidence

  • Achieving fitness goals builds a sense of accomplishment and self-worth
  • Improved body image from physical changes increases confidence
  • Mastery of new skills and techniques boosts self-belief
  • Positive feedback from coaches and teammates reinforces self-esteem
  • Regular exercise creates a sense of discipline and control

Emotional Control

  • Exercise provides a constructive way to channel anger and frustration
  • Regular activity improves emotional regulation and resilience
  • Reduces symptoms of anxiety and panic through controlled breathing
  • Helps manage mood swings and irritability
  • Improves the ability to cope with setbacks and disappointments

Reduced Depression

  • Regular exercise is as effective as medication for mild to moderate depression
  • Combines serotonin release, social interaction and sense of achievement
  • Provides structure and routine that depression often removes
  • Gives a sense of purpose and something to work towards
  • NHS now prescribes exercise as a treatment for depression
Chemical Effects of Exercise on the Brain:
Serotonin → Improves mood, reduces depression
Endorphins → Natural painkillers, create euphoria
Dopamine → Reward and motivation, reinforces positive behaviour
These chemical changes explain why regular exercise is such a powerful treatment for mental health conditions.

🤝 Social Health Benefits

Physical activity and sport provide significant social benefits that combat isolation and build community connections.

Teamwork

  • Team sports require cooperation, communication and trust between members
  • Learning to work towards a common goal with others
  • Developing leadership and followership skills
  • Understanding different roles within a team structure
  • Sharing successes and supporting each other through failures

Cooperation

  • Sport teaches the value of working with others rather than against them
  • Pair and group activities develop cooperation skills
  • Learning to compromise and adapt to others' strengths and weaknesses
  • Following rules and respecting the decisions of officials
  • Understanding fair play and mutual respect

Socialising

  • Sport provides a structured social environment outside of work or school
  • Regular training sessions and matches create routine social contact
  • Post-match socialising builds friendships and community bonds
  • Shared experiences (wins, losses, training) create strong social connections
  • Provides common ground and topics of conversation

Making Friends

  • Joining a club or team introduces people to others with shared interests
  • Regular attendance builds familiarity and friendships over time
  • Shared challenges and achievements create bonds between people
  • Sport can be particularly important for people who struggle to make friends in other contexts
  • Clubs provide a support network beyond the sport itself

Sense of Belonging

  • Being part of a team or club provides identity and purpose
  • Wearing a team kit and representing a community creates belonging
  • Shared traditions, songs and rituals strengthen group identity
  • Combats loneliness and social isolation, especially in older adults
  • Provides a sense of being valued and included

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain three physical health benefits of regular exercise.

Q2: How does exercise improve emotional and mental health? Refer to specific chemicals released during physical activity.

Q3: Describe three social health benefits of participating in sport and give a sporting example for each.

Q4: Explain how regular exercise reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes.

Q5: "Exercise is as effective as medication for treating mild depression." Evaluate this statement with reference to how exercise affects mental health.

Q6: How can sport help combat social isolation? Explain with specific examples.

✅ Answers

  1. Three physical health benefits: (1) Reduced risk of coronary heart disease - exercise strengthens the heart muscle, lowers blood pressure, increases HDL cholesterol and reduces fatty deposits in arteries. (2) Reduced risk of obesity - exercise burns calories and builds muscle mass, helping maintain a healthy energy balance and body weight. (3) Improved cardiovascular fitness - regular aerobic exercise increases VO₂ max, stroke volume and capillarisation, making the heart and lungs more efficient at delivering oxygen to working muscles.
  2. Exercise improves emotional and mental health through several chemical mechanisms: (1) Serotonin - exercise stimulates the release of serotonin, a neurotransmitter that improves mood and reduces depression. Low serotonin is linked to depression. (2) Endorphins - the body's natural painkillers are released during exercise, creating a feeling of euphoria (the "runner's high"). (3) Dopamine - exercise releases dopamine, which creates a sense of reward and motivation. Additionally, exercise reduces cortisol (stress hormone) levels, provides a sense of achievement, and improves self-esteem and body image - all contributing to better emotional health.
  3. Three social health benefits: (1) Teamwork - sport requires working together towards shared goals, e.g. a football team coordinating attacks and defending as a unit. (2) Making friends - joining a club introduces you to people with shared interests, e.g. a new member of a running club meeting others at weekly sessions. (3) Sense of belonging - being part of a team gives identity and community, e.g. wearing a club kit and representing your local area creates a feeling of being valued and included.
  4. Regular exercise reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes by: improving the body's sensitivity to insulin, meaning cells can more effectively absorb glucose from the blood; using glucose for energy during physical activity, which directly lowers blood sugar levels; helping maintain a healthy body weight, as obesity is a primary risk factor for type 2 diabetes; and building muscle mass, which increases the body's capacity to store and use glucose, keeping blood sugar levels more stable over time.
  5. This statement is supported by research showing that regular exercise can be as effective as antidepressant medication for mild to moderate depression. Exercise works through multiple pathways: serotonin release improves mood, endorphins reduce pain and create euphoria, dopamine provides reward and motivation, and the sense of achievement and routine combats the withdrawal and hopelessness of depression. Exercise also improves self-esteem, provides social interaction and reduces stress hormones. However, for severe depression, medication and professional therapy may still be necessary, and exercise should be part of a broader treatment plan rather than a complete replacement for medical care.
  6. Sport combats social isolation by: providing regular, structured social contact through training sessions and matches, giving isolated people a reason to leave the house and interact with others; creating a sense of belonging through team membership and shared identity (e.g. wearing a club shirt, being part of a squad); introducing people to others with shared interests, making it easier to form friendships than in unstructured social settings; and providing a support network where members look out for each other's wellbeing. For example, an elderly person joining a walking group gains weekly social contact, friendship and a sense of community that combats the isolation they might otherwise experience living alone.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Organise your answer into physical, emotional and social categories
  • Use specific chemical names: serotonin, endorphins, dopamine, cortisol
  • Physical health = disease reduction (CHD, obesity, diabetes) plus fitness improvements
  • Emotional health = mood, stress, self-esteem, emotional control, depression
  • Social health = teamwork, cooperation, friends, belonging - give sport examples
  • Remember the health triangle - all three dimensions are interconnected

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often confuse emotional and social health. Wrong: "Making friends is an emotional benefit." Correct: "Making friends is a social benefit. Emotional benefits include stress relief, improved mood through serotonin release, increased self-esteem and reduced depression."

Students often say exercise "makes you happy" without explaining the mechanism. Wrong: "Exercise makes you happy because it's fun." Correct: "Exercise improves mood by stimulating the release of serotonin and endorphins, which are neurotransmitters that reduce depression and create feelings of euphoria and wellbeing."

📝 Exam Technique

PE Exam Tips:
Cover all three dimensions of health (physical, emotional, social). For obesity, explain both causes and consequences. For nutrition, know the role of each nutrient. For somatotypes, justify with specific sporting examples. Always evaluate, not just describe.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think you need to be slim to be fit. Wrong: You need to be slim to be fit Correct: Fitness depends on the ability to meet demands placed on you. Sumo wrestlers, rugby forwards and powerlifters may have high body fat but are extremely fit for their sport.

Students often think drinking water during exercise is enough to stay hydrated. Wrong: Drinking water during exercise is enough to stay hydrated Correct: For prolonged exercise (over 60 minutes), electrolyte replacement may also be needed. Drinking too much plain water can cause hyponatraemia (dangerously low sodium).

Students often think obesity is just about eating too much. Wrong: Obesity is just about eating too much Correct: Obesity has multiple causes: genetics, socio-economic factors, sedentary lifestyle, medical conditions, and mental health. It is not simply a matter of willpower or overeating.

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE PE tests four AOs: AO1 (Knowledge, 30%) — recall facts about Physical, Emotional and Social Health including definitions, classifications and specific examples; AO2 (Application, 30%) — apply knowledge to sporting contexts and training scenarios; AO3 (Analysis and Evaluation, 25%) — analyse data (graphs, tables), evaluate training methods or strategies, and justify recommendations; AO4 (Practical, 15%) — demonstrate relevant skills. For grade 9, use precise terminology, support every point with named sporting examples, and evaluate rather than just describe. The difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of application and depth of evaluation.
