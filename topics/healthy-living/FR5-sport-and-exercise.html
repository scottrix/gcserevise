❓ Practice Questions

✅ Answers

  2. A structured paragraph with: introduction (à mon avis...), opinion with reason (je pense que... parce que...), a different viewpoint (cependant...), and a conclusion (en conclusion...). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Je pense que [topic] est important(e) parce que..." — ensure adjective agreement (important vs importante depending on noun gender), correct verb form, and give a specific reason.

  • Check adjective agreement (gender and number) before finishing
  • Use complex structures: negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais), pronouns (le, la, les), conjunctions (cependant, donc, en plus)
  • Include cultural references to French-speaking countries
  • For writing: plan your response, check verb conjugations, re-read for errors

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often misuse faire de and jouer à. Wrong: Je joue du tennis / je fais à le foot Correct: Je joue au tennis (= jouer à + le sport) / je fais du foot (= faire de + le sport). Students also forget partitive articles change to de/d' after negation. Wrong: Je ne mange pas du porc. Correct: Je ne mange pas de porc.

Notes Détaillées

Faire de vs Jouer à

The distinction between faire de and jouer à is one of the most tested grammar points at GCSE. Jouer à is used with ball games and competitive sports: je joue au football, je joue au tennis, je joue au rugby. Note the contractions: à + le = au, à + les = aux (je joue aux cartes). Faire de is used with activities and sports that don't involve a ball game: je fais de la natation, je fais du ski, je fais de l'escalade.

A common mistake is mixing these up: je fais du football is wrong — it should be je joue au football. Similarly, je joue à la natation is incorrect — say je fais de la natation. Remember: if you play against someone with a ball, use jouer à; if you "do" an activity, use faire de. The partitive article after faire de changes: du (masculine), de la (feminine), de l' (vowel), des (plural).

For Higher tier, use these structures in different tenses. Perfect: J'ai fait du ski pendant les vacances (I did skiing during the holidays). Imperfect: Quand j'étais petit, je jouais au rugby chaque semaine (When I was young, I played rugby every week). Future: Je vais faire de la gymnastique demain (I am going to do gymnastics tomorrow).

Exemple

Je joue au basket le mercredi et je fais de la natation le samedi. L'année dernière, j'ai fait du ski dans les Alpes. — I play basketball on Wednesday and I go swimming on Saturday. Last year, I went skiing in the Alps.

Les Bienfaits du Sport et de l'Exercice

Being able to explain the benefits of sport is key for GCSE writing and speaking. Physical benefits: Le sport renforce le cœur et les muscles (Sport strengthens the heart and muscles), ça aide à rester en forme (it helps to stay fit), ça brûle des calories (it burns calories). Mental health benefits: Le sport réduit le stress (Sport reduces stress), c'est bon pour la santé mentale (it's good for mental health), ça améliore l'humeur (it improves mood).

France has a strong sporting culture. Le Tour de France, the world's most famous cycling race, has run since 1903. Le football is the most popular sport — France won the World Cup in 1998 and 2018. Le rugby is especially popular in the south-west of France. Le pétanque (boules) is a traditional sport played in every village square, especially in the south. In Sénégal, la lutte sénégalaise (Senegalese wrestling) is the most popular sport, attracting huge crowds.

Express your opinions about sport using complex structures: À mon avis, le sport est essentiel pour la santé (In my opinion, sport is essential for health). Je trouve que faire du sport régulièrement améliore la concentration (I think doing sport regularly improves concentration). Use cependant (however) to show balance: Cependant, il ne faut pas en faire trop — on risque les blessures (However, one must not overdo it — you risk injuries).

Exemple

À mon avis, le sport est essentiel pour rester en bonne santé. D'une part, ça renforce le corps, et d'autre part, ça réduit le stress. Cependant, il ne faut pas en faire trop. — In my opinion, sport is essential for staying healthy. On one hand, it strengthens the body, and on the other hand, it reduces stress. However, one must not overdo it.

Le Sport dans la Francophonie

Sport plays different roles across the francophone world. In France, les JO (Jeux Olympiques) were held in Paris in 2024 — a huge cultural event. School sport in France is compulsory, with l'EPS (Éducation Physique et Sportive) being a mandatory subject. Students typically do two hours per week, and it counts towards the baccalauréat.

In Québec, le hockey sur glace (ice hockey) is a national passion, with the Canadiens de Montréal being the most famous team. In francophone Africa, football dominates — Sénégal won the Africa Cup of Nations in 2022. La lutte traditionnelle (traditional wrestling) is deeply rooted in Senegalese culture and is more popular than football in rural areas.

For the exam, comparing sporting cultures shows depth: En France, le cyclisme est très populaire grâce au Tour de France, tandis qu'au Québec, c'est le hockey sur glace qui est la passion nationale. Use tandis que, alors que, and par contre for comparisons.

Exemple

Au Sénégal, la lutte traditionnelle est le sport le plus populaire. Au Québec, le hockey sur glace est la passion nationale, avec les Canadiens de Montréal. — In Sénégal, traditional wrestling is the most popular sport. In Québec, ice hockey is the national passion, with the Montreal Canadiens.
