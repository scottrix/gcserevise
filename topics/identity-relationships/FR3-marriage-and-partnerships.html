❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Write 4 sentences in French about marriage and partnerships.

Q2: Write a paragraph (40-60 words) giving your opinion on marriage and partnerships.

Q3: Translate into French: "I think marriage and partnerships is important because..."

✅ Answers

  1. Four sentences using topic vocabulary, with correct adjective agreement, articles and verb conjugation. Include at least two different tenses for Higher tier.
  2. A structured paragraph with: introduction (à mon avis...), opinion with reason (je pense que... parce que...), a different viewpoint (cependant...), and a conclusion (en conclusion...). Use connecting words and varied vocabulary.
  3. "Je pense que [topic] est important(e) parce que..." — ensure adjective agreement (important vs importante depending on noun gender), correct verb form, and give a specific reason.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use a range of tenses (present, past, future) to access higher marks
  • Include opinions with reasons (je pense que... parce que...)
  • Check adjective agreement (gender and number) before finishing
  • Use complex structures: negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais), pronouns (le, la, les), conjunctions (cependant, donc, en plus)
  • Include cultural references to French-speaking countries
  • For writing: plan your response, check verb conjugations, re-read for errors

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often forget adjective agreement. Wrong: Elle est intelligent. Correct: Elle est intelligente. (Add -e for feminine.) Students also confuse mon/ma/mes. Wrong: Ma père Correct: Mon père (use mon before masculine nouns starting with a vowel for euphony).

Notes Détaillées

Le Mariage et les Traditions

Marriage vocabulary is essential for GCSE: le mariage (marriage/wedding), se marier (to get married), la cérémonie (ceremony), la réception (reception), les fiançailles (engagement), le divorce (divorce), la séparation (separation). In France, a wedding must take place at la mairie (town hall) in a civil ceremony (le mariage civil) before any religious ceremony. The mayor or deputy performs the ceremony, and the couple signs le livret de famille (family record book).

French wedding traditions include la voiture balai — the last car in the wedding procession, decorated with tin cans and ribbons. Guests honk horns through the streets. At the reception, la pièce montée — a tower of small cream puffs (choux) bound with caramel — is the traditional wedding cake. The vin d'honneur (reception drinks) precedes the sit-down meal, which can last six hours or more.

Use the future tense to discuss marriage plans: Nous allons nous marier l'année prochaine (We are going to get married next year). Use the conditional for hypothetical situations: Je voudrais me marier dans une église (I would like to get married in a church).

Exemple

En France, le mariage civil à la mairie est obligatoire. Après, beaucoup de couples ont aussi une cérémonie religieuse. La pièce montée est le gâteau traditionnel. — In France, a civil wedding at the town hall is compulsory. After that, many couples also have a religious ceremony. The pièce montée is the traditional cake.

Le PACS et les Relations Modernes

The PACS (Pacte Civil de Solidarité), introduced in 1999, is a civil union available to both same-sex and opposite-sex couples. It provides many legal benefits of marriage but is easier to dissolve. In 2013, France legalised same-sex marriage (le mariage pour tous), making it one of the first major European countries to do so. These are important cultural points for the GCSE — they demonstrate understanding of contemporary French society.

Many young French people choose to vivre en concubinage (live together without being married) before — or instead of — marriage. Le taux de divorce (divorce rate) in France is around 50%, which is similar to the UK. Understanding these social trends helps you discuss marriage and partnerships with cultural depth.

Expressing opinions on marriage is a common exam task. Use a range of structures: À mon avis, le mariage n'est plus aussi important qu'avant (In my opinion, marriage is no longer as important as before). Je pense que le PACS est une bonne alternative (I think the PACS is a good alternative). Cependant, certaines personnes préfèrent les traditions (However, some people prefer traditions).

Exemple

De plus en plus de jeunes Français choisissent le PACS plutôt que le mariage, parce que c'est plus flexible. Cependant, le mariage pour tous, légal depuis 2013, est une grande avancée. — More and more young French people choose the PACS rather than marriage, because it is more flexible. However, same-sex marriage, legal since 2013, is a great step forward.

Les Cérémonies dans la Francophonie

Wedding customs vary greatly across the francophone world. In Sénégal, weddings are major community events lasting several days. The tess (engagement ceremony) and la cérémonie du cauri (cowrie shell ceremony) are traditional elements. The bride wears a boubou (traditional robe) and the celebration includes le sabar — energetic drumming and dancing.

In Québec, weddings blend French and North American traditions. La soirée de réception often includes le bal des mariés (the wedding dance) and les dragées (sugared almonds as favours). A fun Québécois tradition is where guests play pranks on the newlyweds. In Belgium, the tradition of decorating the couple's doorstep is still practised in some regions.

For the exam, you could compare traditions: Tandis qu'en France, la pièce montée est le gâteau traditionnel, au Sénégal, le thiéboudienne est souvent servi lors des célébrations. Using tandis que and alors que shows complex sentence structures.

Exemple

Au Sénégal, le mariage dure plusieurs jours. Il y a le tess, la cérémonie traditionnelle, et le sabar avec des tambours. La mariée porte un boubou magnifique. — In Sénégal, the wedding lasts several days. There is the tess, the traditional ceremony, and the sabar with drums. The bride wears a magnificent boubou.

Questions Supplémentaires

Q4: Quelle est votre opinion sur le mariage? Préférez-vous le mariage ou le PACS? Pourquoi? (What is your opinion on marriage? Do you prefer marriage or the PACS? Why?)

Q5: Décrivez une tradition de mariage dans un pays francophone. (Describe a wedding tradition in a francophone country.)

Réponses Supplémentaires

  1. À mon avis, le mariage et le PACS sont tous les deux valables. Je pense que le PACS est plus flexible et convient mieux aux jeunes d'aujourd'hui, parce qu'il est plus facile à dissoudre. Cependant, le mariage reste important pour beaucoup de gens, surtout pour des raisons religieuses ou traditionnelles. Personnellement, je voudrais me marier un jour, parce que je trouve la cérémonie belle et symbolique. — In my opinion, both marriage and the PACS are valid. I think the PACS is more flexible and suits today's young people better, because it is easier to dissolve. However, marriage remains important for many people, especially for religious or traditional reasons. Personally, I would like to get married one day, because I find the ceremony beautiful and symbolic.
  2. Au Sénégal, le mariage est une grande fête communautaire qui dure plusieurs jours. Le tess est la cérémonie de fiançailles où les familles se rencontrent. Le jour du mariage, la mariée porte un magnifique boubou et il y a le sabar — une célébration avec des tambours et des danses traditionnelles. Toute la communauté participe et on partage le thiéboudienne, le plat national. C'est une tradition magnifique qui montre l'importance de la famille élargie. — In Sénégal, a wedding is a big community celebration lasting several days. The tess is the engagement ceremony where the families meet. On the wedding day, the bride wears a magnificent boubou and there is the sabar — a celebration with drums and traditional dances. The whole community participates and they share thiéboudienne, the national dish. It is a magnificent tradition that shows the importance of the extended family.
