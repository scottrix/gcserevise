RS25: Hajj: Pilgrimage

All Boards

The role and significance of pilgrimage to Makkah; Ka'aba, Mina, Arafat, Muzdalifah

☪️ Hajj: Pilgrimage to Makkah

Key Fact: Hajj is the fifth Pillar of Islam — every Muslim who is physically and financially able must make the pilgrimage to Makkah at least once in their lifetime. Over 2 million Muslims perform Hajj each year during the month of Dhul-Hijjah.

📋 The Stages of Hajj

  1. Ihram — entering a state of purity; wearing simple white garments (two unstitched sheets for men); no cutting hair or nails, no perfume, no arguments
  2. Tawaf — walking seven times around the Ka'bah in the Grand Mosque, the holiest site in Islam
  3. Sa'y — running seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah, remembering Hajar's search for water
  4. Mina — travelling to Mina; spending the day in prayer and reflection
  5. Arafat — the most important day; standing on the Plain of Arafat from noon to sunset, asking Allah for forgiveness. This is the Day of Arafah — the "essence of Hajj"
  6. Muzdalifah — collecting pebbles after leaving Arafat; spending the night under the stars
  7. Ramy al-Jamarat — stoning the three pillars at Mina, representing rejection of the devil (following Ibrahim's example)
  8. Eid al-Adha — sacrificing an animal (or paying for one to be sacrificed), remembering Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son
  9. Tawaf al-Ifadah — final circling of the Ka'bah before leaving Makkah

🔍 Why Hajj Matters

  • It unites the ummah — Muslims of every race and nationality gather as equals in simple white garments
  • It follows Ibrahim's example — the Ka'bah was built by Ibrahim and the stoning remembers his rejection of the devil
  • It brings forgiveness — a properly performed Hajj wipes out all past sins
  • It is a journey of a lifetime — Muslims save for years and prepare spiritually
  • It reminds Muslims of the Day of Judgement — standing before Allah in white garments, all equal

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain two reasons why Hajj is important for Muslims.

✅ Answers

  1. 1) Hajj unites the ummah — over 2 million Muslims from every country gather in Makkah, wearing identical simple white garments. This removes all distinctions of wealth, race and status, showing that all Muslims are equal before Allah. 2) Hajj brings forgiveness — Muslims believe a properly performed Hajj wipes out all past sins. Standing on the Plain of Arafat and sincerely repenting is the most powerful moment, when Muslims feel closest to Allah and experience his mercy.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Islam practices questions: Know the Five Pillars in detail and be able to explain each one. Distinguish between Sunni and Shi'a practices (e.g. additional Ten Obligatory Acts in Shi'a Islam). Explain the difference between greater and lesser jihad. Use specific examples for Hajj (Ka'aba, Arafat, Muzdalifah, Mina).

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often think jihad means "holy war." Wrong: "Jihad means holy war." Correct: "Jihad means "struggle" or "striving." Greater jihad is the internal spiritual struggle to live as a good Muslim. Lesser jihad is armed struggle, which has strict conditions and is a last resort."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that the role and significance of pilgrimage to makkah; ka'aba, mina, arafat, muzdalifah.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
