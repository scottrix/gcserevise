Baroque Music 1650-1750

The Coronation Anthems and Oratorios of Handel; basso continuo; ground bass; contrapuntal writing; ornamentation; terraced dynamics; modal harmony moving to major/minor; basso ostinato; figured bass; Handel's Coronation Anthems (Zadok the Priest); orchestral development.

Key Fact: Baroque music features basso continuo (keyboard + cello/bass providing harmonic foundation) and terraced dynamics (sudden dynamic shifts, not gradual).
Key Fact: Handel's Coronation Anthems exemplify Baroque grandeur: choral polyphony, orchestral doubling, majestic key choices and ceremonial character.
Key Fact: Ground bass (repeated bass pattern) provides structural foundation — used in arias and instrumental works by Purcell and Handel.
Key Fact: Ornamentation (trills, mordents, grace notes) was often improvised by performers — Baroque notation is simpler than what was actually played.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always use PRECISE musical vocabulary — not vague descriptions like it sounds nice

  • For performing: focus on accuracy, expression and interpretation
  • For composing: show development of ideas, not just repetition

📝 Exam Technique

Music Exam Tips:
Identify Baroque features by NAME (basso continuo, terraced dynamics, ground bass). Reference Handel SPECIFICALLY. Explain how Baroque conventions create character.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Baroque music is just old classical music with no dynamics. Correct: Baroque music has TERRACED dynamics (sudden contrasts between loud and soft) as a deliberate style — gradual crescendos/diminuendos came later. The basso continuo provides a continuous harmonic foundation. Understanding Baroque conventions is essential for identifying this style in the listening exam.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Describe the key features of Baroque music using specific examples from Handel's music. Explain how these features create the ceremonial character appropriate to coronation music.

A grade 9 response will: identify specific Baroque features (basso continuo, terraced dynamics, ground bass, ornamentation); reference Handel's Coronation Anthems with specific observations; explain how musical features serve the ceremonial purpose; use precise period vocabulary.

Mark scheme: AO3 (analyse and evaluate) + AO4 (knowledge and understanding) = total marks for listening; AO1 (perform) + AO2 (compose) for coursework components

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Music has three components: Understanding Music (40% — written exam with listening and study-piece questions), Performing Music (30% — one solo and one ensemble performance), Composing Music (30% — one free composition and one to a brief). The four Assessment Objectives are: AO1 Perform with technical control, expression and interpretation (30%); AO2 Compose and develop musical ideas with technical control and coherence (30%); AO3 Analyse and evaluate music using musical vocabulary (20%); AO4 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of musical elements, contexts and language (20%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional technical control, creative and coherent composition, perceptive analysis using precise terminology, and sophisticated understanding of how musical elements create meaning in context.
