Romantic Music 1815-1910

Piano music of Chopin and Schumann; the Requiem of the late Romantic period; expanded orchestra; chromatic harmony; programmatic music; nationalism in music; rubato; thicker textures; emotional expression; larger forms; Verdi and Fauré Requiems.

Key Fact: Romantic music prioritised emotional expression: chromatic harmony, rubato, wider dynamic range and thicker textures created intensity and drama.
Key Fact: Chopin's piano music uses rubato (flexible tempo), elaborate ornamentation and harmonic chromaticism — it demands expressive, personal interpretation.
Key Fact: The Romantic orchestra expanded significantly: more brass (trombones, tuba), more woodwind (piccolo, cor anglais, bass clarinet), harp and percussion.
Key Fact: Late Romantic Requiems (Verdi, Fauré) combined liturgical text with dramatic orchestration — sacred music with theatrical emotional power.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe how romantic music 1815-1910 are used in a piece of music you have studied. Use musical vocabulary.

Q2: Compare how two different pieces use romantic music 1815-1910. Explain the effect in each case.

Q3: How could you use romantic music 1815-1910 in your own composition or performance? Explain your choices.

✅ Answers

  1. A detailed description using precise musical vocabulary (e.g. specific terms from the AQA elements list), referencing a named piece with accurate observations about how the elements are used and their effect.
  2. A comparison of two pieces identifying similarities and differences in how romantic music 1815-1910 are used, explaining the different effects created in each musical context, using comparative language.
  3. A practical application showing understanding of how romantic music 1815-1910 can be manipulated in composition or performance, with specific examples of choices made and their intended effect.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always use PRECISE musical vocabulary — not vague descriptions like it sounds nice
  • Reference SPECIFIC musical elements when analysing any piece
  • For listening questions: identify elements FIRST, then explain their effect
  • For study pieces: know the context, structure and key features in detail
  • For performing: focus on accuracy, expression and interpretation
  • For composing: show development of ideas, not just repetition

📝 Exam Technique

Music Exam Tips:
Identify Romantic features: chromatic harmony, rubato, expanded orchestration, programmatic elements. Compare with Classical style. Explain how features reflect Romantic values.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Romantic music is just classical music that is more emotional. Correct: Romantic music has SPECIFIC identifiable features: chromatic harmony (moving beyond diatonic chords), expanded orchestration, programmatic elements (music telling stories), rubato, thicker polyphonic textures, and nationalism. These are distinguishable features the exam tests you on.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Compare a Romantic piano piece by Chopin with a Classical piano piece by Mozart. Identify at least three differences in musical elements and explain how each reflects the change from Classical to Romantic style.

A grade 9 response will: identify specific Romantic features (chromaticism, rubato, expanded dynamics); compare with Classical conventions; explain how changes in musical elements reflect changing aesthetic values; use period-specific vocabulary throughout.

Mark scheme: AO3 (analyse and evaluate) + AO4 (knowledge and understanding) = total marks for listening; AO1 (perform) + AO2 (compose) for coursework components

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Music has three components: Understanding Music (40% — written exam with listening and study-piece questions), Performing Music (30% — one solo and one ensemble performance), Composing Music (30% — one free composition and one to a brief). The four Assessment Objectives are: AO1 Perform with technical control, expression and interpretation (30%); AO2 Compose and develop musical ideas with technical control and coherence (30%); AO3 Analyse and evaluate music using musical vocabulary (20%); AO4 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of musical elements, contexts and language (20%). For grade 9, you must show exceptional technical control, creative and coherent composition, perceptive analysis using precise terminology, and sophisticated understanding of how musical elements create meaning in context.
