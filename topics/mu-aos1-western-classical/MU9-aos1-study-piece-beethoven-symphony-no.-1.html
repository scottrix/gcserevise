AoS1 Study Piece: Beethoven Symphony No. 1

Beethoven: Symphony No. 1, Movement 1 (Adagio molto - Allegro con brio). First assessed 2026. Sonata form structure; key: C major; orchestration; harmonic language; Beethoven's innovative treatment of Classical conventions; opening dissonance; slow introduction; theme groups; development techniques; recapitulation and coda.

Key Fact: The opening starts on a dissonant dominant seventh of F major — not the tonic C major — a deliberate subversion of Classical expectation.
Key Fact: The slow introduction (Adagio molto) delays the allegro, building anticipation before the first theme group enters in C major.
Key Fact: Beethoven's development section is unusually dramatic for a first symphony — he pushes harmonic and dynamic boundaries beyond Haydn's models.
Key Fact: The orchestration follows the Classical model but with Beethoven's characteristic weight: prominent winds, dynamic brass, and driving rhythmic energy.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: You just need to describe what happens in each section of the symphony. Correct: The exam requires CRITICAL APPRAISAL, not just description. You must explain HOW and WHY Beethoven uses musical elements, how his treatment is innovative compared to Haydn/Mozart, and how the music reflects its context. Description alone achieves low marks.
