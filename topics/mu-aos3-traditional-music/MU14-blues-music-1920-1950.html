Blues Music 1920-1950

Blues music from 1920-1950; 12-bar blues structure; blue notes (flattened 3rd, 5th, 7th); call and response; vocal techniques (melisma, falsetto, slide); walking bass; shuffle rhythm; AAB lyric structure; Delta blues to Chicago electric blues; Robert Johnson; Muddy Waters.

Key Fact: Blue notes (flattened 3rd, 5th and 7th of the major scale) create the characteristic blues sound — they fall between the notes of the Western scale.
Key Fact: 12-bar blues follows a I-IV-V chord progression over three four-bar phrases: the AAB lyric structure mirrors this harmonic pattern.
Key Fact: Call and response between vocalist and guitar/harp creates dialogue — a fundamental feature inherited from African musical traditions.
Key Fact: Shuffle rhythm (long-short subdivision of the beat) gives blues its distinctive swing feel — it is notated in 12/8 or with triplets in 4/4.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Blues is just sad music with guitar. Correct: Blues has SPECIFIC musical features: 12-bar blues structure, blue notes, call and response, AAB lyric form, shuffle rhythm, walking bass, and melismatic vocals. These are technical features the exam tests — not an emotional description. Identify features by name.
