N1: Ordering Numbers

Foundation Higher All Boards

Order positive and negative integers, decimals and fractions; use the symbols =, ≠, <, >, ≤, ≥

📋 Key Concepts

Definition: Ordering numbers means arranging them from smallest to largest (ascending) or largest to smallest (descending).

Comparison Symbols

SymbolMeaningExample
=Equal to5 = 5
≠Not equal to3 ≠ 7
<Less than2 < 8
>Greater than9 > 4
≤Less than or equal tox ≤ 10
≥Greater than or equal tox ≥ 0

Number Line

Negative numbers are smaller than positive numbers. The further left on a number line, the smaller the value.

... -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3 ...
(left = smaller, right = larger)

📝 Ordering Integers

Rule: Negative numbers are always smaller than positive numbers. The larger the negative number (ignoring the minus sign), the smaller its value.
Example 1

Order from smallest to largest: -5, 3, -1, 0, 2

Solution: -5, -1, 0, 2, 3

Explanation: -5 is furthest left on the number line, so it is the smallest.

Example 2

Which is smaller: -7 or -3?

Solution: -7 is smaller than -3

Explanation: -7 is further left on the number line.

📝 Ordering Decimals

Method: Compare digits from left to right, starting with the highest place value. Use place value columns if needed.
Example 3

Order from smallest to largest: 0.45, 0.5, 0.398, 0.42

Solution: 0.398, 0.42, 0.45, 0.5

Tip: Add zeros to make them the same length: 0.450, 0.500, 0.398, 0.420

📝 Ordering Fractions

Method 1: Convert all fractions to have the same denominator (common denominator)
Method 2: Convert all fractions to decimals
Example 4

Order from smallest to largest: 3/4, 2/5, 1/2

Solution (using common denominator):

Common denominator = 20

3/4 = 15/20, 2/5 = 8/20, 1/2 = 10/20

Order: 2/5, 1/2, 3/4

Example 5

Order from smallest to largest: 2/3, 5/8, 7/10

Solution (using decimals):

2/3 ≈ 0.667, 5/8 = 0.625, 7/10 = 0.7

Order: 5/8, 2/3, 7/10

📝 Mixed Numbers

Tip: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions or decimals for easier comparison.
Example 6

Order from smallest to largest: 2 1/4, 2.5, 2 3/8

Solution:

2 1/4 = 2.25, 2.5 = 2.5, 2 3/8 = 2.375

Order: 2 1/4, 2 3/8, 2.5

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Order from smallest to largest: -8, 4, -2, 0, 6

Q2: Order from smallest to largest: 0.7, 0.08, 0.71, 0.8

Q3: Order from smallest to largest: 1/2, 2/5, 3/10

Q4: Which symbol makes this correct? -5 ___ -2

Q5: Order from smallest to largest: 1.5, 1 1/3, 1.25, 1 3/4

✅ Answers

  1. -8, -2, 0, 4, 6
  2. 0.08, 0.7, 0.71, 0.8
  3. 3/10, 2/5, 1/2 (same as: 0.3, 0.4, 0.5)
  4. -5 < -2 (negative 5 is smaller)
  5. 1.25, 1 1/3, 1.5, 1 3/4

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always check if you need ascending (smallest to largest) or descending (largest to smallest)
  • For decimals, add zeros to make all numbers the same length
  • For fractions, convert to decimals if the denominators are hard to compare
  • Draw a number line for negative numbers if you get confused
  • Remember: -10 is smaller than -1

🧠 Problem-Solving Strategies

Problem-Solving

When ordering a mix of fractions, decimals and negatives: convert everything to the same format first. Decimals are usually easiest for comparison. For negatives, remember the number line — further left means smaller. Always re-read the question to check if ascending or descending order is needed.
Multi-Step Problem

Three students scored 5/8, 0.62 and 61% in a test. Put their scores in order from lowest to highest.

Solution: Convert all to decimals: 5/8 = 0.625, 0.62 = 0.62, 61% = 0.61. So 0.61, 0.62, 0.625. Order: 61%, 0.62, 5/8.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

1. Wrong: -7 is bigger than -3 Correct: -7 is smaller than -3 (it is further left on the number line)

2. Wrong: 0.45 is bigger than 0.5 because 45 > 5 Correct: 0.5 = 0.50, so 0.45 < 0.50 — add zeros to compare

3. Wrong: 3/5 is bigger than 2/3 because 3 > 2 and 5 > 3 Correct: Convert to common denominator: 3/5 = 9/15, 2/3 = 10/15, so 2/3 is bigger

✍️ 6-Mark Exam Question

Extended Answer

6 marks: A race has three runners with finish times of 9.87 s, 9 4/5 s and 9.79 s. (a) Convert all times to the same format. (b) State who finished first, second and third. (c) What is the time difference between first and last place?

(a) 9 4/5 = 9.8 s. Times in decimals: 9.87 s, 9.8 s, 9.79 s.

(b) Ascending order: 9.79 s (1st), 9.8 s (2nd), 9.87 s (3rd).

(c) Difference = 9.87 − 9.79 = 0.08 s.

Mark scheme: 2 marks for correct conversions, 2 marks for correct ordering, 2 marks for correct difference with working

📊 AO3: Reason & Interpret

Reasoning and Interpretation

A scientist measures three substances. Substance A has a temperature of −18°C, Substance B has −23°C, and Substance C has −15°C.

(a) Which substance has the lowest temperature?

(b) A fourth substance, D, has a temperature between A and C. What integer values could D's temperature be?

(c) The scientist says "Substance B is the coldest so it must have the largest temperature value." Is this correct? Explain your reasoning.

Answers: (a) Substance B (−23°C is furthest left on the number line). (b) D must be between −18 and −15, so integer values are −17 or −16. (c) No — −23 is the smallest value numerically, not the largest. The word "largest" refers to magnitude but in temperature, −23°C is the lowest/coldest, not the largest.
