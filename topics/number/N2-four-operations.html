N2: Four Operations

Foundation Higher All Boards

Add, subtract, multiply and divide integers, decimals and fractions; mixed numbers; place value; financial terms

📋 Key Concepts

The Four Operations: Addition (+), Subtraction (−), Multiplication (×), and Division (÷) are the foundation of arithmetic.

📝 Working with Integers

Adding Integers: Same signs → add and keep the sign. Different signs → subtract the smaller from the larger, keep the sign of the larger number.
Example 1

Calculate: (-7) + (-5)

Solution: -7 + (-5) = -12

Both negative, so add: 7 + 5 = 12, keep negative sign.

Example 2

Calculate: 15 + (-8)

Solution: 15 + (-8) = 7

Different signs: 15 - 8 = 7, positive is larger.

Subtracting Integers: Subtracting a negative is the same as adding a positive. a - (-b) = a + b
Example 3

Calculate: 12 - (-5)

Solution: 12 - (-5) = 12 + 5 = 17

📝 Decimal Operations

Addition/Subtraction: Line up decimal points vertically. Add zeros if needed to make the same number of decimal places.
Example 4

Calculate: 3.45 + 12.7

Solution:

Multiplication: Multiply as whole numbers, then count decimal places in both numbers to place the decimal point.
Example 5

Calculate: 2.4 × 3.5

Solution: 24 × 35 = 840

Two decimal places total: 8.40

Division: Make the divisor a whole number by multiplying both numbers by the same power of 10.
Example 6

Calculate: 15.6 ÷ 0.4

Solution: Multiply both by 10: 156 ÷ 4 = 39

📝 Fraction Operations

Addition/Subtraction: Find a common denominator first, then add/subtract the numerators.
Example 7

Calculate: 2/3 + 1/4

Solution: Common denominator = 12

2/3 = 8/12, 1/4 = 3/12

8/12 + 3/12 = 11/12

Multiplication: Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together. Simplify if possible.
Example 8

Calculate: 3/5 × 2/7

Solution: (3 × 2)/(5 × 7) = 6/35

Division: Multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction).
Example 9

Calculate: 3/4 ÷ 2/5

Solution: 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8 = 1 7/8

📝 Mixed Numbers

Method: Convert to improper fractions, calculate, then convert back to mixed numbers.
Example 10

Calculate: 2 1/3 × 1 1/2

Solution:

2 1/3 = 7/3, 1 1/2 = 3/2

7/3 × 3/2 = 21/6 = 7/2 = 3 1/2

Example 11

A shop buys items for £15 and sells them for £23. Find the profit.

Solution: Profit = £23 - £15 = £8

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Calculate: (-8) + 15 - (-3)

Q2: Calculate: 4.56 + 2.8

Q3: Calculate: 0.6 × 2.4

Q4: Calculate: 3/8 + 1/6

Q5: Calculate: 5/6 ÷ 2/3

✅ Answers

  1. -8 + 15 + 3 = 10
  2. 4.56 + 2.80 = 7.36
  3. 6 × 24 = 144 → two decimal places → 1.44
  4. Common denom. 24: 9/24 + 4/24 = 13/24
  5. 5/6 × 3/2 = 15/12 = 5/4 = 1 1/4

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always check your answer is sensible (e.g., multiplying by a number less than 1 gives a smaller answer)
  • For negative numbers, draw a number line if stuck
  • When dividing fractions, "keep it, flip it, multiply"
  • Watch out for mixed numbers - convert them first
  • In finance questions, profit = selling price - cost price
