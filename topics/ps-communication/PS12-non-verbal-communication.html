Non-Verbal Communication

Communication without words: body language, facial expressions, eye contact, posture, and personal space. The role of NVC in social interaction and emotional expression.

Key Fact: Non-verbal communication (NVC) conveys meaning without words: body language (posture, gestures), facial expressions (emotions), eye contact (interest, honesty), proxemics (use of personal space), and paralinguistics (tone, pitch, speed of voice).
Key Fact: Facial expressions: Ekman found six basic emotions expressed universally across cultures - happiness, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, disgust. This suggests facial expressions are innate (supporting nature). However, display rules (cultural rules about when to show emotions) affect how freely people express them.
Key Fact: Eye contact: signals interest and attention. Too little suggests dishonesty or disinterest; too much can feel threatening. Cultural differences: in some cultures, direct eye contact with authority is respectful; in others, it is rude.
Key Fact: Proxemics (Hall): four zones of personal space - intimate (0-0.5m, close relationships), personal (0.5-1.2m, friends), social (1.2-3.6m, acquaintances), public (3.6m+, strangers). Violating personal space causes discomfort.
Key Fact: Posture and gestures: open posture (uncrossed arms, facing someone) signals openness and interest; closed posture (crossed arms, turned away) signals defensiveness or disinterest. Emblems are gestures with specific cultural meanings (e.g. thumbs up).

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe the key types of non-verbal communication and explain how each conveys meaning.

Q2: Evaluate whether facial expressions are universal or culturally specific, using research evidence.

Q3: Explain how proxemics can affect social interactions, referring to Hall's zones of personal space.

✅ Answers

  1. Types of NVC: body language (posture and gestures convey openness or defensiveness), facial expressions (convey six basic emotions universally), eye contact (signals interest or threat), proxemics (personal space zones signal relationship closeness), paralinguistics (tone and pitch convey emotion beyond words). Each type provides additional information beyond spoken language.
  2. Ekman found universal recognition of six basic emotions across cultures, including the Fore tribe in Papua New Guinea who had limited Western contact. This supports the nature view (innate expressions). However, display rules vary: Japanese participants masked negative emotions more than Americans (Ekman & Friesen). Conclusion: facial expressions are biologically based but culturally regulated.
  3. Hall's four zones: intimate (close family/partners), personal (friends), social (colleagues/acquaintances), public (strangers/speeches). Being too close causes discomfort and anxiety (e.g. crowded lifts). Violating personal space can be used deliberately to intimidate or show intimacy. Cultural differences exist: some cultures prefer more personal space than others.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • 6 universal facial expressions: happiness, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, disgust (Ekman).
  • Proxemics: Intimate (0-0.5m), Personal (0.5-1.2m), Social (1.2-3.6m), Public (3.6m+).
  • NVC = 55% body language + 38% paralinguistics + 7% words (Mehrabian - only for emotional messages!).

📝 Exam Technique

Psychology Exam Tips:
When evaluating NVC, consider: are findings universal or culturally specific? Can NVC be reliably interpreted? Is Mehrabian's 55-38-7 rule applicable beyond emotional messages? (It's not - it only applies to ambiguous emotional communication.)

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Body language always reveals the truth because non-verbal signals are unconscious and cannot be controlled. Correct: While some NVC is unconscious (e.g. micro-expressions), much non-verbal behaviour can be consciously controlled. Politicians and actors deliberately manage their body language. Furthermore, NVC is ambiguous - crossed arms might mean defensiveness, or it might mean the person is cold. NVC must be interpreted in context alongside verbal communication, not treated as a lie detector.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the claim that non-verbal communication is more important than verbal communication in social interaction.

A grade 9 response will: describe types of NVC and their functions; present evidence for NVC importance (Mehrabian's study, universal facial expressions, personal space violations); evaluate Mehrabian (only applies to ambiguous emotional messages, not all communication); argue verbal is also crucial (abstract ideas, complex instructions, written language); conclude: NVC is essential for emotional and relational communication, but verbal language is essential for abstract and complex communication. They are complementary, not competitors - the importance depends on the type of message being communicated.
