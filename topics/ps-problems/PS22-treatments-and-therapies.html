Treatments & Therapies

Psychological and biological treatments: CBT, systematic desensitisation, drug therapy, and evaluating the effectiveness of different therapies for mental health problems.

Key Fact: CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy): identifies and challenges irrational/negative thoughts, tests them against evidence, and develops more realistic beliefs. Used for depression, anxiety, OCD, and phobias. Typical course: 6-20 sessions. Homework between sessions (thought records, behavioural experiments).
Key Fact: Systematic desensitisation (Wolpe): a behavioural therapy for phobias. Step 1: relaxation training (deep breathing, muscle relaxation). Step 2: construct anxiety hierarchy (least to most feared situations). Step 3: gradually expose patient to feared stimulus while maintaining relaxation, progressing up the hierarchy. Based on counterconditioning (replacing fear response with relaxation).
Key Fact: Drug therapy: SSRIs (e.g. Prozac) for depression - increase serotonin by blocking reuptake. Benzodiazepines (e.g. Valium) for anxiety - enhance GABA (inhibitory neurotransmitter) to calm brain activity. Antipsychotics (e.g. chlorpromazine) for schizophrenia - block dopamine receptors.
Key Fact: Evaluating CBT: strengths - addresses root causes (negative thinking), no side effects, skills for long-term management, effective for many conditions. Limitations - requires motivation and effort, not suitable for severe symptoms (too unwell to engage), limited therapist availability, takes time (weeks-months).
Key Fact: Evaluating drug therapy: strengths - rapid effect (especially benzodiazepines), easy to take, effective for many, widely available. Limitations - side effects (weight gain, insomnia, dependency for benzodiazepines), treats symptoms not causes, withdrawal effects, 30-40% don't respond, placebo effect is significant.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe how systematic desensitisation works and explain why it is effective for treating phobias.

Q2: Compare CBT and drug therapy as treatments for mental health problems.

Q3: Evaluate whether biological or psychological treatments are more effective for mental health problems.

✅ Answers

  1. Systematic desensitisation: (1) Teach relaxation techniques. (2) Build anxiety hierarchy (e.g. for spider phobia: picture -> toy spider -> small spider in jar -> large spider). (3) Patient relaxes while exposed to each step, moving up when anxiety is minimal. Effective because: it replaces the fear response with relaxation (counterconditioning), it's gradual (not overwhelming), and the patient maintains control. McGrath et al. found 75% success rate for phobias.
  2. CBT: addresses thinking patterns (root causes), teaches long-term skills, no side effects, effective for depression/anxiety. But: slow, requires effort, limited availability. Drug therapy: fast-acting, easy to prescribe, widely available. But: side effects, treats symptoms not causes, dependency risk (benzodiazepines), relapse when stopped. Combined: March et al. found CBT + SSRI most effective for adolescent depression.
  3. Neither is universally more effective - it depends on the condition and severity. Severe depression/schizophrenia: medication essential first. Mild-moderate depression/anxiety: CBT may be sufficient. Phobias: systematic desensitisation is highly effective. Combined approaches often work best. Evidence-based practice recommends matching treatment to the individual's specific needs and preferences.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Systematic desensitisation: 1) Relax, 2) Hierarchy, 3) Gradual exposure with relaxation. Replace FEAR with RELAXATION.
  • CBT = change THOUGHTS to change BEHAVIOUR and EMOTIONS. Drugs = change BRAIN CHEMISTRY to change mood.
  • Combined treatment (drugs + therapy) is often most effective for moderate-severe conditions.

📝 Exam Technique

Psychology Exam Tips:
When evaluating treatments, consider: does it address causes or symptoms? Are effects long-lasting? What are the side effects? Is it accessible? Use specific research evidence (March et al., McGrath et al.) to support evaluation.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Drug therapy is always the best treatment for mental health problems because it directly addresses the biological causes. Correct: While drug therapy addresses biological symptoms, it doesn't address the psychological and social factors that contribute to mental health problems. CBT is as effective as medication for mild-moderate depression and more effective at preventing relapse. For phobias, systematic desensitisation is more effective than drugs. The best treatment depends on the specific condition, severity, and individual preferences. A biopsychosocial approach suggests combined treatment is often most effective.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the effectiveness of psychological and biological treatments for mental health problems.

A grade 9 response will: describe CBT, systematic desensitisation, and drug therapy with mechanisms; evaluate each treatment (strengths and limitations with specific research evidence); compare effectiveness across conditions (drugs for severe, CBT for mild-moderate, combined for best results); discuss issues (placebo effect, individual differences, access to therapy); conclude: no single treatment is best for all conditions - effective treatment should be personalised, evidence-based, and where appropriate combine biological and psychological approaches. The biopsychosocial model supports integrated treatment.
