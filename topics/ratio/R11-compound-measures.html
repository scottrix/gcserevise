R11: Compound Measures

Foundation Higher All Boards

Compare lengths, areas and volumes using ratio notation and scale factors; make links to similarity

📋 Key Concepts

Definition: Compound measures involve two or more units combined, such as speed (distance/time), density (mass/volume), and pressure (force/area).

Key Formulae

MeasureFormulaUnits
SpeedSpeed = Distance ÷ Timem/s, km/h, mph
DensityDensity = Mass ÷ Volumekg/m³, g/cm³
PressurePressure = Force ÷ AreaN/m² (Pa)
Rate of PayRate = Pay ÷ Time£/hour

📝 Speed Problems

Distance = Speed × Time
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Time = Distance ÷ Speed
Example 1

A car travels 210 miles at an average speed of 60 mph. How long does the journey take?

Solution:

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Time = 210 ÷ 60 = 3.5 hours = 3 hours 30 minutes

Example 2

Convert 25 m/s to km/h.

Solution:

25 m/s = 25 × 60 × 60 = 90000 m/h

90000 m/h = 90 km/h

Or: 25 × 3.6 = 90 km/h

📝 Density Problems

Mass = Density × Volume
Density = Mass ÷ Volume
Volume = Mass ÷ Density
Example 3

A block of wood has volume 0.4 m³ and density 650 kg/m³. Find its mass.

Solution:

Mass = Density × Volume

Mass = 650 × 0.4 = 260 kg

Example 4

A 200g block of metal has dimensions 5cm × 4cm × 2cm. Calculate its density in g/cm³.

Solution:

Volume = 5 × 4 × 2 = 40 cm³

Density = Mass ÷ Volume

Density = 200 ÷ 40 = 5 g/cm³

📝 Pressure Problems

Force = Pressure × Area
Pressure = Force ÷ Area
Area = Force ÷ Pressure
Example 5

A force of 240 N acts on an area of 0.8 m². Calculate the pressure.

Solution:

Pressure = Force ÷ Area

Pressure = 240 ÷ 0.8 = 300 N/m² (or 300 Pa)

📝 Best Value Comparisons

Method: Calculate the unit price or rate to compare value for money.
Example 6

Which is better value: 2.5 kg for £4.00 or 3 kg for £5.10?

Solution:

Option A: £4.00 ÷ 2.5 = £1.60 per kg

Option B: £5.10 ÷ 3 = £1.70 per kg

Option A is better value (lower cost per kg)

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: A train travels 315 km in 3.5 hours. Calculate the average speed.

Q2: A liquid has density 1.2 g/cm³. What is the mass of 150 cm³?

Q3: Convert 72 km/h to m/s.

Q4: A force of 500 N creates a pressure of 2500 Pa. Find the area.

Q5: Which is better value: 400g for £3.20 or 600g for £4.50?

✅ Answers

  1. 90 km/h
  2. 180 g
  3. 20 m/s
  4. 0.2 m²
  5. 600g for £4.50 (£7.50/kg vs £8/kg)

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Use triangles to remember formulae (cover what you need)
  • Check units are consistent before calculating
  • km/h to m/s: divide by 3.6
  • m/s to km/h: multiply by 3.6
  • For best value, always find the cost per single unit
