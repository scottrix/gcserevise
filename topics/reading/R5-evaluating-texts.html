R5: Evaluating Texts

Foundation Higher All Boards AO4

Critically evaluating texts and supporting opinions with textual evidence.

📋 What is Evaluation?

Definition: Evaluation means making judgements about a text and supporting your opinions with evidence. You assess how successfully the writer achieves their purpose.

Evaluation involves:

  • Making a judgement about the text's effectiveness
  • Supporting your view with specific evidence
  • Considering how the text affects the reader
  • Analysing the writer's methods and choices
Key Principle:
There's no "right" opinion - but you must support your view with evidence from the text.

📝 Types of Evaluation Questions

1. "To What Extent..." Questions

Example Question

To what extent do you agree that the writer creates a sense of danger?

You should write about the writer's methods and support your ideas with quotations.

2. Statement Evaluation

Example Question

"The writer makes the reader feel sorry for the main character."

To what extent do you agree? Support your answer with detailed references to the text.

3. Overall Effectiveness

Example Question

How successfully does the writer convey their viewpoint?

🔍 How to Evaluate a Text

Step-by-Step Approach

Step 1: Decide your overall opinion - agree, disagree, or partially agree
Step 2: Find evidence that supports your view
Step 3: Analyse how the writer achieves the effect
Step 4: Consider alternative viewpoints if appropriate
Step 5: Reach a conclusion

Structuring Your Response

  • Start with a clear statement of your opinion
  • Use PEEL paragraphs to develop your argument
  • Include detailed analysis of methods
  • Use quotations to support your points
  • End with a reasoned conclusion
Example Evaluation

I largely agree that the writer creates a sense of danger. The opening establishes threat immediately through the metaphor "the shadows stalked him," personifying darkness as a predator. This is reinforced by short, punchy sentences like "He ran. Faster. Harder." which accelerate the pace and mirror his panic. However, the writer also creates moments of false security, such as the "deceptive calm" of the moonlit clearing, which actually heighten tension by suggesting danger could emerge at any moment.

📊 Key Skills for Evaluation

Using Evidence

  • Select relevant, specific quotations
  • Embed quotes naturally into your writing
  • Explain how the evidence supports your judgement
  • Use quotations throughout your response

Analysing Methods

  • Comment on language choices and effects
  • Consider structural techniques
  • Discuss tone and style
  • Link methods to the overall impact

Expressing Opinions

Phrase Type Examples
Agreement I agree that... / This effectively creates... / The writer successfully...
Partial agreement To some extent... / While... I also think... / However, this could also be seen as...
Disagreement I disagree because... / This is not convincing because... / The effect is actually...

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Correction
Just retelling the story Focus on evaluating, not summarising
Not giving an opinion Make a clear judgement
Lack of evidence Support every point with quotations
Only discussing language Consider structure and form too
Being too vague Use specific examples and analyse effects

📊 Mark Scheme Guidance

AQA Paper 1 Question 4 (20 marks)

  • Level 4 (17-20 marks): Perceptive evaluation with detailed analysis
  • Level 3 (13-16 marks): Clear evaluation with clear analysis
  • Level 2 (9-12 marks): Some evaluation with some comment
  • Level 1 (5-8 marks): Simple comment with limited support
  • Level 0 (0-4 marks): Nothing relevant or response not linked to text
Examiner's Tip: This is worth 20 marks - the most on the paper. Spend about 20-25 minutes on it. Make sure you give a clear opinion and back it up with detailed analysis of the text.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What does "evaluate" mean in the context of English Language?

Q2: How should you structure an evaluation response?

Q3: Why is it important to use evidence in evaluation?

Q4: What phrases can you use to show partial agreement?

✅ Answers

  1. To make a judgement about how effective or successful a text is, supported by evidence and analysis.
  2. Start with your opinion, use PEEL paragraphs with evidence and analysis, consider alternative views, and reach a conclusion.
  3. Evidence proves your opinion is based on the text rather than just personal preference - it shows you can support your ideas.
  4. "To some extent...", "While I agree that..., I also think...", "However, this could also be seen as..."

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Start by deciding your overall opinion
  • Make your judgement clear from the start
  • Use "I agree/disagree" language confidently
  • Support every point with specific evidence
  • Analyse language AND structure where relevant
  • Consider both sides for a balanced argument
  • This is a high-mark question - manage your time well
