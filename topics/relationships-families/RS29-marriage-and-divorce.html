RS29: Marriage and Divorce

All Boards

The nature and purpose of marriage; same-sex marriage and cohabitation; divorce and remarrying

💍 Marriage and Divorce

Key Fact: Christians and Muslims both see marriage as important, but have different teachings about divorce. Understanding these differences — and the diversity WITHIN each tradition — is essential for GCSE RS.

✝️ Christian Teachings on Marriage

  • Marriage is a sacrament in Catholic and Orthodox churches — a sign of God's grace
  • Jesus taught: "What God has joined together, let no one separate" (Mark 10:9)
  • Marriage vows include "till death us do part" — intended to be lifelong
  • The purpose of Christian marriage: companionship, procreation, support, and a symbol of Christ's love for the Church

✝️ Christian Teachings on Divorce

  • Catholic: Divorce is NOT allowed — marriage is a sacrament that cannot be dissolved. However, annulment is possible (declaring the marriage was never valid). Remarriage after divorce is not permitted.
  • Anglican: Divorce is recognised in civil law but is regretted. Remarriage in church is permitted at the minister's discretion.
  • Methodist/Baptist: Divorce is sometimes the lesser of two evils. Remarriage is generally permitted.

☪️ Muslim Teachings on Marriage and Divorce

  • Marriage (nikah) is a contract, not a sacrament — it can be dissolved
  • Muslim men may marry up to four wives, but only if they can treat them all equally (most Muslims practise monogamy)
  • Divorce is allowed as a last resort — "Of all permitted things, divorce is the most hated by Allah" (Hadith)
  • Talaq — a husband can initiate divorce by saying "talaq" three times
  • Khula — a wife can also initiate divorce, usually by returning the mahr (dowry)
  • There is a three-month waiting period (iddah) to ensure the wife is not pregnant and to allow reconciliation

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain contrasting Christian and Muslim views on divorce.

✅ Answers

  1. Catholics do not permit divorce because marriage is a sacrament and Jesus taught "let no one separate" what God has joined (Mark 10:9). However, annulment is possible if the marriage was never valid. In contrast, Islam allows divorce as a last resort. Marriage is a contract (nikah) rather than a sacrament, so it can be dissolved. Both husbands (talaq) and wives (khula) can initiate divorce, though there is a waiting period to allow reconciliation. Islam sees divorce as permitted but disliked by Allah.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Theme A questions: You must explain contrasting beliefs using Christianity AND Islam (or another religion). Consider non-religious views (humanist, atheist). Address the three required contrasts: contraception, premarital sex, same-sex relationships. Always refer to specific teachings and sources of authority.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often oversimplify religious views on relationships. Wrong: "All Christians oppose same-sex relationships." Correct: "While the Catholic Church teaches homosexual acts are wrong, many Protestant denominations (e.g. Quakers, United Reformed Church) accept and bless same-sex relationships. Views vary within and between traditions."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that the nature and purpose of marriage; same-sex marriage and cohabitation; divorce and remarrying.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
