👥 Contraception and Family Planning

Religious teachings on contraception; contrasting Christian and Muslim perspectives

Key Fact: This topic covers key aspects of contraception and family planning within the GCSE Religious Studies specification. You need to know specific teachings, be able to explain different perspectives, and evaluate the significance of beliefs and practices.

📋 Key Content

For Contraception and Family Planning, you must understand:

  • The main beliefs and teachings related to this topic
  • How different religious traditions interpret and practise these beliefs
  • The significance and impact of these beliefs on individuals and communities
  • Different and contrasting viewpoints within and between religions
  • How to evaluate religious and non-religious perspectives

Key sources of wisdom and authority relevant to this topic include scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders and community traditions. Always support your answers with specific references to these sources.

🔍 Different Perspectives

GCSE Religious Studies requires you to understand contrasting beliefs and teachings. For Contraception and Family Planning:

  • Consider different views within a religious tradition (e.g. Catholic vs Protestant, Sunni vs Shi'a)
  • Consider different views between religious traditions (e.g. Christian vs Muslim)
  • Consider non-religious perspectives (e.g. atheist, humanist, secular)
  • Acknowledge diversity of belief in contemporary British society

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain two beliefs about contraception and family planning.

Q2: Explain two contrasting views on contraception and family planning in contemporary British society.

Q3: "Religious belief about contraception and family planning is outdated." Evaluate this statement. [12 marks]

✅ Answers

  1. Belief 1: Explain a specific teaching or belief with reference to a source of authority. Belief 2: Explain a different or contrasting teaching, again with specific reference to scripture or tradition.
  2. View 1: One religious perspective with specific teaching. View 2: A contrasting religious or non-religious perspective, acknowledging diversity in contemporary British society.
  3. Evaluation: Consider religious arguments (scripture, tradition, authority), non-religious arguments (science, secularism, human rights), and reach a reasoned conclusion. Top marks require analysis of both sides and a justified judgement.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Theme A questions: You must explain contrasting beliefs using Christianity AND Islam (or another religion). Consider non-religious views (humanist, atheist). Address the three required contrasts: contraception, premarital sex, same-sex relationships. Always refer to specific teachings and sources of authority.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often oversimplify religious views on relationships. Wrong: "All Christians oppose same-sex relationships." Correct: "While the Catholic Church teaches homosexual acts are wrong, many Protestant denominations (e.g. Quakers, United Reformed Church) accept and bless same-sex relationships. Views vary within and between traditions."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that religious teachings on contraception; contrasting christian and muslim perspectives.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
