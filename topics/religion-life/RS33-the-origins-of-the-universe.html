RS33: The Origins of the Universe

All Boards

Religious teachings about origins; the Big Bang theory; the relationship between scientific and religious views

🌍 The Origins of the Universe

Key Fact: GCSE RS requires you to understand both scientific and religious explanations for the origins of the universe, and how they relate to each other.

🔬 The Big Bang Theory

  • The universe began approximately 13.8 billion years ago from a single point of infinite density
  • This point expanded rapidly, creating space, time, matter and energy
  • Evidence: cosmic microwave background radiation, the ongoing expansion of the universe, and the abundance of light elements (hydrogen and helium)
  • The Big Bang is the accepted scientific explanation for the origin of the universe

✝️ Christian Teachings

  • Literalist/Creationist: God created the universe in six 24-hour days as described in Genesis 1; the Big Bang is wrong
  • Non-literalist/Liberal: Genesis teaches theological truths (God is creator) but not science; the Big Bang may be HOW God created — God caused the Big Bang
  • The Catholic Church accepts the Big Bang — Pope Pius XII (1951) said it supports the idea of a Creator
  • Many Christians see no conflict: science explains the mechanism; religion explains the meaning and purpose

☪️ Muslim Teachings

  • The Qur'an describes creation: "He is the Originator of the heavens and the earth" (Surah 6:101)
  • Most Muslims accept the Big Bang as compatible with Islam — if Allah created everything, he created the Big Bang
  • The Qur'an describes the universe as once being "one united whole" that was "split apart" (Surah 21:30) — some see this as referencing the Big Bang
  • Muslims believe Allah is the ultimate cause regardless of the mechanism

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain how the Big Bang theory can be compatible with religious belief.

✅ Answers

  1. Many Christians and Muslims accept the Big Bang as the scientific explanation for HOW the universe began, while believing that God/Allah was the cause behind it. The Catholic Church officially accepts the Big Bang, and the Qur'an's description of the universe being "split apart" (Surah 21:30) can be read as referencing the Big Bang. Science explains the mechanism; religion explains the meaning and purpose. The two address different questions and are not necessarily in conflict.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Theme B questions: You must explain contrasting beliefs using Christianity AND Islam (or another religion). Address the three required contrasts: abortion, euthanasia, animal experimentation. Consider scientific perspectives (Big Bang, evolution) alongside religious views. Use the sanctity of life and quality of life as key evaluative concepts.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often confuse sanctity of life with quality of life. Wrong: "Sanctity of life means life should have good quality." Correct: "Sanctity of life means all life is sacred because it is God-given and has intrinsic value. Quality of life refers to the standard of health, comfort and happiness. They are different principles that can lead to different ethical conclusions."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that religious teachings about origins; the big bang theory; the relationship between scientific and religious views.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
