RS35: The Origins of Human Life

All Boards

Religious teachings about the origins of human life; evolution and religious views

🧬 The Origins of Human Life

Key Fact: GCSE RS requires you to understand both the scientific theory of evolution and religious responses to it, including whether they can be compatible.

🔬 Evolution by Natural Selection

  • Charles Darwin proposed that life evolved over billions of years through natural selection
  • Organisms with beneficial traits survive and reproduce; harmful traits are eliminated
  • All life shares a common ancestor; humans evolved from earlier hominids
  • Evolution is the accepted scientific explanation for the diversity of life

✝️ Christian Responses to Evolution

  • Creationist: Evolution is wrong — God created each species directly as described in Genesis
  • Theistic evolution: Evolution is the HOW; God is the WHO. God used evolution as his method of creation. This is the Catholic Church's position.
  • Intelligent Design: Evolution happened but was guided by God; the complexity of life points to a designer
  • Many Christians see no conflict between Genesis and evolution — Genesis teaches WHY God created, not the scientific mechanism

☪️ Muslim Responses to Evolution

  • Most Muslims accept some form of evolution for animals and plants
  • However, most believe humans were created specially by Allah — "We created man from sounding clay" (Surah 15:26)
  • Some Muslim scholars accept human evolution, arguing Allah could have used any process
  • The key belief: whatever the process, Allah is the Creator

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain how some religious believers accept the theory of evolution.

✅ Answers

  1. Many Christians accept theistic evolution — the idea that God used evolution as his method of creation. The Catholic Church supports this view, teaching that evolution is the scientific mechanism and God is the ultimate cause. Similarly, many Muslims accept evolution for animals and plants while believing humans were specially created. Both traditions can accept evolution because it explains HOW life developed, while religion explains WHO caused it and WHY. The two address different questions.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always refer to specific sources of authority (scripture, sacred texts, religious leaders)
  • Explain contrasting beliefs — different views within and between traditions
  • Use key terminology precisely (e.g. tawhid, atonement, sanctity of life)
  • For 12-mark evaluation questions, consider both sides before reaching a justified conclusion
  • Link beliefs to their impact on individuals, communities and society
  • Acknowledge diversity — no religious tradition has a single, uniform view

📝 Exam Technique

RS Exam Tips:
For Theme B questions: You must explain contrasting beliefs using Christianity AND Islam (or another religion). Address the three required contrasts: abortion, euthanasia, animal experimentation. Consider scientific perspectives (Big Bang, evolution) alongside religious views. Use the sanctity of life and quality of life as key evaluative concepts.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often confuse sanctity of life with quality of life. Wrong: "Sanctity of life means life should have good quality." Correct: "Sanctity of life means all life is sacred because it is God-given and has intrinsic value. Quality of life refers to the standard of health, comfort and happiness. They are different principles that can lead to different ethical conclusions."

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

12 marks: Evaluate the view that religious teachings about the origins of human life; evolution and religious views.

A strong 12-mark answer will: (1) Present a religious viewpoint with specific teaching/scripture; (2) Present a contrasting religious or non-religious viewpoint; (3) Analyse the strengths and weaknesses of each position; (4) Reach a justified conclusion. For top marks, show awareness of diversity within traditions, use precise terminology, and demonstrate independent critical thinking rather than just rehearsing arguments.

Mark scheme: AO1 (knowledge and understanding) = 6 marks; AO2 (analysis and evaluation) = 6 marks

📊 AO Deep Dive

Assessment Objective Analysis

GCSE Religious Studies tests two AOs equally: AO1 (Knowledge and Understanding, 50%) — demonstrate knowledge of beliefs, teachings and practices; use specialist vocabulary; refer to sources of wisdom and authority. AO2 (Analysis and Evaluation, 50%) — analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief; consider contrasting views; construct well-reasoned and balanced arguments; reach justified conclusions. For grade 9, demonstrate nuanced understanding of diversity within traditions, use precise scriptural references, and show independent critical evaluation. The key difference between grade 5 and grade 9 is the quality of evaluation — not just describing views but critically analysing their strengths and weaknesses.
