Power, Authority & the State

How power is distributed: Weber's types of authority, the role of the state, and how power relations affect different social groups.

Key Fact: Power: the ability to make others do what you want. Lukes identified three dimensions: decision-making, agenda-setting, and ideological power.
Key Fact: Weber's three types of authority: Traditional (custom - monarchy), Charismatic (personal qualities - MLK), Rational-legal (rules and laws - elected officials). Most modern authority is rational-legal.
Key Fact: The state: institutions that govern (government, parliament, police, courts). Marxists: the state serves ruling class interests. Functionalists: the state serves collective interests. Pluralists: the state balances competing interests.
Key Fact: Power elites: Mills argued power is concentrated in a small group of corporate leaders, military chiefs and politicians. In the UK, the 'establishment' (Eton, Oxbridge) dominates politics, media and business.
Key Fact: Democracy and power: formal equality through elections but real power is unequal: wealthy fund parties, media owners shape opinion, corporations influence policy, and voter turnout has declined.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Describe Weber's three types of authority and evaluate their usefulness.

Q2: Compare Marxist and Functionalist views of the state.

Q3: Evaluate whether democracy ensures that power is equally distributed.

✅ Answers

  1. Traditional: custom (monarchy). Charismatic: personality (MLK). Rational-legal: rules (elected officials). Evaluation: useful but some leaders combine types. Charismatic brings change but is unstable; rational-legal is stable but may resist change.
  2. Marxist: state serves ruling class (protects property, maintains capitalist order). Functionalist: state serves everyone (maintains order, provides services). Evidence supports Marxist view but the state also provides welfare that benefits the working class.
  3. Democracy gives formal equality (one vote) but real power is unequal: wealth funds parties, media shapes opinion, lobbyists influence policy. Marxists: democracy is a sham. Pluralists: democracy works through competing groups. Conclusion: some distribution but significant inequalities remain.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Weber's 3 types: Traditional (custom), Charismatic (personality), Rational-legal (rules).
  • Marxist: state = tool of ruling class. Functionalist: state = serves everyone.
  • Democracy = formal equality but REAL power is unequal (money, media, lobbying).

📝 Exam Technique

Sociology Exam Tips:
When discussing power, always: distinguish power from authority, use Weber's typology, and evaluate whether power is democratically distributed.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: In a democracy, power is equally distributed because everyone has one vote. Correct: While everyone has one vote, real political power is very unequal: wealthy donors, media owners, corporate lobbyists, and establishment networks dominate. Lukes' third dimension shows the most powerful influence is shaping what people WANT. Voting equality is necessary but not sufficient for genuine democratic power.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the view that power in modern Britain is held by a small elite rather than democratically distributed.

A grade 9 response will: present elite theory evidence (Mills, establishment networks); present democratic counter-evidence (suffrage, free press, unions); evaluate: formal democracy exists but is undermined by economic inequality; conclude: real power is disproportionately held by a small elite. Reform could make democracy more genuine.
