Gender & Inequality

Gender as a form of stratification: the gender pay gap, the glass ceiling, gender roles, and Feminist explanations of gender inequality.

Key Fact: The gender pay gap: women earn about 8% less per hour (2023). Causes: lower-paid occupations, part-time work, career breaks, glass ceiling. The gap is wider for older women and ethnic minority women.
Key Fact: The glass ceiling: invisible barriers preventing women from reaching top positions. Only 10% of FTSE 100 CEOs are women. The 'glass cliff': women more likely to be appointed during crises.
Key Fact: Gender roles: girls socialised into caring/nurturing, boys into active/independent. Oakley: gender roles are socially constructed. This affects subject choices, career choices, domestic roles.
Key Fact: Feminist perspectives: Liberal (reform through legislation), Marxist (capitalism + patriarchy), Radical (patriarchy is primary oppression), Difference (intersectionality of class, ethnicity, sexuality).
Key Fact: Progress: legislation, girls outperform boys in education. Remaining: 8% pay gap, glass ceiling, domestic violence (1 in 4 women), women do twice as much unpaid care work, 35% of MPs.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain why the gender pay gap exists and evaluate whether legislation can eliminate it.

Q2: Compare different Feminist perspectives on gender inequality.

Q3: Evaluate whether gender inequality has been significantly reduced in modern Britain.

✅ Answers

  1. The gap exists because: occupational segregation, part-time work, career breaks, glass ceiling, discrimination. Legislation has reduced but not eliminated it because laws address direct discrimination but not structural factors.
  2. Liberal: reform through law - effective but slow. Marxist: capitalism + patriarchy - need to overthrow both. Radical: patriarchy is root cause - need fundamental restructuring. Difference: women are not one group - intersectionality matters.
  3. Progress: girls outperform boys, equal pay legislation, more women in professional jobs. Remaining: 8% pay gap, glass ceiling, domestic violence, care work, under-representation. Conclusion: significant progress but full equality not achieved.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Gender pay gap = 8%. Causes: occupation, part-time, motherhood penalty, glass ceiling.
  • Glass ceiling = barrier to TOP jobs. Glass cliff = women appointed during CRISIS.
  • Progress YES. Full equality NO. Formal equality exists; substantive equality is incomplete.

📝 Exam Technique

Sociology Exam Tips:
When evaluating gender inequality, always: use specific statistics, distinguish formal from substantive equality, and consider intersectionality.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Gender inequality no longer exists because women have equal rights under the law. Correct: While legal equality exists, substantive inequality persists: 8% pay gap, only 10% of FTSE 100 CEOs are women, women do twice as much unpaid care work, domestic violence affects 1 in 4 women. Legal equality is necessary but not sufficient.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the view that gender inequality is the most significant form of inequality in modern Britain.

A grade 9 response will: present evidence for gender inequality; compare with class and ethnic inequality; consider intersectionality; conclude: gender inequality is significant but cannot be isolated from class and ethnic inequality. No single form is 'most significant' - they interact and compound each other.
