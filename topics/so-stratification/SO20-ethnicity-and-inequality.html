Ethnicity & Inequality

Ethnicity as stratification: racial inequality in employment, education, crime and justice. Institutional racism and Islamophobia.

Key Fact: Ethnic inequality in employment: ethnic minorities earn less on average. Wood's CV study: identical CVs, 'White' names got 74% more callbacks. This proves discrimination operates in hiring.
Key Fact: Institutional racism (Macpherson Report, 1999): 'the collective failure of an organisation to provide an appropriate service to people because of their colour, culture or ethnic origin'. Found the Met Police was institutionally racist.
Key Fact: Ethnicity and crime: Black people are 9x more likely to be stopped and searched; 12% of prison vs 3% of population. Self-report studies show similar offending rates - the disparity reflects policing bias.
Key Fact: Islamophobia: prejudice against Muslims increased after 9/11 and 7/7. Muslims face employment discrimination, media stereotyping, and rising hate crime.
Key Fact: Anti-racist legislation (Race Relations Act 1976, Equality Act 2010) has improved formal equality but not eliminated discrimination.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: Explain what institutional racism is and evaluate its usefulness.

Q2: Describe the evidence for ethnic inequality in employment and crime.

Q3: Evaluate whether legislation has effectively reduced ethnic inequality.

✅ Answers

  1. Institutional racism: collective failure of an organisation due to race (Macpherson). Found in police, education, CJS. Useful: explains systemic patterns without individual racist intent. Criticism: can be too vague.
  2. Employment: Wood's CV study proved discrimination (74% more callbacks for White names). Crime: Black people 9x more likely to be stopped; 12% of prison vs 3% of population. Causes: discrimination, institutional racism, socio-economic factors.
  3. Legislation has improved formal equality but discrimination persists (CV studies, stop and search). Legislation addresses individual discrimination but not structural inequality. Conclusion: necessary but not sufficient.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Institutional racism = SYSTEMIC bias (Macpherson/Stephen Lawrence). Not just individual prejudice.
  • Employment: CV study PROVES discrimination (White names = 74% more callbacks).
  • Legislation necessary but NOT sufficient: laws change faster than attitudes and structures.

📝 Exam Technique

Sociology Exam Tips:
When discussing ethnic inequality, always: distinguish individual prejudice from institutional racism, use specific evidence, and evaluate legislation's effectiveness.

⚠️ Common Errors

Watch Out!

Students often make mistakes here. Wrong: Ethnic inequality is caused by cultural differences rather than discrimination. Correct: Wood's CV study proved identical qualifications received different responses based on ethnic names; stop and search data shows policing bias; the Macpherson Report identified institutional racism. Cultural explanations risk blaming the victim.

✍️ Model Answer

Full-Mark Response

Evaluate the view that institutional racism is the main cause of ethnic inequality in modern Britain.

A grade 9 response will: define institutional racism; present evidence (stop and search, CV studies); evaluate: it explains systemic patterns; counter-arguments: socio-economic factors also contribute; some ethnic groups achieve above average; conclude: institutional racism is significant but interacts with class and cultural factors. The most effective approach addresses all three.
