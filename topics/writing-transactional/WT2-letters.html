WT2: Letters

Foundation Higher All Boards AO5 AO6

Writing formal and informal letters - understanding structure, register, and conventions.

📋 Types of Letters

Two main types: Formal letters (to officials, businesses, newspapers) and Informal letters (to friends and family). The exam usually requires formal letters.

Formal Letters

Written to people you don't know or in professional contexts:

  • To a newspaper (letter to the editor)
  • To a local council or MP
  • To a company or business
  • To a headteacher or school

Informal Letters

Written to people you know well:

  • To friends or family
  • Personal correspondence
  • Thank you letters
  • Personal invitations

📝 Formal Letter Structure

Formal Letter Layout
Your Address                    Date

Recipient's Name
Recipient's Address

Dear [Name/Title],

[Content - Introduction, main body, conclusion]

Yours sincerely/faithfully,
[Your Name]

Opening the Letter

Salutations:
  • Dear Mr/Mrs/Ms [Surname] - if you know the name
  • Dear Sir/Madam - if you don't know the name
  • To the Editor - for letters to newspapers

Closing the Letter

Sign-offs:
  • "Yours sincerely" - if you used a name (Dear Mr Smith)
  • "Yours faithfully" - if you used Dear Sir/Madam

🎯 Writing a Formal Letter

The Introduction

State clearly why you are writing:

Example Openings

"I am writing to express my concern about..."

"I am writing to complain about the service I received..."

"I am writing in response to your article about..."

The Main Body

  • Present your points logically
  • Use one paragraph per main point
  • Support with evidence and examples
  • Use formal, persuasive language

The Conclusion

  • Summarise your main points
  • State what action you want
  • End politely but firmly
Example Conclusions

"I trust you will give this matter urgent attention."

"I look forward to your prompt response."

"Please reconsider this decision in light of the evidence presented."

✍️ Formal vs Informal Language

Feature Formal Informal
Vocabulary Sophisticated, precise Everyday, casual
Contractions Do not, cannot, it is Don't, can't, it's
Sentences Complex, varied Shorter, simpler
Tone Respectful, objective Personal, emotional
Sign-off Yours sincerely Best wishes, Love from
Top Tip: In exams, you're almost always asked to write a formal letter. Avoid contractions and slang. Use sophisticated vocabulary appropriately.

📰 Letters to the Editor

A common exam task - writing to a newspaper about an article or issue.

Purpose: To express an opinion, agree/disagree with an article, or raise awareness about an issue.
Example Opening

Dear Editor,

I am writing in response to the article "School Uniform Should Be Banned" published on 15th March. While I understand the arguments presented, I strongly disagree with the conclusion reached.

Key Features

  • Reference the article you're responding to
  • State your viewpoint clearly
  • Use persuasive techniques
  • Keep to the word limit (usually concise)
  • End with a powerful closing statement

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It's Wrong How to Fix It
Wrong sign-off "Sincerely" with Sir/Madam is incorrect Use "faithfully" with Sir/Madam
Using contractions Too informal for formal letters Write "do not" instead of "don't"
Forgetting addresses Loses marks for format Include sender and recipient addresses
Starting with "Hi" Too informal for formal letters Use "Dear" followed by name/title
Wrong tone Rude or overly casual Be respectful and formal

📊 Mark Scheme Guidance

AO5: Content and Organisation (24 marks)

  • Clear purpose and viewpoint
  • Well-structured paragraphs
  • Appropriate register for audience
  • Effective use of persuasive techniques

AO6: Technical Accuracy (16 marks)

  • Correct letter format
  • Varied sentence structures
  • Accurate spelling and punctuation
  • Appropriate formal vocabulary
Examiner's Tip: Get the format right straight away. Write your address, skip a line, write the date, then the recipient's address. This sets up your letter correctly and shows you understand the conventions.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: When should you use "Yours faithfully" versus "Yours sincerely"?

Q2: What is the correct order for a formal letter's components?

Q3: Write the opening of a letter to your local council about litter in your area.

Q4: Why should you avoid contractions in formal letters?

✅ Answers

  1. "Yours faithfully" with Dear Sir/Madam; "Yours sincerely" when using a specific name (Dear Mr Smith).
  2. Sender's address, date, recipient's address, salutation, content, sign-off, signature.
  3. Your opening should include: sender's address, date, council's address, "Dear Sir/Madam", and a clear statement of purpose.
  4. Contractions are too informal for formal writing. Writing "do not" instead of "don't" maintains a professional, respectful tone.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Always check if formal or informal is required
  • Set out addresses correctly - they're easy marks
  • Never use contractions in formal letters
  • Match your sign-off to your salutation
  • State your purpose clearly in the first paragraph
  • Use persuasive techniques appropriate to your purpose
  • Keep paragraphs focused - one main idea each
