WT3: Speeches

Foundation Higher All Boards AO5 AO6

Writing engaging speeches for different audiences - using rhetorical devices to persuade and inspire.

📋 What Makes a Good Speech?

Key Features: A good speech is memorable, persuasive, and connects emotionally with the audience. It uses rhetorical devices to create impact and is designed to be heard, not just read.

Effective speeches:

  • Hook the audience from the opening
  • Use direct address to create connection
  • Employ rhetorical devices for memorability
  • Have a clear, logical structure
  • End with a powerful, memorable conclusion
Speech Success Formula:
Strong opening + Clear structure + Rhetorical devices + Direct address + Memorable ending = Effective speech

🎯 Key Speech Techniques

1. Direct Address

Speak directly to your audience using personal pronouns.

Examples

"Today, we stand together..."

"You have the power to make a difference."

"Friends, colleagues, citizens..."

2. Rhetorical Questions

Questions that make the audience think and reflect.

Examples

"What kind of world do we want to leave for our children?"

"Ask yourself: is this acceptable?"

"How long must we wait for change?"

3. Repetition and Anaphora

Repeating words or phrases for emphasis and rhythm.

Examples

"We will not give up. We will not back down. We will succeed."

"I have a dream that one day... I have a dream that..."

4. The Rule of Three

Grouping three ideas for impact and memorability.

Examples

"Government of the people, by the people, for the people."

"Education. Opportunity. Hope."

5. Emotive Language

Words that evoke strong emotional responses.

Examples

"Innocent children suffer while we stand by and watch."

"Together, we can build a brighter, fairer, stronger future."

6. Imperatives

Commands that urge action.

Examples

"Stand up for what you believe in!"

"Make your voice heard!"

"Join us today!"

📝 Speech Structure

The Classic Structure

Introduction (Opening):

  • Hook: Start with a striking statement, question, or anecdote
  • Establish your credibility and purpose
  • Connect with your audience

Main Body:

  • Present 3-4 key points
  • Use one paragraph per point
  • Support with evidence and examples
  • Include rhetorical devices throughout
  • Address counter-arguments if needed

Conclusion (Ending):

  • Summarise your main message
  • End with a powerful call to action
  • Leave a lasting impression

🎤 Types of Speeches

Persuasive Speeches

Aim to convince the audience to agree with your viewpoint or take action.

  • Use strong rhetorical devices
  • Appeal to both logic and emotion
  • Include clear calls to action

Debate Speeches

Argue for or against a motion in a formal debate setting.

  • Address the chair: "Chair, ladies and gentlemen..."
  • Present structured arguments
  • Rebut opposing views
  • Conclude with a summary

Motivational Speeches

Inspire and encourage your audience.

  • Use uplifting, positive language
  • Share personal stories or examples
  • End with an inspiring call to action
Example Opening

"Good morning, everyone. Imagine standing at the edge of a cliff, looking out at an uncertain future. That's where we find ourselves today. But I'm here to tell you that together, we can build a bridge to that future - if we have the courage to take the first step."

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It's Wrong How to Fix It
Writing for reading, not speaking Speeches should sound natural when spoken Read your work aloud to check rhythm
Weak opening Loses audience attention immediately Start with a hook - question, statement, anecdote
No direct address Feels disconnected from audience Use "we", "you", "us" frequently
Too many complex sentences Difficult to follow when heard Use varied but clear sentence structures
Forgetting the call to action Speech lacks purpose and impact End with clear action you want audience to take

📊 Mark Scheme Guidance

AO5: Content and Organisation (24 marks)

  • Clear viewpoint and purpose
  • Effective speech conventions (direct address, etc.)
  • Well-structured with clear introduction and conclusion
  • Range of rhetorical devices used appropriately

AO6: Technical Accuracy (16 marks)

  • Varied sentence structures for effect
  • Wide vocabulary range
  • Accurate spelling and punctuation
  • Appropriate tone for audience
Examiner's Tip: A speech needs to sound like a speech. Use techniques like direct address, rhetorical questions, and repetition. Your writing should feel different from an essay - it should have rhythm and be memorable when spoken aloud.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What is the purpose of a "hook" in a speech opening?

Q2: Name three rhetorical devices commonly used in speeches.

Q3: Write an opening paragraph for a speech about the importance of voting.

Q4: Why is direct address important in speeches?

✅ Answers

  1. A hook grabs the audience's attention immediately, making them want to listen. It could be a question, striking statement, or anecdote.
  2. Rhetorical questions, repetition, rule of three, emotive language, direct address, imperatives - any three of these.
  3. Your opening should include: a greeting, a hook (question or statement), direct address, and clear indication of the topic.
  4. Direct address creates a connection with the audience, making them feel involved and personally addressed. It transforms a speech from a lecture into a conversation.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Start with a strong hook - grab attention immediately
  • Use direct address throughout: "we", "you", "us"
  • Include a range of rhetorical devices
  • Vary your sentence lengths for rhythm and impact
  • End with a powerful, memorable conclusion
  • Include a clear call to action
  • Read your work aloud to check it sounds like a speech
