WT5: Reviews

Foundation Higher All Boards AO5 AO6

Writing reviews of films, books, products, etc. - balancing judgment with evidence and recommendations.

📋 What is a Review?

Definition: A review evaluates a product, service, or creative work, providing a judgment supported by evidence to help readers make informed decisions.

Reviews can cover:

  • Films and television shows
  • Books and literature
  • Restaurants and food
  • Music albums and concerts
  • Video games
  • Theatre productions
  • Products and services
Review Formula:
Overview + Evaluation + Evidence + Judgment + Recommendation = Effective Review

📝 Review Structure

1. Introduction

Introduce what you're reviewing and provide essential context.

Include:
  • Title and type of work/product
  • Key details (author, director, release date)
  • A brief overview of what it's about
  • An engaging hook to interest readers
Example Introduction

"Christopher Nolan's 'Oppenheimer' (2023) is a three-hour epic that chronicles the life of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the physicist behind the atomic bomb. It's a film that demands your attention - and rewards it magnificently."

2. Main Body

Provide your detailed evaluation with supporting evidence.

Key Elements:
  • Discuss specific aspects (acting, directing, plot, etc.)
  • Use examples to illustrate your points
  • Balance positive and negative aspects
  • Use new paragraphs for different aspects

3. Conclusion

Summarise your judgment and give a recommendation.

Include:
  • Clear overall judgment
  • Who would enjoy this
  • Star rating or recommendation
  • Final memorable comment
Example Conclusion

"Despite its lengthy runtime, 'Oppenheimer' is a masterpiece of modern cinema. Essential viewing for history buffs and film lovers alike. ★★★★★"

🎯 Key Review Elements

Judgment

State your opinion clearly and confidently.

Judgment Examples

"This is a triumph of storytelling."

"Disappointingly, the film falls short of its potential."

"An outstanding debut that marks this author as one to watch."

Evidence

Support every judgment with specific examples.

Evidence Examples

"The opening sequence, with its haunting visuals, immediately establishes the tone."

"Particularly impressive is the lead performance, which conveys vulnerability and strength simultaneously."

Balanced View

Acknowledge both strengths and weaknesses for credibility.

Useful Phrases:
  • "While the pacing occasionally drags..."
  • "On the positive side..."
  • "However, this is balanced by..."
  • "For all its flaws..."

Recommendation

Help readers decide if this is for them.

Recommendation Examples

"Highly recommended for fans of historical drama."

"Best avoided if you prefer fast-paced action."

"Worth seeing for the performances alone."

✍️ Writing Style for Reviews

Feature Description Example
Descriptive language Paint a picture for readers "Visually stunning, with sweeping cinematography"
Clear judgments Don't be vague - be direct "This is exceptional" not "It's quite good"
Specific examples Back up every claim "The restaurant's signature dish..."
Appropriate tone Match the publication style Formal for broadsheets, casual for blogs
Engaging voice Make it enjoyable to read Use wit, personality, and flair
Top Tip: Be honest but fair. A review that's overly negative or gushing without substance won't be taken seriously. Balance your judgment with specific evidence.

📺 Types of Reviews

Film Reviews

  • Discuss: plot, acting, directing, cinematography, soundtrack
  • Avoid major spoilers
  • Mention genre and target audience

Book Reviews

  • Discuss: plot, characters, writing style, themes
  • Don't give away the ending
  • Quote memorable passages

Restaurant Reviews

  • Discuss: food quality, service, atmosphere, value
  • Mention specific dishes
  • Consider practical details (price, booking)
Full Review Example (Book)

"The Midnight Library" by Matt Haig (2020)

Matt Haig's "The Midnight Library" is a thought-provoking exploration of regret and possibility. The premise - a library between life and death where each book represents a different life you could have lived - is both imaginative and deeply human.

The novel's greatest strength lies in its central character, Nora Seed, whose journey through countless alternative lives is both heartbreaking and hopeful. Haig writes with empathy and insight, making philosophical concepts accessible without being patronising.

However, some readers may find the pacing uneven, with certain life explorations feeling rushed while others linger unnecessarily. The message about appreciating your current life, while valuable, is somewhat predictable.

Despite these minor flaws, "The Midnight Library" is a beautifully crafted novel that will resonate with anyone who has ever wondered "what if?" Recommended for fans of thoughtful, uplifting fiction. ★★★★☆

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It's Wrong How to Fix It
Just summarising the plot A review needs evaluation, not just description Always add your judgment and evidence
Being too vague "It was good" means nothing Use specific examples to support opinions
Major spoilers Ruins the experience for readers Warn readers or avoid key plot reveals
One-sided view Lacks credibility Acknowledge both strengths and weaknesses
No recommendation Readers don't know what to do State who would enjoy this and why

📊 Mark Scheme Guidance

AO5: Content and Organisation (24 marks)

  • Clear judgment with supporting evidence
  • Well-structured with introduction and conclusion
  • Balanced evaluation of strengths and weaknesses
  • Engaging style appropriate for publication

AO6: Technical Accuracy (16 marks)

  • Varied sentence structures
  • Descriptive, engaging vocabulary
  • Accurate spelling and punctuation
  • Appropriate tone for audience
Examiner's Tip: Every judgment needs evidence. Don't just say "the acting was good" - explain which actor, which scene, and why it was effective. Specific examples make your review credible and engaging.

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What are the four key elements of an effective review?

Q2: Why should you avoid major spoilers in a film or book review?

Q3: Write an introduction for a review of a restaurant you've visited.

Q4: What's the difference between a summary and a review?

✅ Answers

  1. Judgment (your opinion), Evidence (specific examples), Balanced view (pros and cons), Recommendation (who should see/read/use it).
  2. Spoilers ruin the experience for readers who haven't seen the film or read the book. You want to inform their decision, not reveal the ending.
  3. Your introduction should include: the restaurant name, location/cuisine type, and an engaging hook that hints at your overall judgment.
  4. A summary describes what happens. A review evaluates how good it is, with judgment, evidence, and recommendations.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Start with a hook that engages your reader
  • State your judgment clearly and early
  • Support every opinion with specific examples
  • Balance positive and negative points
  • Avoid major spoilers in film and book reviews
  • End with a clear recommendation
  • Consider a star rating or similar summary
