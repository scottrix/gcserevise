W2: Essay Structure

Foundation Higher All Boards AO1 AO4

Organising literature essays effectively - introductions, paragraphs, and conclusions.

📋 Why Structure Matters

Good Structure:

  • Shows you can organise ideas logically
  • Helps examiner follow your argument
  • Ensures you cover all parts of the question
  • Keeps you focused, prevents rambling
AO4 (5-10%) covers spelling, punctuation, and structure. A well-organised essay scores higher.

📝 The Basic Structure

Three Parts

td>Set up your argument
Part Length Purpose
Introduction 2-3 sentences
Body Paragraphs 3-5 paragraphs Develop your argument with evidence
Conclusion 2-3 sentences Sum up and evaluate
Essay Formula:
Introduction → Point 1 → Point 2 → Point 3 → Conclusion

🎯 Writing Introductions

What an Introduction Does

  • Identifies the text and writer
  • Responds to the question
  • States your main argument

Introduction Formula

  1. Name - text and writer
  2. Context - if relevant
  3. Thesis - your main argument
Introduction Example

Question: How does Shakespeare present Macbeth's ambition?

Introduction: In "Macbeth," Shakespeare presents ambition as a destructive force that leads to moral corruption and eventual downfall. Through Macbeth's soliloquies and interactions with Lady Macbeth, we see how ambition transforms a noble warrior into a tyrannical murderer.

What to Avoid

  • Don't retell the plot
  • Don't give a biography of the writer
  • Don't start with "In this essay I will..."

📊 PEEL Paragraphs

The PEEL Structure

Each body paragraph should follow PEEL:

Letter What What You Do
P Point Make a clear argument
E Evidence Quote from the text
E Explain Analyse the quotation
L Link Connect to question/theme/context

Example PEEL Paragraph

PEEL in Action

P: Shakespeare presents Macbeth's ambition as immediately destructive to his moral character.

E: Macbeth acknowledges his "vaulting ambition, which o'erleaps itself / And falls on th'other."

E: The metaphor of horse-riding suggests ambition is reckless - it leaps without caution. "O'erleaps itself" implies ambition exceeds its own bounds, while "falls" foreshadows Macbeth's eventual downfall.

L: This establishes the tragic pattern of the play: ambition leads to overreaching, which leads to destruction.

🔗 Linking Paragraphs

Why Link?

Linking creates a COHESIVE essay - one that flows rather than jumping between points.

Linking Words

Type Examples
Adding Furthermore, Additionally, Moreover
Contrasting However, Conversely, In contrast
Developing Building on this, This deepens when
Concluding Ultimately, In conclusion, Therefore
Linking in Practice

End of Paragraph 1: ...shows Macbeth's initial hesitation.

Start of Paragraph 2: However, this hesitation disappears after the murder, and Macbeth becomes increasingly ruthless.

📝 Writing Conclusions

What a Conclusion Does

  • Summarises your main points
  • Evaluates the writer's methods
  • Offers a final insight

Conclusion Formula

  1. Summarise: Restate main argument
  2. Evaluate: Comment on writer's effectiveness
  3. Context: Link to broader significance
Conclusion Example

Shakespeare presents ambition as a corrupting force that destroys Macbeth's humanity. Through soliloquies, imagery, and contrast with other characters, Shakespeare shows how unchecked ambition leads to isolation, tyranny, and death. For a Jacobean audience, this reinforced the dangers of overreaching and the importance of accepting one's place in the divine order.

What to Avoid

  • Don't introduce new points
  • Don't just repeat the introduction
  • Don't say "In conclusion" - just conclude

📐 Different Question Types

Character Questions

Structure:

  • Introduction: overall presentation
  • Paragraph 1: first impression
  • Paragraph 2: development/turning point
  • Paragraph 3: final impression
  • Conclusion: overall significance

Theme Questions

Structure:

  • Introduction: define theme, state argument
  • Paragraph 1: how theme introduced
  • Paragraph 2: how theme developed
  • Paragraph 3: how theme resolved
  • Conclusion: overall message about theme

Extract Questions

Structure:

  • Introduction: identify extract's place in text
  • Paragraphs 1-3: analyse extract (language, structure, form)
  • Paragraph 4: link to whole text
  • Conclusion: significance of this moment

⏱️ Time Management

For a 45-Minute Essay

Task Time
Plan 5 minutes
Introduction 3 minutes
Body paragraphs 30 minutes (10 per paragraph)
Conclusion 5 minutes
Check 2 minutes

Planning Tips

  • Identify 3-4 main points before writing
  • Know your quotations for each point
  • Sketch the structure: Intro → P1 → P2 → P3 → Conclusion

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Mistake Fix
Retelling the story Analyse, don't narrate
No clear argument Have a thesis in your introduction
Unlinked paragraphs Use linking words between paragraphs
New points in conclusion Conclusion summarises only
Running out of time Plan first, stick to time limits

❓ Practice Questions

Q1: What are the three parts of an essay?

Q2: What does PEEL stand for?

Q3: What should an introduction include?

Q4: What should you avoid in a conclusion?

✅ Answers

  1. Introduction, Body Paragraphs, Conclusion. Introduction sets up argument, body develops with evidence, conclusion summarises and evaluates.
  2. Point - Make your argument. Evidence - Quote the text. Explain - Analyse the quotation. Link - Connect to question/theme/context.
  3. Introduction should: identify text and writer, respond to the question, state your main argument (thesis). Keep it 2-3 sentences.
  4. Don't introduce new points. Don't just repeat the introduction. Don't say "In conclusion." The conclusion should summarise and evaluate, not add new ideas.
